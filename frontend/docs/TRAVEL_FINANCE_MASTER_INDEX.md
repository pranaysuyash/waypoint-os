## Series Overview

This series covers travel finance — the Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) product that converts price-sensitive customers into confirmed bookings. From partnerships with Bajaj Finserv, HDFC, and LazyPay for instant travel loans, to no-cost EMI promotions that make a ₹4 lakh honeymoon package feel like ₹33K/month, travel financing is the most powerful conversion tool that Indian OTAs have and most travel agencies don't. MakeMyTrip's "Pay Later" feature alone increased conversions by 18% — agencies without financing lose those customers to OTAs.

**Target Audience:** Finance managers, product managers, agency owners

**Key Insight:** The average Indian middle-class customer can afford ₹15-20K/month for travel EMI but cannot pay ₹2 lakh upfront. The agency that offers "book now, pay over 6 months" converts customers who would otherwise choose a cheaper destination, delay the trip, or book with MMT instead. Travel financing isn't a payment feature — it's a revenue multiplier that increases average booking value by 40% and conversion rate by 18%.

## Key Themes

### 1. Financing Wins Deals That Price Objections Kill
"I love the Bali trip but it's too expensive" becomes "Bali trip for ₹28K/month — let's do it!" The same customer, the same trip, the same revenue — but with financing, the deal closes. Without it, the customer walks.

### 2. No-Cost EMI Is a Marketing Expense, Not a Financial Loss
The 3-5% merchant discount rate (MDR) on no-cost EMI is a customer acquisition cost — cheaper than running Instagram ads. ₹10,000 MDR on a ₹3 lakh booking (3.3%) is a small price for winning a ₹3 lakh deal.

### 3. Instant Approval Prevents Drop-off
BNPL approval must be instant (30-second OTP-based). Multi-day loan approval processes kill conversions — the customer books with someone else while waiting.

### 4. Financing in the Proposal Increases Perceived Affordability
Every proposal should show the EMI option alongside the total price: "₹3,20,000 total OR ₹53,333/month × 6 months (no-cost EMI)". The monthly number reframes the price as affordable.
