## Series Overview

This series covers conversion rate optimization (CRO) and sales rate optimization (SRO) — the systematic discipline of improving the percentage of visitors who inquire, book, and return. CRO optimizes digital touchpoints (landing pages, forms, CTAs); SRO optimizes human touchpoints (agent conversations, proposals, follow-ups). Together, they're the highest-ROI activity in the agency: improving conversion from 5% to 8% triples the value of every marketing dollar spent.

**Target Audience:** Product managers, marketing managers, agency owners, sales agents

**Key Insight:** Most agencies focus on getting MORE inquiries (spending more on ads) instead of converting MORE of the inquiries they already get. Increasing proposal-to-booking conversion from 35% to 50% generates more revenue than doubling ad spend — at zero additional cost. CRO/SRO is the cheapest growth lever.

## Key Themes

### 1. Optimize What You Have Before Adding More
Before spending ₹50K more on ads, spend 2 weeks optimizing the landing page. A 2% conversion improvement from 5% to 7% is worth more than 10,000 additional impressions.

### 2. CRO Is Digital, SRO Is Human
CRO optimizes the website, forms, and checkout flow. SRO optimizes the agent's response time, proposal quality, follow-up persistence, and objection handling. Both are needed — a great website with a slow agent wastes the inquiry.

### 3. Test One Thing at a Time
Change the headline AND the CTA AND the image simultaneously, and you won't know what worked. Single-variable testing with statistical confidence is slower but produces learnings, not guesses.

### 4. The Follow-Up Is the Sale
70% of bookings happen at the 3rd-5th follow-up. System-enforced follow-up cadences are the single biggest SRO lever — and the most commonly neglected.
