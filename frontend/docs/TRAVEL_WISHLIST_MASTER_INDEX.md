# Travel Wishlist & Bucket List — Master Index

> Research on travel wishlists, destination bucket lists, price drop alerts, saved trip ideas, collaborative wishlists, and dream-to-booking conversion for the Waypoint OS platform.

---

## Series Overview

This series covers the travel wishlist and bucket list system — the engagement layer that captures travelers' aspirations and nurtures them into bookings over time. From destination saves with priority levels (dreaming → planning → ready) and price drop alerts that create urgency, to collaborative group wishlists with voting and shareable bucket list achievement cards, the wishlist system is the bridge between passive interest and active booking.

**Target Audience:** Product managers, marketing managers, agents

**Key Insight:** The average Indian traveler starts dreaming about a trip 6-12 months before booking. During this dreaming phase, they browse destinations, save ideas, and discuss with companions. The agency that captures this intent early (via wishlists) and nurtures it (via price alerts, seasonal nudges, and deal matches) converts 15-25% of wishlisted items into bookings — a massive pipeline of future revenue that would otherwise go to competitors.

## Key Themes

### 1. Capture Intent Early
The traveler browsing Bali at 11 PM on a Tuesday is 6 months from booking — but they're signaling intent. The wishlist captures this signal so the agency can nurture it with price alerts, deals, and content until the traveler is ready.

### 2. Price Drops Create Urgency from Aspiration
"I want to go to Maldives someday" becomes "Maldives is 15% cheaper this week — should we go?" with a single price drop alert. The right nudge at the right price converts dreams into bookings.

### 3. Collaborative Planning Drives Group Bookings
Group trips start with disagreement. A shared wishlist where everyone adds destinations and votes turns chaos into consensus — and the agency captures the group booking that would otherwise fragment.

### 4. Wishlist Data Is Demand Intelligence
500 customers wishlisting Japan for cherry blossom season is a demand signal. The agency that pre-negotiates inventory based on wishlist data has the best deals ready when customers are ready to book.
