## Series Overview

This series covers the platform business model — how Waypoint OS makes money from the agencies that use it. From freemium tiers (free for solo agents, paid for teams) and per-booking transaction fees to premium features (AI copilot, analytics, white-label) and revenue-share models, the platform business model determines both the platform's revenue and the agency's willingness to pay. The right model aligns incentives: the platform succeeds when agencies succeed.

**Target Audience:** Platform founders, product managers, finance

**Key Insight:** The most successful vertical SaaS platforms charge 2-5% of their customers' revenue, not a flat fee. A per-booking fee of ₹50-200 (or 0.5-1% of booking value) means the platform earns more when agencies book more — perfectly aligned incentives. A ₹5,000/month flat fee feels expensive when business is slow and cheap when business is good. Revenue-linked pricing grows with the customer.

## Key Themes

### 1. Align Incentives — Platform Wins When Agency Wins
Per-booking pricing means the platform's revenue grows with agency revenue. A flat fee means the platform earns the same whether the agency books 10 trips or 100. Revenue-linked pricing creates a partnership dynamic, not a vendor dynamic.

### 2. Freemium Acquires, Features Retain
A free tier for solo agents (limited bookings, basic features) gets them onto the platform. When they grow to 2+ agents or 20+ bookings/month, they hit natural upgrade triggers. The free tier is the best acquisition channel.

### 3. Premium Features Justify Premium Pricing
AI copilot, advanced analytics, white-label customer portal, and API access are premium features that agencies will pay extra for. The gap between "managing trips" (commodity) and "growing a travel business" (premium) is where margin lives.

### 4. The Agency Must See ROI
An agency paying ₹10,000/month must see ₹30,000+ in value: time saved, conversions won, errors prevented, commissions captured. If they can't see ROI, they churn. Platform analytics must show the agency exactly what they're getting.
