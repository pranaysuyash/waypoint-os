# Trip Handoff — Master Index

> Research on agent-to-agent trip transfer, context preservation, customer communication during transitions, and seamless service continuity for the Waypoint OS platform.

---

## Series Overview

This series covers trip handoff — the process of transferring a customer's trip between agents without service disruption or trust erosion. From automated context transfer packages and proactive customer introduction to satisfaction guardrails and capacity-based routing, the handoff system ensures the customer never feels the impact of an agent change.

**Target Audience:** Operations managers, product managers, travel agents

**Key Insight:** The #1 complaint during agent changes is "I had to repeat everything to the new person." A structured handoff that transfers full conversation history, relationship context, pending items, and the customer's communication preferences makes the transition invisible to the customer. The new agent should sound like they've been there from the beginning.

## Key Themes

### 1. Context Transfer Is Non-Negotiable
Every conversation, decision, concern, and promise from the original agent must transfer to the new agent. One missed detail ("Aarav loves animals") can undo weeks of trust-building.

### 2. Proactive Introduction Beats Reactive
The customer should hear about the handoff from the agency before they notice their agent isn't responding. A proactive WhatsApp introduction with specific details proves the new agent is informed and ready.

### 3. No Repetition, Ever
If the customer told the original agent they're vegetarian, they should never be asked about dietary preferences again. The system must surface relevant context before the first conversation.

### 4. Handoff Quality = Customer Retention
A poorly handled handoff (missing context, slow introduction, repeated questions) is a top reason customers switch agencies. A well-handled handoff builds confidence in the agency as an organization, not just an individual agent.
