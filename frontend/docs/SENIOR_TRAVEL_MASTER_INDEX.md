## Series Overview

This series covers senior citizen travel — the underserved but high-value segment (140M+ Indians aged 60+) that prioritizes comfort, safety, and pace over adventure and nightlife. From NRI parent visit logistics (door-to-door companion service for elderly parents flying to Seattle to visit children) and pace-adjusted group tours to medical-awareness in itinerary design, senior travel requires a fundamentally different product philosophy: slow, supported, and family-connected.

**Target Audience:** Product managers, operations managers, agents

**Key Insight:** NRI parent travel is the hidden gem — 5M+ families abroad want their aging parents to visit but worry about the journey. An agency that offers "we'll take care of your parents from Delhi to Seattle and back" with live tracking, medical support, and daily updates to the family commands premium pricing (₹1-2L per trip) and fierce loyalty. This is trust-based selling at its purest.

## Key Themes

### 1. Slow Is the Right Speed
Senior travel isn't about seeing everything — it's about enjoying each thing. Two activities per day, generous rest periods, and every third day free. Rushed seniors are unhappy seniors.

### 2. Family Peace of Mind Is the Product
For NRI parent visits, the customer is the child abroad, not the parent traveling. The product is peace of mind: live tracking, daily updates, medical backup, and the assurance that parents are being looked after.

### 3. Medical-Awareness in Every Decision
From flight selection (direct, lie-flat) to accommodation (ground floor, walk-in shower) to meal timing (medication schedules), every decision in senior travel is filtered through a medical lens.

### 4. WhatsApp and Phone, Not Apps
Senior travelers don't want to download apps. WhatsApp messages, phone calls, and family member dashboard access are the channels. Design for the senior's comfort, not the developer's convenience.
