## Series Overview

This series covers the WhatsApp Business Platform as the primary channel for Indian travel agencies — from API integration and template management to AI-assisted sales conversations, payment collection, marketing campaigns, and the agent toolkit. WhatsApp is not a feature; it's the primary interface.

**Target Audience:** Product managers, backend engineers, frontend engineers

**Key Insight:** 78% of Indian travel bookings close on WhatsApp. The cost per booking via WhatsApp is ₹130 vs. ₹2,500+ for traditional channels. The 53x ROI makes WhatsApp the single most important channel for the platform.

## Key Themes

### 1. WhatsApp IS the Platform
For Indian travel agencies, WhatsApp is not an integration — it's the primary platform. Sales, service, marketing, payments, and delivery all happen in WhatsApp. The web app is the power tool for complex tasks.

### 2. AI-Assisted, Human-Driven
AI categorizes inquiries, suggests responses, auto-answers routine queries, and generates proposals. But the human agent makes the sale. The AI handles the tedious 60% so agents focus on the critical 40%.

### 3. Template Compliance as a Feature
WhatsApp template management is a necessary constraint. Pre-approved templates, category optimization (utility over marketing), and version tracking turn a limitation into a structured communication system.

### 4. 24-Hour Window as Design Constraint
The 24-hour conversation window shapes the entire engagement strategy. Proactive messages (templates) open windows; fast responses keep them open; automated sequences maintain engagement when agents are away.
