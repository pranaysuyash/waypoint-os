## Key Themes

### 1. Dual-Sided Incentive Structure
The referral program works because both parties benefit. The referrer earns travel credit (₹2,000+) and the referee gets a discount (₹2,000 off). This removes the awkwardness of recommending a business and makes sharing genuinely helpful. Tiered rewards (Bronze through Platinum) create long-term motivation beyond single referrals.

### 2. Signal-Based, Not Calendar-Based
Referral requests are triggered by behavioral signals (high NPS, memory share, repeat booking) rather than arbitrary dates. This respects customer intelligence and dramatically improves conversion — memory share triggers convert at 18% vs. anniversary triggers at 4%.

### 3. Trust as the Viral Medium
Travel purchases are high-trust decisions. A friend's recommendation carries 5x more weight than advertising. The viral engine doesn't try to manufacture virality — it amplifies genuine customer enthusiasm through shareable memory products, testimonials, and easy referral flows.

### 4. Quality Over Quantity
With a viral coefficient of 0.18, the program won't go viral in the social media sense. That's fine — travel is infrequent. The goal is high-quality referrals that convert at 15%+ and generate 47% higher LTV, not mass sharing that dilutes trust. Every referral is a warm lead with pre-built credibility.
