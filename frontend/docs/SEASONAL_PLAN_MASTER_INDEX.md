## Series Overview

This series covers seasonal campaign planning — the system that aligns marketing campaigns, pricing strategies, and inventory commitments with India's predictable travel demand cycles. From summer holiday early-bird campaigns (January-March) to Diwali booking pushes (September-October), seasonal planning ensures the agency captures demand when it peaks and activates demand when it doesn't.

**Target Audience:** Marketing managers, agency owners, revenue managers

**Key Insight:** 55-60% of annual travel revenue comes from just 6 months (April-June and October-December). Agencies that start marketing for summer holidays in January capture 30-40% of their annual bookings before competitors even launch campaigns. The seasonal calendar is the single most important planning document in a travel agency.

## Key Themes

### 1. Plan in January, Execute in April
Summer holiday trips are decided in January-March. The agency that reaches families during this planning window with early-bird pricing wins the booking. Late campaigns in April capture only the leftovers.

### 2. Shoulder Season Is the Margin Opportunity
Shoulder season (September-October, February-March) offers lower supplier costs with reasonable selling prices — the highest margins. Campaigns that activate demand during these periods are more profitable than peak-season campaigns.

### 3. Off-Season Isn't Dead Season
Monsoon months (July-August) are the cheapest time to travel. Agencies that position this as "escape the rain" for destinations with good weather (Dubai, Bali dry season, Europe summer) can generate 10-15% of annual revenue during the "dead" months.

### 4. Inventory Commitment Is a Bet
Locking hotel allotments 6 months before peak season guarantees supply but risks unsold inventory. The seasonal planner must balance commitment levels against historical booking velocity to minimize risk.
