## Series Overview

This series covers influencer and affiliate marketing as a customer acquisition channel — from program tier design and commission structures to content collaboration frameworks, attribution tracking, and fraud prevention. Influencer marketing generates 9.3x ROI at ₹2,100 cost per booking.

**Target Audience:** Marketing managers, growth teams, agency owners

**Key Insight:** Micro-influencers (5K-50K followers) deliver the best ROI (15x) due to higher engagement rates and niche audiences. A single sponsored Singapore trip with a micro-influencer costs ₹15-25K in net inventory value and generates 3-8 bookings worth ₹5-15L in revenue.

## Key Themes

### 1. Commission Over Free Trips
The affiliate model (commission-only per booking) is more sustainable than giving free trips to every influencer. It aligns incentives — the influencer only earns when real bookings happen. Free trips are reserved for proven performers.

### 2. Content as a Long-Term Asset
Influencer content (reels, vlogs, posts) lives on social media for months and continues generating inquiries long after the initial post. A single YouTube vlog about Singapore can generate bookings for 12+ months.

### 3. Multi-Method Attribution
No single attribution method catches all influencer-driven bookings. Using code + UTM + self-report + phone match together captures ~85% of influencer-influenced bookings. The last influencer touch gets the commission.

### 4. Micro-Influencer Focus
For Indian travel agencies, micro-influencers in specific niches (family travel, honeymoon, adventure) deliver the best returns. Their audiences are smaller but highly engaged and trust their recommendations.
