## Series Overview

This series covers post-booking value protection — the system that protects customers after they've paid. From monitoring flight prices for post-booking drops and proactively claiming refunds, to advocating for customers in hotel disputes ("the room isn't as booked"), guiding travelers through insurance claims, and handling chargebacks, value protection is the agency's "we've got your back" promise made tangible. Most agencies stop caring after payment is collected. The agency that actively protects the customer's money after booking earns the highest possible trust.

**Target Audience:** Product managers, operations managers, agents

**Key Insight:** A customer who booked a flight at ₹45,000 and sees it drop to ₹38,000 the next week feels cheated — even though no one cheated them. The agency that proactively detects this drop, files the airline's price-drop refund claim, and credits the ₹7,000 back to the customer's wallet doesn't just save money — it creates a customer for life. Post-booking protection is the single most powerful loyalty action an agency can take, and almost no Indian agencies do it.

## Key Themes

### 1. Proactive, Not Reactive
The customer shouldn't have to discover the price drop or file the dispute themselves. The agency monitors proactively and acts on the customer's behalf. "We noticed your flight price dropped — we've filed for the ₹7,000 refund" is the most powerful trust-building message possible.

### 2. Advocate, Not Intermediary
When a hotel says "we can't help," the agency doesn't relay that to the customer. The agency escalates, threatens review impact, invokes contract terms, and fights for the customer. The agency is the customer's advocate, not the supplier's messenger.

### 3. Insurance Claims Are Mystifying to Customers
Filing a travel insurance claim requires forms, evidence, receipts, and patience — all while the customer is dealing with the travel disruption that caused the claim. Guided claim assistance (telling the customer exactly what to submit, following up with the insurer) is high-value, low-cost service.

### 4. The Recovery Fund Is the Ultimate Guarantee
A small reserve (1-2% of revenue) that pays out when the agency can't recover from the supplier gives customers absolute confidence. "If we can't get your money back from the hotel, we'll credit it from our recovery fund" eliminates booking anxiety.
