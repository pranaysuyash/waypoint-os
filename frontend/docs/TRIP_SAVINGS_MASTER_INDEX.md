## Series Overview

This series covers the customer trip savings and budget planning engine — a unique capability that helps customers save for trips over months while the agency keeps them engaged with destination content, price alerts, and booking triggers. In India, major trips are often planned 6-12 months in advance with dedicated savings, making this a key differentiator.

**Target Audience:** Product managers, frontend engineers, growth teams

**Key Insight:** 78% of Indian families save for 3-8 months before booking an international trip. The savings program converts 28% of savers into bookers at 74x ROI — making it one of the highest-return customer acquisition channels. The key insight is that engaged savers are the warmest leads: they have declared intent, timeline, and budget.

## Key Themes

### 1. Savings as a Lead Funnel
Every savings goal is a declared intent to travel. Unlike anonymous website visitors, savers have shared their destination, timeline, and budget. This makes them the highest-quality leads in the pipeline. The savings phase is a 3-8 month nurture sequence.

### 2. Readiness Detection
The readiness score (0-100) combines financial signals (savings progress), engagement signals (itinerary views), timing signals (proximity to travel date), and behavioral signals (asking specific questions). When the score crosses 60, the agent creates a personalized proposal.

### 3. Group Savings as Social Proof
Family pool savings create organic engagement — grandparents, siblings, and friends all become aware of the agency through the contribution process. Each contributor is a potential future customer.

### 4. Price Lock as Commitment Device
The lock-in option (deposit now, guarantee price for 6 months) is the strongest conversion tool. It converts a browser into a committed customer while protecting them from price increases.
