## Series Overview

This series covers accessible travel services — the capabilities needed to serve travelers with disabilities, senior citizens, and travelers with special needs. From wheelchair-accessible hotel rooms and transport to visual/hearing accommodation and senior-friendly pacing, accessible travel is both a moral imperative and an underserved market. 15% of India's population has some form of disability; the vast majority of travel agencies are not equipped to serve them.

**Target Audience:** Product managers, travel agents, operations managers

**Key Insight:** The accessible travel market is worth $13B+ globally and growing 8% annually. Indian agencies that invest in accessibility data and trained staff capture a loyal, high-value market with minimal competition. A wheelchair traveler who finds an agency that truly understands their needs becomes a customer for life — and refers others in the disability community.

## Key Themes

### 1. Accessibility Data Is the Foundation
Without verified accessibility data for hotels, attractions, and transport, agents cannot plan accessible trips. The database must include specific measurements (doorway width, elevator dimensions), not vague labels like "accessible."

### 2. Customer Feedback Is the Best Verification
Hotels self-report accessibility features optimistically. Post-trip ratings from disabled travelers provide the most reliable verification. Build a feedback loop: every accessible trip generates data that improves the next traveler's experience.

### 3. Standardize, Don't Guess
Every accessible trip follows the same checklist: entrance access, room access, bathroom access, transport access, attraction access, emergency plan. Standardized checklists ensure nothing is missed, regardless of destination.

### 4. This Is an Underserved Market, Not a Niche
15% of the population has a disability. Add seniors (12%+ over 60) and families with infants, and this is a massive market that most agencies ignore. Serving it well is a competitive moat.
