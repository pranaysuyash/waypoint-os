## Series Overview

This series covers the agency's own insurance needs — not customer travel insurance, but the business protection policies that shield the agency from professional liability, booking errors, cyber breaches, and business interruption. Most Indian agencies operate underinsured: 70%+ have no professional indemnity or cyber insurance despite handling passport data, payment information, and customer funds. A single uninsured claim can wipe out a year's profit.

**Target Audience:** Agency owners, operations managers, risk officers

**Key Insight:** The average Indian travel agency spends ₹0 on business insurance while storing thousands of passport numbers, Aadhaar details, and payment credentials. A data breach costs ₹8L-2Cr to remediate (IBM 2024). Professional indemnity insurance (₹15-50K/year for ₹50L coverage) and cyber insurance (₹20-50K/year for ₹1Cr coverage) cost less than 1% of revenue but protect against existential financial risk.

## Key Themes

### 1. Insurance Is Cheaper Than a Claim
₹1L/year in premiums protects against ₹2-3Cr in potential claims. No agency is too small to insure — a single booking error lawsuit or data breach can cost more than the entire business is worth.

### 2. Cyber Insurance Is Non-Negotiable
Agencies that store passport numbers, Aadhaar, and payment data without cyber insurance are one breach away from closure. DPDP Act 2023 imposes penalties up to ₹250Cr for data protection failures. Cyber insurance covers breach costs; lack of it doesn't just risk money — it risks the business.

### 3. Prevention Beats Claims
The best insurance claim is the one never filed. System-enforced booking verification, PII encryption, and financial controls prevent the incidents that lead to claims. Insurance is the last line of defense, not the first.

### 4. Pandemic Coverage Is the New Normal
COVID wiped out 80-90% of agency revenue in 2020-2021, and most had no coverage. Post-pandemic BI policies with pandemic add-ons (20-30% extra premium) are now available and essential for agencies with high fixed costs.
