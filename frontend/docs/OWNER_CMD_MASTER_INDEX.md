## Series Overview

This series covers the owner and executive command center as the strategic nerve center of the travel agency — from morning dashboards and KPIs to demand forecasting, growth planning, risk monitoring, compliance tracking, and board reporting. The owner doesn't use the platform to book trips; they use it to run the business.

**Target Audience:** Agency owners, managing directors, advisory board members

**Key Insight:** Agency owners currently run their business on WhatsApp messages, spreadsheets, and gut feel. The command center replaces this with data-driven morning pulses (WhatsApp), weekly reviews (email), monthly reports (PDF), and real-time alerts for issues requiring immediate attention. The agency health score (76/100) gives a single number to track business performance over time.

## Key Themes

### 1. Morning Pulse as a Habit
The owner's day starts with a WhatsApp message at 8 AM: how many trips are active, what payments came in, what needs attention today. This replaces the "check everything manually" routine with a single, scannable pulse. If something needs action, it's flagged in the message.

### 2. Health Score as North Star
The composite agency health score (0-100) gives owners a single metric to track over time. Breaking it down by dimension (financial 78, customer 82, operations 74, team 71, growth 76) shows exactly where to focus. Monthly trend tracking prevents gradual decline from going unnoticed.

### 3. Insights Over Data
The command center doesn't just show numbers — it generates insights with actions. "Singapore inquiries up 42%" is data. "Singapore inquiries up 42% — lock hotel rates by May 10 or risk losing allotment" is an insight with a deadline. Every alert includes what to do about it.

### 4. Proactive Risk Management
Most agency crises are preventable with early detection. The risk monitor tracks 15+ risk signals continuously and escalates based on severity. Financial risks (overdue payments), operational risks (agent overload), and compliance risks (filing deadlines) are all surfaced before they become crises.
