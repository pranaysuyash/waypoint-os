## Series Overview

This series covers travel registries as a new product category — from honeymoon wish lists where wedding guests fund trip experiences, to milestone birthday and anniversary group gifting. Registry-driven bookings have 35% higher close rates (the money is already committed) and create viral loops as contributors discover the agency through the registry page.

**Target Audience:** Product managers, marketing teams, agency owners

**Key Insight:** Honeymoon registries convert wedding gift-giving (₹50K-5L in cash envelopes at Indian weddings) into structured trip funding. The couple gets their dream trip funded, guests contribute meaningfully instead of buying unwanted appliances, and the agency captures a high-margin booking with built-in social proof (15-40 people now know the agency).

## Key Themes

### 1. Registry as Social Proof Engine
Every registry has 15-40 contributors who visit the agency's registry page, see the trip details, and receive post-trip updates. This is organic exposure to 15-40 potential customers at zero acquisition cost — each contributor is a warm lead for their own travel.

### 2. Committed Money Converts
Registry-funded trips have 35% higher close rates than regular inquiries because the money is already committed. The couple has skin in the game, and contributors have emotional investment in seeing the trip happen.

### 3. Experience Gifting Over Cash
Indian wedding gifting is moving from physical items (appliances, gold) toward experiences. A honeymoon registry where guests fund "candlelight dinner in Bali" or "Sentosa experience" feels more meaningful than a ₹3,000 cash envelope.

### 4. Wedding Ecosystem Integration
Travel registries plug into the existing wedding planning ecosystem — wedding planners recommend them, they appear on wedding websites, and QR codes go on invitation cards. The wedding industry is a ₹10L+ crore market that travel agencies can tap.
