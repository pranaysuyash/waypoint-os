# Competitive Landscape

**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Purpose**: Who else is doing this? Why you?

---

## The "Why Build This?" Question

If someone asks "what about ChatGPT?" or "doesn't Expedia do this?", you need an answer.

---

## Direct Competitors Analysis

### 1. **Duffel** (duffel.com)

**What they do**: Platform for travel agents to book flights/hotels

| Strengths | Weaknesses (your opportunity) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Clean booking interface | Focused on booking, not planning |
| Good flight/hotel APIs | No intent extraction |
| Strong tech team | Doesn't handle messy conversations |

**Your edge**: You start where Duffel ends — before the booking.

---

### 2. **Travelex** / Agency CRMs

**What they do**: Back-office systems for travel agencies

| Strengths | Weaknesses (your opportunity) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Established, trusted | Legacy UI, clunky |
| Full agency management | No AI, manual data entry |
| Integrates with GDS | Doesn't "think" for you |

**Your edge**: Modern UX + AI vs. legacy database.

---

### 3. **TravelPerk**

**What they do**: Business travel platform

| Strengths | Weaknesses (your opportunity) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Great product | Corporate market, not leisure |
| Strong brand | Expensive, enterprise-focused |
| Booking + management | Doesn't do custom planning |

**Your edge**: You serve leisure/custom trips, not business travel.

## Indirect Competitors (What agencies use NOW)

### The REAL competition isn't software — it's habits.

| Current solution | Why they use it | Your wedge |
|------------------|-----------------|------------|
| **WhatsApp + notes app** | Familiar, free | You organize the chaos |
| **Excel/Google Sheets** | Flexible, they control it | You automate the work |
| **Email + PDFs** | What clients expect | You make it interactive |
| **Phone calls** | Personal, builds trust | You give them better things to say |

### Your Positioning

> "Not another tool to learn — an assistant that does the work you hate."

## The ChatGPT Question

**Customer**: "Why not just use ChatGPT?"

**Your answer**:

| ChatGPT | Your Tool |
|---------|-----------|
| No context between conversations | Remembers client preferences, trip history |
| No agency workflow | Built into agency process |
| No source verification | Source envelope on every suggestion |
| No "proceed/ask" gates | Knows when to ask vs. decide |
| No traveler-safe output | Filters what traveler sees |
| Generic | Calibrated for agency work |

## Your Moat (Defensibility)

| Potential Threat | Your Defense |
|------------------|--------------|
| **OpenAI builds this** | You have agency-specific workflow + data |
| **Big travel tech builds this** | You're faster, focused on this one problem |
| **Another startup** | You have first pilot relationships |
| **Agencies build internally** | You do this cheaper/better than a side project |

**Real answer**: Your moat is not tech — it's relationships + workflow depth.

## Summary

**Your competition isn't software — it's agent habits.**

Most agencies still use:
- WhatsApp + notes
- Excel sheets
- Phone calls
- Email

You win by:
1. Fitting into existing workflow (not replacing it)
2. Doing the boring work (extraction, normalization)
3. Keeping the human in control (gates, source envelope)

**Don't worry about "competition" — worry about solving the problem better than status quo.**
