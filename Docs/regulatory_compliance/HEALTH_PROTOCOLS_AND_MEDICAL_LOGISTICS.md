# Domain Knowledge: Health Protocols & Medical Logistics

**Category**: Documentation & Health  
**Focus**: Vaccinations, Fit-to-fly rules, and medical history management.

---

## 1. Vaccinations & The "Yellow Book"

- **Logic**: Entry to many countries (e.g., in Africa/South America) is contingent on proof of **Yellow Fever** vaccination.
- **SOP**: The agent must check the **TIMATIC** database for every itinerary. "Recommendation" vs. "Requirement" is a critical distinction.

---

## 2. "Fit-to-Fly" & Medical Clearances (MEDIF)

### The MEDIF Form
- **Logic**: If a traveler has a recent surgery, a cast, or a chronic condition (e.g., needs Oxygen), the airline requires a **MEDIF** (Medical Information Form) signed by a doctor.
- **SOP**: The form must be submitted to the airline's **"Medical Desk"** at least 48–72 hours before departure.

---

## 3. High-Altitude & Scuba Risks

- **Logic**: Divers cannot fly for 18–24 hours after their last dive due to "The Bends" (Decompression Sickness).
- **SOP**: Itineraries for "Diving trips" must have a mandatory **"Dry Day"** before the return flight.

---

## 4. Medication Transport (The "Narcotic" Risk)

- **Logic**: Many common medications (e.g., Adderall, strong painkillers) are strictly illegal in countries like Japan, Dubai, or Singapore.
- **SOP**: The agent must advise the client to carry an **"Original Prescription"** and check the local embassy rules for "Restricted Substances."

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Yellow Fever" Denied Entry**: A traveler is denied boarding for a flight to Brazil because they forgot their "Yellow Book." The agent must find a "Vaccination Clinic" at the airport (rare) or re-book for 10 days later.
- **The "Wet Cast" Rejection**: A traveler arrives at the gate with a leg cast that is only 24 hours old. The airline denies boarding due to "Swelling Risk." The agent must explain the "Bivalve" cast rule.
- **Diving/Flight Sync Error**: A client dives at 9 AM and has a flight at 6 PM. The agent must catch this "Lethal" error in the itinerary.
- **Medication Seizure**: A client's ADHD medication is seized at customs in Tokyo. The agent must coordinate with the embassy to find a local legal alternative.
