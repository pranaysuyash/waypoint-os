## 2. Interview Scenario**

**Context:** Sunita switched from OTAs to a boutique agent (Priya) 6 months ago. Priya uses Agency OS. Sunita has experienced both the old OTA way and the new agency-AI-assisted way. This interview explores the "why switch" and "what's still broken" from a tech-savvy traveler's view.

---

## 3. Interview Transcript**

### Opening: The OTA Breaking Point**

**Interviewer:** Sunita, what made you finally leave MakeMyTrip after 6 years?

**Sunita:** *(laughs)* It was the Bali trip in 2025. I booked through MMT — ₹3.2L for a "family-friendly luxury package." Looked great on the app. 4.8 rating. Gorgeous photos.

We landed in Bali at 8 PM. Got to the hotel. It was... not what I expected. The "family room" was ONE room with a curtain divider. My 4-year-old and 7-year-old were supposed to sleep behind a curtain while my husband and I were in the same room. And it was on the 4th floor with NO ELEVATOR. I had a stroller.

I called MMT's customer care. "Sorry ma'am, the hotel is as per booking. Please rate us 5 stars." Click. That was it.

**Interviewer:** And that's when you switched to an agent?

**Sunita:** Not immediately. I tried Booking.com next. Better filters, I thought. "Family-friendly" checkbox. Booked a resort in Phuket.

Again: "family-friendly" meant "kids' club available." But the kids' club was for ages 12+. My 4-year-old couldn't go. And the resort was 45 minutes from the beach. NOT what I wanted.

I realized: OTA filters are GARBAGE. "Family-friendly" means NOTHING. It's a marketing term, not a real filter.

I asked a friend who travels a lot. She said: "Use an agent. Yes, you pay commission. But they actually LISTEN."

---

### Section 1: The First Agency Experience (Without Tool)**

**Interviewer:** Your first trip with Priya — what was that like?

**Sunita:** Honestly? Better than OTAs, but still... manual.

I sent her a long WhatsApp: "Goa, 3 days, 2 adults + 2 kids (4 & 7), budget ₹80K, want beach access, kids' activities, not too crowded."

She responded 2 days later with options. They were... okay. Better than OTAs because she picked actual family hotels. But the options felt like SHE liked them, not like THEY FIT US.

And the revision loop! I said "Can we add a water park day?" She said "Sure" and sent a new PDF 3 days later. THREE DAYS for one change.

I remember thinking: "I'm paying a commission for... slow service?"

But then we went on the trip. And the hotel DID have a kids' pool. The restaurant DID have a kids' menu. The transport HAD a child seat. Those details? OTAs never got those right.

So: slower process, better outcome. I stayed with her.

---

### Section 2: The Shift to Agency OS (The Second Trip)**

**Interviewer:** Now tell me about the second trip, after she started using the tool.

**Sunita:** THIS was different.

I messaged: "Thinking Vietnam. 5 days. Us + parents (60+). Budget flexible. Mom can't walk too much."

Priya responded SAME DAY. And her questions were... specific:

- "Does 'flexible' mean up to ₹1.5L or ₹2L?"
- "Any mobility aids for mom? (Walker, cane?)"
- "Prefer North Vietnam (cooler) or South (warmer, beaches)?"
- "Kids (now 5 & 8) — more theme parks or nature?"

I was like... "Wow, she actually read my message." WITHIN 24 HOURS, she sent 3 options. Each had a section: **"Why this fits YOUR family"**:

- "Hotel X — elevator access (mom's mobility), kids' pool (ages 5 & 8 suitable), vegetarian Indian restaurant in building"
- "Day 1: Light schedule (mom's jet lag), 2-hour max walking"
- "Theme park day: height requirements checked — both kids qualify for all rides"

I read that and thought: "This is what I WISHED OTAs did." It wasn't about the CHEAPEST hotel. It was about the RIGHT hotel.

**Interviewer:** And the kids?

**Sunita:** *(smiles)* My 8-year-old said: "Mom, this hotel has a slide INTO the pool!" That's all she cared about. And it was TRUE. The tool had flagged "kids' pool with slide" as a match for my family.

---

### Section 3: What OTAs Still Do Better**

**Interviewer:** Be honest — what does MMT/Booking.com still do better?

**Sunita:** Three things.

**1. Speed of booking.** I can book a hotel on MMT in 3 minutes. With Priya, even with the tool, it's 1-2 days. I know the outcome is better, but the INSTANT gratification is gone. Sometimes I just want to book and be done.

**2. Reviews and photos.** MMT has 10,000 reviews per hotel. Priya sends me her agency's preferred hotels. They're good, but I can't see what OTHER people think. I have to TRUST her.

**3. Price comparison.** On MMT, I can see: "Same room, ₹12K on MMT, ₹15K on Booking." With Priya, I see ONE price. I don't know if it's the best deal.

**Interviewer:** Would you go back to OTAs for these reasons?

**Sunita:** *(firmly)* No. The outcome is SO much better with Priya. Vietnam was perfect. Mom was comfortable. Kids were happy. I wasn't stressed.

But if the tool could give me SOME of that OTA magic? Like, "Here's the TripAdvisor rating and recent reviews" or "We compared — this is ₹2K above market, but worth it because of X, Y, Z"?

That would be the perfect blend: OTA's data + agent's personalization.

---

### Section 4: The Trust Question**

**Interviewer:** How would you feel knowing an AI helped plan your trip?

**Sunita:** Hmm. This is interesting.

If AI is helping Priya pick the RIGHT hotel for my mom's mobility? I'm happy. That's using technology to serve me better.

But if I felt like NO ONE actually looked at my trip? Like, if Priya just clicked "Generate" and sent me robot options? I'd be offended.

The difference is: Priya ADDED her own notes. "I've sent the Sharma family to this hotel — they had similar needs, they loved the kids' pool." THAT's the human touch. The AI gave her the data, but SHE gave me the reassurance.

**Interviewer:** What if Priya told you "an AI planned this"?

**Sunita:** *(laughs)* I'd ask for my commission back! No, seriously — I'm paying her for HER expertise. If she's just clicking buttons, I might as well use MMT.

The AI should be INVISIBLE. It should make Priya faster and more thorough. But the client-facing output should sound like HER.

---

### Section 5: The Pricing Question**

**Interviewer:** OTAs show you exactly what you're paying. How does that compare to agency pricing?

**Sunita:** With OTAs, I see: Hotel ₹40K + Flights ₹60K + Taxes ₹10K = ₹110K. Transparent.

With Priya, I see: "Total package ₹125K." Then I ask: "What's included?" and she says "Hotels, flights, breakfast, transfers, taxes."

I don't know: did she mark up the hotel by 20%? Is the flight commission built in? 

I don't MIND paying a commission. But I want to KNOW what I'm paying for.

**Interviewer:** Would you want itemized pricing?

**Sunita:** YES. Not for every paisa, but at least: "Flights: ₹60K, Hotels: ₹40K, Our service fee: ₹15K, Taxes: ₹10K."

That way, if I think the service fee is too high, I can discuss it. Right now, it's a black box.

---

### Section 6: The Post-Trip Memory**

**Interviewer:** You mentioned you've done 2 trips with Priya. Did she remember the first one when planning the second?

**Sunita:** *(pause)* Not really.

For Vietnam, she asked: "Any dietary restrictions?" I said: "Kids are now vegetarians (they decided after that resort in Phuket)."

For the NEXT trip (we're thinking Japan), if she asks me again "Any dietary?" and I have to re-explain "kids are vegetarians now"... I'll be annoyed. Shouldn't she REMEMBER?

OTAs don't remember either. But I don't pay OTAs a commission. I DO pay Priya. If I'm paying for "personalized service," that should include REMEMBERING ME.

**Interviewer:** What if the tool remembered for her?

**Sunita:** That's the dream, right? "Sunita's kids are now vegetarians. Last trip: loved water slides, hated long walks. Japan itinerary adjusted accordingly."

If Priya showed up with THAT knowledge? I'd be like: "Okay, you're worth every rupee of that commission."

---

### Section 7: Dream Feature**

**Interviewer:** Your magic wand for the client experience?

**Sunita:** *(thinks for a long time)*

A "Client Portal" where I can:

1. **See my trip progress.** "Docs collected: 4/5. Options: generating. Booking: pending." Not chase Priya on WhatsApp.
2. **Compare options side-by-side.** Not 3 separate PDFs. A clean comparison table: price, highlights, trade-offs.
3. **See REAL reviews.** "TripAdvisor: 4.3/5, recent review: 'Great for kids but far from beach.'" That's the OTA magic I miss.
4. **Rate each trip.** After Vietnam: "Hotel 5/5, Transport 3/5 (late pickup), Kids' activities 5/5." So next time, Priya KNOWS what we loved/hated.
5. **Refer friends with confidence.** "Here's my portal link, you can see exactly what we did and loved."

The tool makes Priya faster. But the portal makes ME feel valued. And THAT's why I'd stay with her instead of going back to MMT.

## 4. Key Findings**

### Finding 1: OTA Switchers Have a "Feature Withdrawal"**

Sunita misses 3 OTA features: instant booking (3 min vs. 1-2 days), massive review databases, and price transparency. The agency tool must reclaim SOME of these to prevent "I'll just book on MMT" moments.

**Implication:** "Hybrid" features: include TripAdvisor/Google review snippets in options, provide price comparison context ("₹2K above market, but worth it because X"), and enable faster booking workflows (<4 hours from inquiry to confirmation).

### Finding 2: The Commission Black Box Creates Anxiety**

Sunita doesn't mind paying commission, but she wants to KNOW what she's paying for. "Total package ₹125K" without breakdown feels shady compared to OTA transparency.

**Implication:** Itemized pricing display: "Flights: ₹X, Hotels: ₹Y, Service Fee: ₹Z, Taxes: ₹W." Even a simple breakdown rebuilds trust and justifies the commission.

### Finding 3: Post-Trip Memory Is the Loyalty Lock-In**

The biggest gap: Priya didn't remember the kids are now vegetarians. For a commission-paying client, this feels like a breach of the "personalized service" promise. OTAs don't remember either — but they don't charge 15% commission.

**Implication:** Traveler memory/profile must persist across trips and be VISIBLE to the client: "Last trip: kids became vegetarians, loved water slides." This is the competitive moat vs. OTAs.

### Finding 4: AI Must Be Invisible to Clients**

Sunita is comfortable with AI assisting Priya, but if she felt "a robot planned my trip," she'd ask for her commission back. The agent's personality and voice must be preserved in all outputs.

**Implication:** All client-facing outputs must feel authored by the agent. No "Generated by AI" disclaimers. The tool is invisible; the agent's expertise is visible.

### Finding 5: OTA Speed Is the Biggest Competitive Threat**

3-minute booking on MMT vs. 1-2 days with Priya. For "instant gratification" trips, clients will switch back to OTAs unless the agency tool enables "fast lane" workflows.

**Implication:** A "Quick Quote" mode: simplified options in <4 hours for simple trips. Not every trip needs 3-day deep research. Speed matters for retention.

### Finding 6: Review Integration Is the Missing Data Layer**

Sunita trusts Priya's recommendations but wishes she could see "what 10,000 other people think" — the OTA review advantage. Integrating TripAdvisor/Google review snippets would blend OTA data depth with agency personalization.

**Implication:** Pull review snippets into option outputs: "TripAdvisor 4.3/5. Recent: 'Great for kids but far from beach.'" This adds OTA-grade confidence to agent recommendations.

### Finding 7: Client Portal = Feeling Valued**

Sunita's dream feature is a portal where she can see trip progress, compare options, rate experiences, and refer friends. This transforms her from "customer" to "partner."

**Implication:** Even a lightweight "share link" portal (trip status + option comparison + post-trip rating) would dramatically improve retention and referrals. Full portal is v2, but the link is v1.

### Finding 8: The "Personalized Service" Promise Must Be Delivered**

Sunita pays commission specifically for "personalized service." If the tool makes Priya faster but NOT more personal, the value proposition collapses. The tool must enhance personalization, not just efficiency.

**Implication:** Every client-facing output must include "Why this fits YOUR family" with specific details from their profile. Generic options = OTA behavior = no reason to pay commission.
