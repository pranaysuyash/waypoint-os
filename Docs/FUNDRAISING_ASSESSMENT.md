## 1. Should You Raise at All?

The skill asks: *"Determining whether to raise at all"* is the first question.

### The Case Against Raising (Stay Bootstrapped)

| Factor | Your Situation | Verdict |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| Capital efficiency | Breakeven at 7-12 customers. ₹15K/mo fixed costs. | ✅ Extremely efficient |
| Founder control | 100% ownership. No board, no investors. | ✅ Full control |
| Profitability path | v6 shows ₹5.6Cr profit in 36 months (Full PLG). | ✅ Profitable alone |
| Fundraising cost | 10-25% dilution + legal fees + months of distraction. | ❌ Expensive distraction |
| Speed vs sustainability | You'd grow slower without funding. But you'd still grow. | ⚖️ Trade-off |

### The Case For Raising

| Factor | What Money Unlocks |
|--------|-------------------|
| Hire faster | Customer success person, developer → build add-ons faster |
| Host agency partnerships | Travel costs, relationship building, dedicated outreach |
| Paid acquisition | Google Ads, LinkedIn Sponsored → faster than organic |
| Risk buffer | 24+ months runway instead of 6 |

### Verdict

**You don't NEED to raise.** The business works without it.

**But raising could make sense IF** you have a specific acceleration goal:
- "I want to close 5 host agency partnerships in 6 months" → needs capital for travel + outreach
- "I want to build 3 add-on modules this year" → needs a developer hire
- "I want to get to 200 customers in 18 months instead of 36" → needs marketing spend

Without a specific acceleration goal, stay bootstrapped. The distraction of fundraising isn't worth it.

## 4. Investor Pitch Narrative (If You Raise)

Based on what seed investors look for (40% team, 25% market, 20% traction, 10% product, 5% timing):

### Team (40% — Your Weakest Point)

As a solo founder, the team section is your biggest gap. Investors prefer 2+ founders.

**What you can say**: *"Solo by design. I'm building capital-efficiently with 6 years of industry domain expertise. First hire will be customer success within 3 months of funding."*

**Stronger option**: Find a technical co-founder or a travel industry partner before raising. This alone multiplies your valuation.

### Market (25% — Strong)

- 15,000+ boutique travel agencies in India
- ₹500Cr+ annual spend on tools and back-office
- Currently underserved (generic CRM or manual spreadsheets)
- Growing as travel rebounds post-COVID

### Traction (20% — Need to Build This)

Pre-fundraising, get:
- 10-20 paying customers at $20/mo
- 40%+ activation rate (first inquiry processed within 48h)
- 1 letter of intent from a host agency (50+ agents)
- Testimonial from at least 1 agency willing to be referenced

This alone makes your pre-seed raiseable.

### Product (10% — Strong)

Working MVP with 3-stage pipeline (intake, decision, strategy). Proven architecture with 38+ tests.

### Timing (5% — Strong)

- AI in travel is exploding but mostly focused on consumer (ChatGPT for trip planning)
- B2B operations for travel agencies is wide open
- WhatsApp is ubiquitous in India travel → perfect distribution channel

## 5. The Fundraising Roadmap (If You Decide to Raise)

```
Phase 1: Build Traction (3-6 months)
  - Get 10-20 paying customers (₹1.86-3.7L MRR)
  - Close 1 host agency LOI
  - Refine unit economics (know your LTV/CAC)
  - Document everything

Phase 2: Warm Up Investors (1-2 months)
  - Reach out to 10 angel investors with no ask
  - Share monthly updates
  - Build relationships before you need money

Phase 3: Raise (2-4 weeks)
  - Target: 15-20 investors
  - Run a structured 2-week process
  - 1 lead investor sets the terms
  - Close with 3-5 investors

Phase 4: Deploy
  - Hire for the specific acceleration goal
  - Don't change your cost structure overnight
  - Keep the same frugal mindset with more runway
```

## 6. Anti-Patterns to Avoid

From the skill, the ones most relevant to you:

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like for You | Prevent It |
|-------------|---------------------------|------------|
| Raising too early | "I need $100K to build the product." | Your MVP works. Raise only for acceleration. |
| Tourist investors | An investor who doesn't know travel tech | Only take money from investors who understand B2B SaaS or travel. |
| No warm intro | Cold emailing 50 VCs | Build relationships 3-6 months before raising. Start with 1 update email/month. |
| No "why now" story | "Travel agencies need better tools" | Why NOW? Answer: AI cost dropped 90% in 2025. WhatsApp + AI = perfect moment. |
| Uncapped SAFE | No valuation cap | Always set a cap. $3-5M for pre-seed is reasonable. |
