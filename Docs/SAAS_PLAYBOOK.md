## 1. The One-Page Sales Playbook

For a side project, you don't need a 20-page playbook. You need a single page you can glance at before a conversation.

```yaml
WAYPOINT OS — FOUNDER SALES PLAYBOOK

Your job: "Help travel agencies save time on inquiry processing."

Who you talk to: Agency owners (2-5 people, India metros)
What you sell: A $20/mo tool that cuts inquiry time by 60%
How you sell: DM conversations, no calls, no demos unless they ask
Free trial: 14 days, no credit card
Close rate to expect: ~15% of people who start a conversation

YOUR STAGES (from first message to paid):
  1. Connect (reply to their FB post / answer their question)
  2. Diagnose (DM: "How do you handle inquiries currently?")
  3. Prescribe ("I built a tool that does this automatically")
  4. Trial ("Try it free for 14 days. I'll set it up with you.")
  5. Onboard (help them process their first real inquiry)
  6. Convert (day 12: "Trial ends in 2 days. Want to keep going?")
  7. Retain (monthly check-in: "How's it going? Any questions?")
```

## 2. Conversation Scripts

### Opening DM (after they post about a pain point)

```
"Hey [name], I saw your post about [specific pain].
I had the same problem — was taking me 20 minutes
per inquiry just to figure out what the traveler wanted.

I built something that does that initial triage
automatically. Want to see if it works for you?"
```

### Discovery (qualifying questions)

```
Don't pitch yet. Ask first:

1. "How many inquiries do you get per week?"
   → If < 10: "Might not be worth it for you yet.
      Check back when you're busier."
   → If 10+: "Great, let me show you what I built."

2. "What's your current process — WhatsApp to spreadsheet?"
   → If they say yes, they're your customer.
   → If they say "I use a CRM," ask which one.
      If it's a travel-specific solution, they might not need you.

3. "What's the most frustrating part of that process?"
   → Listen for: "takes too long," "juniors make mistakes,"
      "I have to review everything myself"
   → Those are your hooks.

4. "If I could cut that time in half, would you try it?"
   → If yes → offer trial. If no → they're not feeling the pain yet.
```

### Handling the Trial

```
When they say yes to a trial, don't just send a link. Do this:

YOU: "Great. Paste one real inquiry you got today."
THEM: [pastes WhatsApp message]
YOU: "Give me 2 minutes." [process it in Waypoint OS]
YOU: [sends screenshot of result]
YOU: "Here's what it extracted. Is this accurate?
      Anything it missed?"

Then: "I'll set up your account. Check your email."

This 2-minute investment triples trial conversion.
```

## 3. Objection Handling

### "Too expensive" ($20/mo = ₹1,860)

```
DON'T: "I can give you 50% off."
DO: "I get it. How much time do you spend per inquiry?"
THEM: "About 20 minutes."
YOU: "At 50 inquiries/month, that's 17 hours.
      At ₹500/hr for your time, that's ₹8,500/month.
      The tool costs ₹1,860. It only needs to save you
      4 hours a month to pay for itself."
```

### "Happy with my current process"

```
"What does your current process look like?"
[...they describe manual process...]
"Got it. What would happen if one of your juniors
 misinterpreted a budget and quoted something impossible?
 How much time would that waste?"
 [...let them realize the cost of errors...]
```

### "Not now, busy season"

```
"Totally understand — busy season is when you need it most.
 How about I set you up in 5 minutes, you try it on
 ONE inquiry this week, and see if it helps?
 If it doesn't, no commitment."
```

### "What if the AI gets it wrong?"

```
"Fair question. The AI extracts what it can from the inquiry
 and flags anything it's unsure about. You always review
 before sending anything to the traveler.

 Think of it as a junior agent who does the first pass
 — you still do the final review. It saves you
 the 10 minutes of copy-pasting and extracting."
```

### "I'll think about it"

```
"Sure. What questions do you have?"
[...if no questions...]
"How about this: I'll send you a 2-minute video showing
 what it does with a real inquiry. You watch it when
 you have a moment, and I'll check in next week."
```

## 4. Competitive Positioning

### Who Are You Competing Against?

| Alternative | Your Advantage | Their Advantage |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Manual (spreadsheets + WhatsApp)** | 60% faster. Fewer errors. Customer memory. | "Free" (ignores time cost) |
| **Generic CRM (Zoho, HubSpot)** | Travel-specific pipeline. No setup needed. | More features. Brand recognition. |
| **ChatGPT** | Built for travel. Pipeline keeps context. Customer memory. | More flexible. Cheaper. |
| **Doing nothing** | You save them time and money. | No risk. No change. |

### Your Positioning Statement

```
For boutique travel agencies drowning in manual inquiry processing,
Waypoint OS is the AI ops copilot that cuts inquiry-to-quote time by 60%
— unlike generic CRMs or spreadsheets that weren't built for travel.
```

### When Asked "How Is This Different From ChatGPT"

```
"ChatGPT is great for answering questions.
 Waypoint OS is built specifically for travel inquiries:

 1. It knows travel terminology (destinations, visa types, fare classes)
 2. It follows a structured pipeline — intake → gap analysis → strategy
 3. It remembers customer preferences across trips
 4. It flags gaps and contradictions (missing dates, budget too low)

 Think of it as ChatGPT + a travel operations manual + a CRM
 — all in one, specifically for travel agencies."
```

## 5. The Side Project Sales Cadence

### Weekly Routine (4-6 hours/week)

| Day | Activity | Time |
|-----|----------|------|
| Monday | 20 min in FB groups (answer questions, look for pain signals) | 20 min |
| Tuesday | Reply to DMs, follow up with trials from last week | 30 min |
| Wednesday | 20 min in FB groups | 20 min |
| Thursday | Process feedback from current users, make 1 small fix | 1-2 hrs |
| Friday | Weekly check-in with all active trials (DM or email) | 30 min |
| Weekend | 1 deeper conversation (qualify, demo, onboard a new trial) | 1 hr |

**Total: 4-6 hrs/week on sales + support.** The rest is product.

## 6. Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | Prevention |
|-------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Discounting too fast** | "I'll give you 50% off to try it." If they don't see value at $20, they won't see it at $10 either. | Hold the price. Offer more value instead (setup help, priority support). |
| **Chasing every lead** | Spending 2 hours with someone who processes 3 inquiries/month. | Qualify early. If they're too small, tell them politely and move on. |
| **No follow-up system** | "I'll remember to check in with them." You won't. | Use the spreadsheet. Batch follow-ups on Fridays. |
| **Over-explaining features** | "It uses a two-loop pipeline with NB01, NB02..." Nobody cares. | Talk about outcomes, not features. "It saves you 10 minutes per inquiry." |
| **Being too available** | Responding to DMs at 11 PM on a Saturday. | Set boundaries. Respond within 24 hours during weekdays. |
