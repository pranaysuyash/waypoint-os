# LinkedIn Outbound for Waypoint OS

**Date**: 2026-04-30
**Target**: "travel industry agency owner" (your search)
**Product**: $20/mo AI ops copilot for travel agencies
**Cost consideration**: $20/mo product + LinkedIn outbound needs a light touch (30 min/prospect doesn't make sense)

---

## 1. The Math Problem

$20/mo product + personalized LinkedIn outreach = doesn't work if you spend 30 min per prospect.

```
Your time: ₹500/hr (conservative)
30 min per prospect = ₹250 cost  
Close rate: ~10% → ₹2,500 cost per acquired customer
At $20/mo ($1,860/mo): payback in 1.3 months

BUT you only have 10-15 hrs/week. Every outreach minute counts.
```

**Solution**: Don't sell on LinkedIn. Use it to build a pipeline that converts elsewhere.

| LinkedIn's job | Not LinkedIn's job |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Get them to follow you | Close the sale |
| Get them to your website | Pricing discussions |
| Build awareness of the problem | Onboarding |
| Start a conversation | Support |

---

## 2. The Strategy: Warm, Then Convert

LinkedIn for travel agency owners is a lower-density channel than Facebook groups (where most agents actually hang out). But agency OWNERS — the decision-makers — are more concentrated here.

### The 3-Touch System

```
TOUCH 1: Connect (with note)
TOUCH 2: Add value (DM after they accept)
TOUCH 3: Soft CTA (link to blog/tool, no pitch)
```

No sales pitch in DMs. The goal is to move them to your blog or free tool.

## 4. Connection Request Templates

Keep these under 300 characters (LinkedIn limit for connection notes).

### Template A: Industry observation (best)

```
Hi [Name] — noticed you run [agency name]. I've been researching how travel agencies handle inquiry overload and thought your perspective would be valuable. Would love to connect.
```

### Template B: Shared context

```
Hi [Name] — I'm building something for travel agencies to help with inquiry management. Would love to hear your take on what tools actually work (or don't). Appreciate a connect.
```

### Template C: Compliment + bridge

```
Hi [Name] — great post about [topic]. I write about travel agency operations and think your experience would add a lot to the conversation. Happy to connect.
```

### What NOT to use

```
❌ "I came across your profile and was impressed by your experience..."
   → Everyone gets this. Zero signal.

❌ "I'd love to share my product with you..."
   → Too salesy. They'll ignore.

❌ "I see you're in the travel industry..."
   → They know. Say something specific.
```

## 5. DM Sequence (After Connection)

### Step 1: Welcome DM (send after they accept, within 24 hrs)

```
Thanks for connecting, [Name]. 

I've been digging into how agencies handle the initial inquiry process — from WhatsApp message to first quote. Curious: what's the most time-consuming part of that flow for you?

Just researching, no pitch. Would love to hear your take.
```

**Goal**: Start a conversation. Don't pitch.

### Step 2: If they reply (engage with their answer)

```
That's exactly what I've been hearing. The extraction phase is where most of the time goes.

I actually wrote up some research on this — [link to blog post]. Quick read, might resonate.

Either way, happy to stay connected and share more as I learn.
```

**Goal**: Drive them to content, not a sales page.

### Step 3: Follow-up (1 week later, if no reply to step 1)

```
No worries if you're busy — totally get it.

If you ever want to see a quick demo of something I'm building that handles the initial inquiry extraction automatically, happy to show you (5 min, no pressure).

Either way, glad to be connected.
```

**Goal**: One low-pressure offer, then done.

### If they say "show me"

```
Sure thing. The simplest way: paste one real inquiry you've received recently into this — [link to a loom video or a live demo page]. It processes it in about 30 seconds.

If that seems useful, happy to set you up with a free trial.
```

## 6. Content to Post (Builds Inbound Pipeline)

You need to post so they come to YOU instead of you chasing them.

### Post Ideas (15 min each to write)

| Post Topic | Format | Why It Works |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| "The average agency loses 1 in 5 leads to slow response times" | Single insight | Pain point, shareable |
| "I asked 50 travel agencies what their biggest operational headache is" | Data point | Research-based, credible |
| "A junior agent costs you ₹25K/month. This tool costs ₹1,860." | Comparison | Creates curiosity |
| "Before you build another itinerary, ask these 3 questions first" | How-to | Useful, saves them time |
| "I built a SaaS for travel agencies as a side project — here's what I learned" | Founder story | Authentic, relatable |

### Post Structure

```
HEADLINE (1 line, bold, problem-focused):
"The #1 mistake travel agencies make with new inquiries"

BODY (3-5 lines, scannable):
They respond too fast without understanding the full picture.
They quote blind — and eat the cost of their own assumptions.
They lose money on trips that were impossible from the start.

SOLUTION (1 line):
A simple 3-step checklist before you quote anything.

CTA (1 line, low pressure):
Full breakdown linked in comments.
```

### Engagement Rule

For every post you publish, spend 5 min replying to every comment. That's where the relationships happen.

## 8. Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | Fix |
|-------------|-------------------|-----|
| **Pitching in connection request** | "I have a tool that..." | Connection note = why connect, not what you sell |
| **DMing too fast** | Sending a pitch 2 min after they accept | Wait 24 hrs. Let the connection breathe. |
| **No profile optimization** | Your profile says "builder" or "founder" with no mention of travel | Update headline: "Building Waypoint OS — AI for travel agencies. Previously [X]." |
| **Mass same message** | 50 people get identical DMs | Personalize the first line. Reference their profile. |
| **Too much time** | Spending 2 hrs/week on LinkedIn for 1 signup/month | LinkedIn = 1 hr/week max. Rest goes to FB groups + WhatsApp. |
