## Core Advice

> "Stop building. Go talk to customers."

The single strongest signal from the conversation: **product building should pause until customer development validates the assumptions.**

### Key Points

| Her Point | What It Means for Waypoint OS |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| **Deep market research first** | We have 200+ docs of internal research, but zero conversations with actual travel agency owners. The docs are hypotheses. Need to validate them. |
| **Understand specific pain points / jobs-to-be-done** | We assume agencies struggle with inquiry processing. Is that actually their #1 pain? Or is it something else (retaining clients, managing suppliers, cash flow)? |
| **Pick one niche** | Travel agencies. Not travel + real estate + e-commerce. One ICP, dominated, then expand. |
| **Consulting vs productized vs SaaS is a question for after customer discovery** | Don't decide the business model until you know what they'll pay for and how. |
| **Positioning is the layer between product and market** | "I build AI tools" is not positioning. "I help travel agencies stop losing deals due to slow response" is. |
| **Cold outreach only works with clear ICP and positioning** | LinkedIn outreach will fail if the message is vague. Needs to be specific to their pain. |

---

## What Needs to Change

### Stop

```
- Building new features
- Adding to the TODO list
- Optimizing the pipeline threshold
- Writing more internal docs
```

### Start

```
- Finding 5-10 travel agency owners to talk to
- Asking them about their actual workflow, not pitching them
- Understanding what they currently use, what frustrates them, what they'd pay for
- Letting them tell you what the product should be
```

### The One Question

> "What is the most frustrating part of handling a new trip inquiry from first message to booked trip?"

Don't pitch. Don't demo. Just listen.

## Customer Interview Guide

### Who to Talk To

| Priority | Type | How to Find |
|----------|------|-------------|
| P0 | Agency owner (2-5 staff) | LinkedIn, FB groups, warm intros |
| P1 | Solo travel agent | FB groups, WhatsApp communities |
| P2 | Host agency ops manager | LinkedIn |

### What to Ask

```
Warm-up (2 min):
  "Tell me about your agency — how long have you been running it?"

Problem exploration (15 min):
  "Walk me through what happens when a new inquiry comes in on WhatsApp."
  "What's the most time-consuming part of that?"
  "What happens when things go wrong?"
  "What have you tried to fix this?"

Current solutions (5 min):
  "What tools do you use today?"
  "What do you like about them? What frustrates you?"
  "What would your ideal solution look like?"

Willingness to pay (3 min):
  "If a tool could save you X hours per week, what would that be worth to you?"
  "Have you ever paid for a SaaS tool before?"

Wrap-up (2 min):
  "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?"
  "Can I follow up if I have more questions?"
```

### What NOT to Do

```
❌ Pitch your product
❌ Say "I'm building something"
❌ Try to sell them
❌ Defend your ideas
❌ Ask leading questions ("Would you use a tool that...?")
```

## How This Changes Everything

The approach flips:

```
BEFORE: Build → Test internally → Polish → Launch → Find customers
AFTER: Find customers → Talk to them → Understand pain → Build what they'll pay for
```

This doesn't mean the code is wasted. The pipeline WORKS. The question is whether it solves a pain people will actually pay to fix. The next 2 weeks of customer conversations will answer that.
