# ChatGPT Advice — Customer Discovery & Positioning

**Date**: 2026-04-30
**Source**: ChatGPT analysis following Ayse call
**Alignment**: Strongly confirms Ayse's core advice. Adds tactical depth.

---

## Core Thesis (Same as Ayse)

> Don't build. Don't pitch. Go talk to customers first.

But ChatGPT adds much more tactical specificity. What follows is what to CHANGE in our approach based on that.

---

## 1. ICP — More Specific Than We Had

**Before**: "Travel agency owners" (too broad)
**Now**:

```
Small outbound leisure travel agencies in India handling custom family/international trips over WhatsApp and email.
3-20 employees. Owner-operated. Custom itineraries, not fixed package resale.
```

**Why this matters**: Generic "travel agencies" includes corporate TMCs, ticketing agents, package resellers, destination management companies — all with different workflows. We need the ones doing custom itineraries via WhatsApp.

**Where to find them**: Instagram is mentioned as a strong signal. Agencies doing custom trips usually post itineraries, destination photos, and client testimonials. If their Instagram shows custom work, they're likely in our ICP.

---

## 2. Volume — We Were Thinking Too Small

| Before (our doc) | Now (ChatGPT) |
|-----------------|---------------|
| 10-20 connection requests | 50 over 7-10 days |
| Expected 2-5 calls | Expect 5-10 calls |
| Minimum: 5 calls | Minimum: 7-10 calls |
| Decision after 5 | Decision after 10 |

The reason: 5 calls can reveal obvious dead ends but isn't enough to choose a wedge confidently. You need to hear the same pain from multiple people before you know it's real.

---

## 3. Two Discovery Tracks — Not Just One

Our doc focused on "inquiry handling." ChatGPT suggests splitting discovery into two tracks because we don't yet know which pain is bigger:

| Track | Focus | Key Question |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Track A** | Inquiry intake & qualification | "How do inquiries come in? What's missing? How many back-and-forths?" |
| **Track B** | Follow-up & lead leakage | "Where do you track open inquiries? How often do leads go cold? What happens if nobody follows up?" |

**Ask both in every call.** Don't lead. See which one they complain about more without prompting.

---

## 4. Commercial Questions — We Missed These

Our doc had good workflow questions but no commercial qualification. Without these, you might discover a real pain that nobody pays to fix.

**Add to every interview:**

```
How many inquiries per week?
How many become serious leads?
How many convert into bookings?
What's the average booking value?
How quickly do you need to respond to win the customer?
Do you currently pay anyone or any tool to manage this?
```

If they handle 5 inquiries/week, ₹2L average booking, and say "response within 2 hours matters" — you have a commercial wedge.

If they handle 3 inquiries/week, ₹5K average, "no rush" — you don't.

---

## 5. DM Script — Handle "What Are You Building?"

Our doc said "don't mention it." ChatGPT agrees but provides a better response if they ask directly:

```
"I've built automation tools before, including one paid tool
around signature extraction. For this travel agency work,
I'm still in discovery — trying to understand the real workflow
before deciding what to build or offer."
```

This gives credibility without pitching.

---

## 6. The Three Wedge Options

ChatGPT lays out three specific offers to test in conversations:

| Option | Offer | Risk Level | ChatGPT Pick |
|--------|-------|------------|--------------|
| **1. Trip inquiry intake → structured brief** | Turn messy WhatsApp/email into structured trip brief with missing questions | Lowest | ✅ **Start here** |
| **2. Quote/itinerary generation** | Generate first-draft itineraries from structured brief | Higher | ❌ Too early |
| **3. Follow-up & lead recovery** | Auto-follow-up on open leads, pending quotes | Medium | ✅ Test as #2 |

**First wedge**: Trip inquiry intake. This maps directly to what your pipeline already does (NB01-NB02).

**Second pain to test**: Follow-up automation. Revenue-adjacent, easier ROI story.

**Do not start with**: Full itinerary generation. Too tempting, too risky, pulls you into harder product too early.

---

## 7. The First Offer (After Discovery)

Not "$20/mo SaaS." Consulting → productized service first.

| Tier | Price | What They Get |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Discovery Audit** | ₹10K-20K | Workflow map + automation plan |
| **Setup Package** | ₹50K-1.5L | Working system for one workflow |
| **Monthly Partner** | ₹25K-75K/mo | Support, improvements, new automations |

ChatGPT specifically warns against the $20/mo anchor: *"For this category, $20/month may be too low if you're solving a real revenue problem. High-touch onboarding, messy data, WhatsApp workflows, and support will eat the economics."*

---

## 8. Track Exact Words

Our doc said "take notes." ChatGPT adds: capture their EXACT language.

> "Your positioning should come from their language, not your builder language."

If 3 different owners say "I hate the back-and-forth" — that's your positioning.
If they say "I lose leads because I take too long to respond" — that's your value prop.

Don't translate their words into SaaS-speak. Use their words.

---

## 9. Decision Criteria After 10 Calls

| Signal | What You Want to Hear |
|--------|----------------------|
| Pain frequency | Happens weekly or daily |
| Pain intensity | They complain without prompting |
| Money link | Affects response time, conversion, revenue |
| Current workaround | Excel, WhatsApp, manual, copy-paste (NOT a CRM) |
| Buying possibility | Owner can decide without approvals |

**Continue if**: 6+ mention same pain, 4+ say it affects revenue, 3+ agree to review a demo later, 1-2 show willingness to pay.

**Pause if**: Everyone has different problems, pain is mild, they already use tools, volume is too low.

## Addendum — Corrections & Nuance (from follow-up feedback)

The main thesis stands: pause product-building, run discovery, narrow ICP, identify repeated pain, THEN decide the business model.

But the first draft overstated a few things. Here's what to correct:

### 1. "Don't build" → "Don't expand product scope before discovery"

Too absolute as written. You may still need a simple demo artifact later. The rule is: don't build new features based on assumptions. Maintaining what exists for learning/demos is fine.

### 2. Instagram is a sourcing hypothesis, not confirmed

Plausible, but not proven. Keep it as a signal to test, not a sourcing strategy to rely on.

### 3. Pricing bands are too specific too early

The ₹10K-20K / ₹50K-1.5L / ₹25K-75K/mo pricing should not be in a strategy doc. It can bias interviews and planning. Better to note: "possible models may include audit, setup, or monthly managed — decide after discovery shows pain intensity and willingness to pay."

### 4. "Consulting → productized service first" is likely, not decided

The logic is: consulting or productized service is the likely path if workflows vary by agency. Software comes later if the same workflow repeats. Keep the decision tied to evidence, not assumption.

### 5. "NB01-NB02" should not appear in a strategy doc

Internal pipeline names don't belong in a customer-facing or strategic doc unless everyone reading knows them. Replace with: "maps well to the current product direction" or similar.

### 6. 10 calls is a first checkpoint, not final validation

Enough to see early patterns. Not enough to finalize the business. After 10 calls: decide continue, narrow, or switch. Don't treat as conclusive.

### Clean verdict

Three things to tighten:

1. Label pricing and offers as hypotheses, not decisions.
2. Keep "don't build" as "don't expand product scope before discovery."
3. Avoid turning travel-agency assumptions into conclusions until interviews confirm them.

**The strongest line to protect**: "Your positioning should come from their language, not your builder language."
