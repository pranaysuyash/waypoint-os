## 1. The Profitability-First Mindset

The skill says: *"Every dollar spent must generate > $1 in return within 12 months."*

For Waypoint OS:

| Spend Decision | Question to Ask | Current Answer |
|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| Hire a customer success person (₹30K/mo) | Will this reduce churn enough to pay for itself? | Not yet — 50-100 customers first |
| Run Google Ads (₹50K/mo) | Will this generate 3x in 90 days? | No — CAC too high. Start with organic. |
| Build flight tracking add-on | Will this unlock revenue? | Yes — if 3 agencies pre-commit to paying for it |
| Upgrade hosting plan (₹15K→₹30K) | Can I afford NOT to? | No — only if performance is suffering |

**Your advantage**: Fixed costs are ₹15K/mo. You breakeven at 7-12 customers. That's a business that can't lose much money.

## 2. Customer-Funded Development

This is the most powerful pattern for a solo founder: **get paid to build features before you build them.**

### How to apply to Waypoint OS

Instead of building flight tracking or price intelligence on spec:

```
1. Find 3 agencies who say "I wish Waypoint had flight tracking"
2. Offer: "I'll build it in 2 weeks. You pay ₹2,000/mo add-on starting day 1."
3. If 3 say yes: ₹6,000/mo guaranteed before writing a line of code
4. Build it. You have paying beta testers. Zero risk.
```

### Other customer-funded opportunities

| Opportunity | What to Offer | Revenue |
|-------------|--------------|---------|
| Specific integration (e.g., Sabre API) | "₹50K to build your integration, ₹5K/mo thereafter" | ₹50K upfront + ₹5K/mo |
| Custom inquiry fields | "₹20K to add your custom fields, included in your plan" | ₹20K upfront |
| White-label for host agency | "₹1L setup + ₹25K/mo for unlimited agents" | ₹1L + ₹25K/mo |

**This is how bootstrapped SaaS works**: customers fund your development. You never build on spec.

## 4. Seasonality — Travel Agency Calendar

The skill highlights the importance of knowing your customer's seasons. For travel agencies:

| Period | Agency Activity | What You Should Do |
|--------|----------------|--------------------|
| Jan-Feb | Peak booking (summer holidays) | **Sell hard**. Outreach, onboarding, convert. Best time to acquire. |
| Mar-Apr | Shoulder season | **Content**. Write case studies from busy season. Build SEO. |
| May-Jun | Wedding/honeymoon bookings | **Upsell**. Agencies are busy. Push add-ons, seat packs. |
| Jul-Aug | Peak travel (execution mode) | **Support**. Agencies are executing trips. Don't sell — support well. |
| Sep-Oct | Planning season (next year) | **Product**. Build features. Quiet time. |
| Nov-Dec | Last-minute bookings + holidays | **Sell + reflect**. Smaller push. Plan next year. |

**Practical takeaway**: January and February are your best months for acquisition. Don't run ads in July/August — agencies are too busy executing trips to evaluate tools.

## 5. Metrics to Track (Revenue-First)

Beyond the v6 model metrics, track these weekly:

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Revenue growth rate | 10-30%/mo | Leading indicator |
| Profit margin | 30-50% | Must hit this before scaling |
| Cash runway | 12+ months | Safety net |
| Paying customer growth | 10-20%/mo | PMF signal |
| Revenue concentration | No customer > 10% | If one agency is 30% of revenue, you're fragile |

## 6. Anti-Patterns to Avoid

The skill flags 5 anti-patterns. Here's what they look like for Waypoint OS:

| Anti-Pattern | What to Watch For |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Growing into unprofitability** | "Let's spend on ads to grow faster." Ads have high CAC early. Grow organically until profit margin is confirmed. |
| **Not raising prices** | Your $20 base is reasonable. But when you add flight tracking as an add-on, don't underprice it. ₹2,000/mo for real-time flight monitoring is cheap compared to what agencies lose in disruption costs. |
| **No cash reserves** | v6 shows profit from month 3. Don't reinvest every rupee. Keep 6 months of fixed costs (₹90K) in the bank. |
| **Burning out trying to grow fast** | "I need 50 customers by month 6." Realistic: 15-20. Slow, steady growth wins. |
| **Not building a moat** | As you get customers, they store inquiry history, traveler preferences, past trips in your system. That's switching cost. But also think: what makes it hard for a competitor to copy? Travel domain expertise in your pipeline is one answer. |
