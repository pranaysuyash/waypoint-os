# Pilot and Customer Discovery Strategy

**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Purpose**: How to find willing agencies and run pilots

---

## Part 1: Customer Discovery (Before You Build)

### The Question

> "How do I check who's willing to pilot?"

**Answer**: You don't ask "will you pilot?" — you first understand their pain.

---

## Step 1: Find Where Agencies Are

### Facebook Groups (Low Friction Entry)

Travel agency groups are active and discuss real problems daily:

| Group | Type | How to approach |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| **Travel Agents Association of India** | Professional | Comment helpful advice, DM active members |
| **Home Based Travel Agents** | Solo/freelance | Share tools, ask about their workflow |
| **Travel Agents Forum** | Mixed | Post polls about planning pain points |
| **Luxury Travel Advisors** | High-ticket | Comment on posts about complex trips |

**Strategy**:
1. Join 5-10 groups
2. Spend 1-2 weeks just listening
3. Identify people posting about:
   - "How do you handle complicated requests?"
   - "Tired of back-and-forth with clients"
   - "My junior agents keep making mistakes"
   - "How do you scale without hiring?"

### LinkedIn (More Formal)

Search for:
- "Travel agency owner" or "Independent travel advisor"
- Filter by 2-50 employees (your sweet spot)
- Look at posts about scaling, operations, hiring

**Strategy**:
1. Connect with 5-10 agency owners per week
2. Personalized message (no templates):
   > "Hi [Name], saw your post about [specific pain]. I'm building a tool to help with that — can I ask 3 questions about your current workflow?"

### Host Agencies (High Leverage)

Host agencies already have hundreds of sub-agents under them:

| Host | Why interesting |
|------|-----------------|
| **Classic Vacations** | Large network, tech-forward |
| **Avoya Travel** | Host model with independent agents |
| **Travel Planners International** | Focus on luxury |
| **Cruise Planners** | Niche but huge network |

**Strategy**:
1. Find their "technology partnership" or "vendor" contact
2. Pitch: "Tool that makes your sub-agents more productive"
3. Host gets revenue share → they have incentive to help you pilot

### WhatsApp/Telegram Communities

Many Indian travel agencies operate in WhatsApp groups:
- Travel agent trade groups
- Regional tourism groups
- B2B travel supplier groups

**How to find**:
- Ask any agency owner you know: "What groups are you in?"
- Search "travel agent WhatsApp group" on Facebook/Google

## Step 2: Discovery Calls (The "3 Questions" Framework)

**Don't pitch. Listen.**

### Goal of Call

Understand:
1. Is this a real problem?
2. How do they solve it now?
3. Would they pay to solve it?

### The Script

**Opening**:
> "Thanks for taking 15 minutes. I'm not selling anything — just trying to understand if a problem I've heard about is real. I'll ask 3 questions, then we're done."

**Question 1**: "Walk me through your trip planning process from when a client first reaches out to when you send options."

   - *Listen for*: How many messages back and forth? Where do they get stuck? What takes the most time?

**Question 2**: "What's the most frustrating or repetitive part of that process?"

   - *Listen for*: Specific pain points. Vague answers = not a real problem yet.

**Question 3**: "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your planning workflow, what would it be?"

   - *Listen for*: This is your feature priority list.

**Closing**:
> "This is really helpful. I'm building something that might help with [specific pain they mentioned]. Can I follow up in 2-3 weeks when I have something to show?"

**If they say "yes, I'd love to see it"** → Warm lead
**If they say "sure, but I'm busy"** → Cold lead
**If they say "we're happy with our current process"** → Not a fit

## Step 3: The "Pilot" Proposal

**Don't call it a "pilot"** — that sounds like work for them.

### Three Approaches

### Approach A: "Free Beta Tester" (Low Commitment)

**Best for**: Early discovery, validating fit

> "I'm building a tool to help with [specific pain]. Want to try it for free? No commitment — just use it when you have a tricky trip and tell me if it helps."

**Pros**:
- Low barrier to say yes
- You get real feedback
- No pressure on either side

**Cons**:
- Low commitment → might not actually use it
- Hard to get structured feedback

### Approach B: "Design Partner" (Higher Commitment)

**Best for**: Agencies you have good rapport with

> "I'm looking for 3-5 design partners to help shape this product. You get:
> - Free lifetime use (or 2 years free)
> - Direct influence on what gets built
> - Priority support
>
> In exchange, I ask for:
> - 1 real trip per week processed through the tool
> - 15-min feedback call every 2 weeks
> - Honest feedback on what doesn't work"

**Pros**:
- Structured feedback loop
- They feel invested in success
- You get champions for later

**Cons**:
- Fewer agencies will say yes
- Requires more relationship-building

### Approach C: "Paid Pilot" (Real Validation)

**Best for**: When you have working MVP

> "I have a working tool. Looking for 5 agencies to try it for 2 months at 50% off (₹500/month for Solo tier). If it doesn't save you at least 5 hours/week, I'll refund 100%."

**Pros**:
- REAL validation — they're paying
- Forces you to build something actually useful
- Creates reference customers

**Cons**:
- Much harder to get yes
- Product must be working

## Step 4: How Many Pilots?

| Stage | Number | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Discovery** | 10-15 conversations | Understand problem space |
| **Design partners** | 3-5 agencies | Build the right thing |
| **Beta testers** | 10-20 agencies | Find bugs, edge cases |
| **Paid pilots** | 5-10 agencies | Prove people will pay |

**Don't try to scale too early.** 3-5 committed agencies > 50 lukewarm ones.

## Part 5: Making Pilots Successful

### Onboarding (First Call)

**Cover**:
1. Their current workflow (understand it deeply)
2. One real trip they're working on NOW
3. Process it together through your tool
4. Identify gaps together
5. Set expectation: "I'll check in weekly"

### Weekly Check-in

**Structure**:
> "How was it this week? Any trips where the tool helped? Any where it didn't?"

**Document**:
- What worked
- What didn't work
- Feature requests (categorize: must-have / nice-to-have / later)

### The "Happy Path" Pilot

For first pilot agencies, **hand-hold aggressively**:

1. **Week 1**: Do it together on a call
2. **Week 2**: They try with you on standby (WhatsApp)
3. **Week 3**: They try alone, you review after
4. **Week 4**: They use it independently

**If they can't do week 4 without help, your UX is not ready.**

## Part 7: Red Flags in Pilots

### Agencies to AVOID

| Red Flag | Why |
|----------|-----|
| "We're too busy to try this" | Will never make time |
| "Our owner needs to approve everything" | Too slow, no access to decision-maker |
| "We already use [complicated tool]" | entrenched in existing workflow |
| "Can you customize this for us?" | You're not a dev shop, you're building a product |
| "We'll try it next quarter" | procrastination = no |

### Signs of GOOD Pilot Agencies

| Green Flag | Why |
|------------|-----|
| "This exact thing is causing me pain right now" | Real problem, urgent |
| "I have a trip I'm working on that we could try" | Immediate use case |
| "I'm the owner/decision-maker" | Can say yes without asking anyone |
| "We're small but growing" | Needs tools to scale, not entrenched |
| "I've tried building something like this" | Understands the problem deeply |

## Part 8: Converting Pilots to Customers

### When to Ask for Money

**Timing**: After they've had at least one "aha moment"

**Signs they're ready**:
- They use it without you reminding them
- They tell you about a success ("this saved me 2 hours!")
- They ask "when can I get more features?" (engagement)
- They refer it to someone else (champion behavior)

### The Ask

> "I'm really glad this is helping. I'm planning to launch this properly soon. For early design partners, I'm offering:
> - 50% off for the first year
> - Your price locked in forever
> - Input on roadmap priorities
>
> Would you like to continue as a paid customer when we launch?"

**If yes** → You have your first customer
**If no** → Ask why gently: "What would make this worth paying for?"

### Simple Spreadsheet

| Agency | Contact | Type | Status | Weekly usage | Feedback | Next check-in |
|--------|---------|------|--------|--------------|----------|---------------|
| Priya Travels | priya@... | Design Partner | Active | 2 trips/week | "Love the constraint extraction" | 2026-04-21 |

### Status Values

- **Discovery**: Had first call, assessing fit
- **Design Partner**: Committed to weekly feedback
- **Beta Tester**: Using it, lighter commitment
- **Paid Pilot**: Paying customer
- **Dormant**: Not using, need re-engagement
- **Churned**: Stopped using, find out why

## Part 10: Quick Reference Scripts

### Cold DM for Discovery

> "Hi [Name], saw your post about [specific pain point]. I'm building a tool to help travel agents with exactly that. Can I ask 3 questions about your current process? No sales pitch, just research. 15 min zoom or WhatsApp voice?"

### After Discovery Call

> "Thanks so much for your time today. Your feedback on [specific thing they said] was really helpful. I'm building this now — can I follow up in 3 weeks when I have something you can try?"

### Pilot Invitation

> "Hey [Name], based on our chat, I think you'd be a great fit to be an early design partner. You'd get free lifetime use + direct input on features. In exchange, I'd ask for weekly feedback on one real trip. Interested?"

### Weekly Check-in

> "How was it this week? Any wins or frustrations with the tool? Quick update here or we can chat 5 mins on WhatsApp."

---

## Summary

1. **Start with discovery**, not pilots. Understand the problem first.
2. **Find agencies in pain**, not just "agencies."
3. **Use design partners** for committed feedback.
4. **Hand-hold early pilots** — your UX is probably bad.
5. **Convert to paid** after they have an "aha moment."
6. **3-5 committed agencies** > 50 lukewarm ones.

**The goal isn't "pilots" — it's understanding whether you're building something people will pay for.**
