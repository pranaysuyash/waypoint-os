## 1. GTM Motion: Founder-Led Hybrid

For a side project, there's only one viable motion:

**Founder-led hybrid** = you do the selling, product does the converting.

| What it means practically |
|--------------------------|
| You find agencies (FB groups, WhatsApp) |
| You qualify them (chat, not calls) |
| They sign up for a free trial (self-serve) |
| They convert to paid (auto, no sales call needed) |
| You support them (email, check-in once a month) |

**No sales-led** (no demo calls, no proposals, no contracts).  
**No pure PLG** (you still need to find them — nobody discovers Waypoint OS by accident yet).

## 2. ICP — Who Exactly to Target

### Primary ICP (First 20 Customers)

```yaml
ICP: Boutique Travel Agency Owner
  Agency size: 2-5 people (owner + 1-4 staff)
  Location: India metros (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore)
  Revenue: ₹24-60L/yr
  Current setup: WhatsApp + spreadsheets + email
  Pain points:
    - Owner reviews every single quote before it goes out
    - Juniors make errors (wrong dates, wrong budget, missed preferences)
    - Takes 20-30 min per inquiry just to extract what the traveler wants
    - Losing deals because response is too slow
  Tech comfort: Uses WhatsApp daily. Has Gmail. May have tried Zoho CRM once.
  Budget: ₹1-5K/month for tools (will pay out of pocket if it saves time)
  Buying trigger: Just lost a deal because of slow response OR junior made a costly error
  Channel: Facebook travel agent groups, WhatsApp agent communities
```

### Secondary ICP (After 20 Customers)

```yaml
ICP: Host Agency Operations Manager
  Responsibility: Provide tools and training to 50-500 independent agents
  Pain: Can't ensure quality across all agents. Support burden is high.
  Decision: Needs approval from owner/CEO
  Budget: ₹25-50K/month for a platform that serves all their agents
  Channel: LinkedIn, direct outreach, industry events
```

### ICP Validation Checklist

Before spending time on outreach, confirm:

| Question | How to Check |
|----------|-------------|
| Do they actually use WhatsApp for inquiries? | Ask in the first conversation |
| How many inquiries do they get per week? | < 10 is too small. > 10 is worth pursuing. |
| What do they use now? | If they say "nothing, I just handle it myself" — pain is high |
| Have they lost a deal recently because of slow response? | Yes = ready to buy |
| Who makes the call on new tools? | If "me" — can close in one conversation |
| What do they pay for other tools? | If ₹0 — will be hard to convert |

## 3. Channel Strategy for a Side Project

As a solo founder with 10-15 hrs/week, you can only do 2 channels well. Pick these:

### Channel 1: Facebook Groups (P0)

| Group | Members | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| Travel Agents Community | 50K+ | Largest India travel agent group |
| Travel Agents India | 20K+ | Active daily |
| Travel Agents Only | 15K+ | High engagement |
| Regional groups (Bangalore Travel Agents, etc.) | 5-10K | Local, personal |

**Strategy**: NOT "post about your product." Here's the actual workflow:

```
Week 1-2: Just answer questions. Be helpful.
  "How do you handle budget blowouts?" →
  "Here's my approach: always get a signed confirmation
   before doing detailed research. Saves hours."

Week 3-4: Share a useful resource.
  "I built a simple checklist for vetting international
   trip viability. Happy to share if useful."

Week 5-6: Mention your tool (as a solution to a problem they have).
  "Someone asked about managing inquiry overload.
   I actually built a tool that does the initial
   triage automatically. Happy to show a few of you."

Week 7+: DM the interested ones. Offer a free trial.
```

**Time budget**: 20 min/day, 3 days/week. Answer 2-3 questions. Don't scroll.

### Channel 2: WhatsApp Communities (P0)

Find existing WhatsApp groups for travel agents. How:

- Ask your first 5 customers: "Are you in any agent WhatsApp groups?"
- Many agent communities have their own groups
- Search for "travel agents India WhatsApp group" on Google/Facebook

**Strategy**: Same as Facebook — be helpful first. Never share a link on your first message in a group.

## 4. The Sales Process (It's Not Sales, It's Helping)

As a side project founder, you don't "sell." You **diagnose and prescribe**.

```
STEP 1: Find someone with the pain
  FB post: "Ugh, lost another deal because I took 6 hours to
  get back to the client. Anyone else struggle with this?"

STEP 2: DM them (don't pitch yet)
  "Hey, I saw your post. I had the same problem.
   What's your current process for handling inquiries?"

STEP 3: Diagnose
  "So you're copy-pasting from WhatsApp into a spreadsheet,
   checking feasibility manually, then drafting options?
   How long does that take per inquiry?"

STEP 4: Prescribe (offer trial)
  "I built something that handles the copy-paste and
   feasibility check automatically. Want to try it
   free for 14 days? No credit card."

STEP 5: Onboard
  "Let me set you up. Paste one real inquiry you got today
   and I'll show you what the tool does with it."

STEP 6: Check in
  3 days later: "How's it going? Processed any inquiries?"
  7 days later: "Any questions? Need help with anything?"
  12 days later: "Your trial ends in 2 days. Want to continue?"
```

**Total time per prospect**: ~45 min spread over 2 weeks.  
**Expected close rate**: 10-20% of people you diagnose.  
**At 5 prospects/month = 5-10 closed deals/month = 10-15 customers in 3 months.**

## 5. Content for the Side Project

You don't need a blog. You need **proof**.

| Asset | Time to Create | Where It Goes |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| 2-3 before/after screenshots | 10 min | FB groups, WhatsApp |
| 1 comparison table (Waypoint vs manual) | 30 min | On your landing page |
| 1 customer quote (from first free user) | 15 min | FB posts |
| 1 Loom demo (3 min: paste inquiry → see results) | 20 min | DMs, landing page |
| 1 FAQ doc ("what happens when the AI gets it wrong?") | 45 min | Landing page footer |

**Don't write**: Blog posts, whitepapers, LinkedIn articles.  
**Do write**: Replies to other people's posts. DMs. Quick tips.

## 6. Anti-Patterns for Side Project GTM

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | Prevent It |
|-------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Selling to everyone** | "Any agency can use this." No. Only agencies with 2+ staff and 10+ inquiries/week. | Reject prospects who are too small. They'll churn and waste your time. |
| **Running ads too early** | Google Ads at ₹50K/mo for a $20 product. You need 200+ customers to break even on that. | Zero ad spend until you have 50 paying customers. |
| **Building instead of selling** | "I'll add flight tracking first, THEN sell." No. Sell what you have. Add features based on what paying customers ask for. | Ship the current MVP. Find 5 customers. Ask them what to build next. |
| **No follow-up** | You talk to someone interested, they say "sounds cool," and you never message them again. | Follow up 3 times (day 1, day 7, day 30). Most sales happen on follow-up 2 or 3. |
| **Trying to look big** | Fancy website, branded email, polished pitch deck. None of that matters to a travel agent who just wants to save time. | Be human. "I built this because I saw the problem. Want to try it?" |
