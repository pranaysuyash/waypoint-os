# Feature: Orbital & Sub-Orbital Logistics

## POV: User & Business POV (Extreme Frontier)

### 1. Objective
To codify the operational requirements for civilian space travel, including orbital hotels, sub-orbital point-to-point transit, and zero-G hospitality management.

### 2. Functional Requirements

#### A. Launch & Window Management
- **Dynamic Launch Windows**: Integrating with launch provider APIs (e.g., SpaceX, Blue Origin, Axiom) to track launch windows, weather constraints, and "Hold" statuses.
- **Sub-Orbital Point-to-Point**: Managing the logistics of "London to Sydney in 2 hours" via sub-orbital flight, including specialized terminal arrivals and G-force health clearance.

#### B. Zero-G Hospitality Operations
- **Orbital PNR Synchronization**: Managing "Room Assignments" in orbital stations (e.g., ISS modules or commercial stations) and "Airlock Handshakes" for docking.
- **Resource Budgeting**: Monitoring the traveler's consumption of oxygen, water, and calorie rations as part of the "Inclusive" package.

#### C. Pre-Flight "Astronaut" Onboarding
- **Training Logistics**: Coordinating the 6-month pre-flight centrifuge training, medical screenings, and weightless simulations.
- **Custom Suit Fitting**: Managing the visual design and technical specs of the traveler's custom EVA or flight suit.

### 3. Core Engine Logic
- **"The Orbital Genome"**: Storing the traveler's physiological tolerance for zero-G, radiation history, and claustrophobia triggers.
- **Interplanetary Pricing**: Handling the "Payload" pricing model (cost-per-kilogram) for personal luggage in orbit.

### 4. Safety & Security
- **De-orbit & Recovery Protocol**: Real-time tracking of re-entry trajectories and coordinating "Splashdown" or "Landing Site" recovery teams.
- **Radiation Exposure Monitoring**: Automatically updating the "Traveler Medical Summary" with cumulative solar radiation doses.
