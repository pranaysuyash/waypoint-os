# Feature: Crisis Orchestration Dashboard

## POV: Business / Agency Owner

### 1. Objective
To provide a mission-control interface for managing mass-scale disruptions (natural disasters, airline strikes, geopolitical unrest), enabling rapid communication and recovery orchestration.

### 2. Functional Requirements

#### A. Fleet-Wide Visibility
- **Impact Map**: Real-time visualization of all active and upcoming travelers relative to the crisis epicenter.
- **Exposure Analytics**: Calculating the total financial and operational liability (e.g., "We have 50 clients stranded in Bali with a total of $40k in pre-paid non-refundable hotel stays").

#### B. Mass Action Tools
- **Broadcast Comms**: Sending emergency updates to all affected travelers via their preferred channel with one click.
- **Bulk Rebooking Engine**: Identifying alternative routes and pre-authorizing rebookings based on "Duty of Care" priority.
- **Supplier Liaison Hub**: Grouping all affected bookings by supplier (e.g., "All 20 Qatar Airways bookings") to facilitate group-level waiver negotiations.

#### C. Recovery & Support Tracking
- **Safety Status Ledger**: Tracking the "Safe/Not Safe" status of every traveler.
- **Volunteer/Agent Surge**: Ability to quickly assign "Temporary Crisis Agents" to the dashboard to handle the spike in volume.

### 3. Business Logic
- **Priority Tiering**: VVIPs and "Stranded" travelers are moved to the top of the queue automatically.
- **Waiver Auto-Detection**: AI monitors airline "Global Waiver" announcements and automatically applies the "Waiver Code" to the PNRs, saving agents hours of manual entry.

### 4. Safety & Governance
- **"Proof of Life" Protocol**: Mandatory check-in intervals for travelers in high-risk zones.
- **Audit of Response**: Logging every action taken during the crisis for insurance and liability review.
