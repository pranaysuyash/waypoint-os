# Feature: Anonymized Supplier Blacklist Network

## POV: Business POV (Network Effects)

### 1. Objective
To create a "Shield" for agencies by sharing anonymized intelligence on unreliable or fraudulent suppliers (hotels, DMCs, airlines), preventing systemic losses across the platform.

### 2. Functional Requirements

#### A. The "Vetted" Graph (Crowdsourced Intelligence)
- **Supplier Risk Scoring**: Aggregating feedback from thousands of agency bookings to flag suppliers with high "No-Show" rates, "Bait and Switch" room tactics, or "Delayed Commission" patterns.
- **Anonymized Conflict Reporting**: "Agency A reported that Hotel X in London has a bed-bug outbreak; all platform agencies are alerted to avoid."

#### B. Fraud & Financial Blacklist
- **Chargeback High-Risk Zones**: Identifying specific hotels or routes that have a statistically high rate of credit card chargebacks or fraudulent claims.
- **DMC Insolvency Alerts**: Real-time monitoring of "Red Flags" for local operators (e.g., "Supplier is requesting 100% cash up-front; possible liquidity crisis").

#### C. Quality Benchmarking
- **The "Real" Star Rating**: A platform-only rating system that differs from public sites (TripAdvisor), focusing on "Agency Friendliness" and "Operational Reliability."
- **Response-Time Leaderboard**: Ranking suppliers by how quickly they respond to agent requests or emergency changes.

### 3. Business Logic
- **"Network Immunity"**: If a new agency joins the platform, they immediately inherit the "Collective Defense" of all existing agencies.
- **Supplier "Sanctions"**: If a hotel consistently violates platform standards, the "Master Orchestrator" automatically deprioritizes them in all agent searches.

### 4. Safety & Privacy
- **Agency Anonymity**: Protecting the identity of the reporting agency to prevent supplier retaliation.
- **Verified Reviews Only**: Only allowing feedback on suppliers with a "Confirmed PNR/Transaction" to prevent malicious rating manipulation.
