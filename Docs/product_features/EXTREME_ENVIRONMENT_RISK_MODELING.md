# Feature: Extreme Environment Risk Modeling

## POV: User & Business POV (Safety)

### 1. Objective
To provide high-precision risk assessment and operational support for travel to the earth's most hostile environments (Antarctica, Mariana Trench, Remote Jungles).

### 2. Functional Requirements

#### A. Environmental "Survival" Logic
- **Weather Window Analysis**: AI monitors polar ice-melt, volcanic ash clouds, or deep-sea current patterns to predict "Safe Entry" and "Extraction" windows.
- **Equipment Auditing**: Cross-referencing the traveler's "Packing List" against the specific thermal or pressure requirements of the destination (e.g., "Your current boots are rated for -20C; you need -50C for the Vinson Massif").

#### B. The "Dead-Reckoning" Support
- **Satellite Connectivity Failover**: Managing the handoff between traditional GPS and Iridium satellite tracking for travelers in "Connectivity Deserts."
- **Autonomous SOS Beacon**: A feature that auto-triggers an emergency rescue if a traveler's "Live Pulse" (GPS/Biometrics) hasn't moved or checked-in for a set interval in a Red Zone.

#### C. Local "Bunker" Identification
- **Safe-Haven Mapping**: Identifying the nearest research stations, deep-sea habitats, or survival caches (food/fuel) in the destination area.

### 3. Business Logic
- **Insurance Premium Real-time Calculation**: Dynamically adjusting travel insurance costs based on the "Risk Score" of the daily activity (e.g., "Diving with sharks today increases your daily premium by $40").
- **Asset Loss Modeling**: Calculating the financial liability of losing high-value equipment (e.g., a $1M submersible) during the trip.

### 4. Safety & Governance
- **"Extraction" Bonding**: Requiring a "Guaranteed Extraction Bond" (e.g., $100k) to be held in escrow for remote polar or desert expeditions.
- **Ecological Impact Monitoring**: Ensuring the trip complies with strict environmental treaties (e.g., The Antarctic Treaty) to prevent agency fines.
