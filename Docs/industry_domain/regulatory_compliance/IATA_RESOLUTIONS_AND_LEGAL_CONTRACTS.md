# IATA Resolutions and Legal Contracts

## Overview
This document explains the key IATA resolutions, industry rules, and contractual frameworks that govern travel agency operations.

## Key IATA resolutions
- **Resolution 810**: BSP operations, including ticketing and settlement procedures.
- **Resolution 885**: Billing and settlement plan for non-IATA agents.
- **Resolution 818**: Agency reporting requirements and audit trails.
- **Resolution 850**: Accreditation and agent credit terms.

## Contractual agreements
- **GDS contracts**: Terms for accessing airline inventory, queue management, and ticketing authority.
- **Supplier contracts**: Hotel allotment, agency commissions, markups, and cancellation rules.
- **Host / IATA agreements**: For host agencies, sub-agents, and co-branded distribution.
- **Client engagement agreements**: Service-level terms, agency fee disclosures, refund policies, and liability limitations.

## Legal implications
- **Ticketing authority**: Agencies must issue tickets within the rules of the carrier, GDS, and BSP.
- **Liability**: Contracts define whether the agency or supplier is responsible for cancellations, refunds, and service failures.
- **Compliance**: Agencies must maintain records to support audits and regulatory inquiries.
- **Dispute resolution**: The contracts often specify arbitration, governing law, and how disputes are handled across jurisdictions.

## Operational application
- Treat IATA resolutions as operational guardrails, not optional best practices.
- Map contract terms into the agency's policy library and mid-office controls.
- Educate front-line agents on when a supplier contract overrides a standard itinerary rule.
- Use audit checklists to verify that carriers, GDS vendors, and suppliers are operating within contracted terms.

## Why this matters
Legal rules and industry resolutions are the bedrock of travel agency accountability. Agencies that ignore them risk financial exposure, accreditation loss, and damaged client trust.
