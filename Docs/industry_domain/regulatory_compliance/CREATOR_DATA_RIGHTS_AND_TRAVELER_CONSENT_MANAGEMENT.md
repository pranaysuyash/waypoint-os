# Creator Data Rights and Traveler Consent Management

This document explains data rights, consent, and privacy governance for creator-driven travel experiences.

## Overview

- Creator products require careful handling of traveler data, especially when creators receive performance or personalization signals.
- Consent management is critical for traveler trust and compliance across jurisdictions.
- Data rights must protect travelers while enabling legitimate creator workflows.

## Data Rights

- Traveler control: let travelers see what data creators can access and how it will be used.
- Purpose limitation: restrict creator data use to agreed experience delivery, personalization, and analytics.
- Data minimization: share only the data necessary for creator activities and avoid broad access.
- Retention policies: define how long creator-related traveler data is stored and when it is deleted.

## Consent Management

- Consent capture: obtain explicit traveler consent for creator communication, personalization, and affiliate tracking.
- Granularity: allow travelers to accept or reject different creator data uses separately.
- Audit trail: log consent events, revocations, and policy versions.
- Cross-border compliance: adapt consent flows to GDPR, CCPA, and local privacy laws.

## Platform Governance

- Creator agreements: require creators to comply with traveler data rights and platform privacy policies.
- Consent UI: make consent prompts clear, contextual, and non-disruptive.
- Monitoring: detect creator access patterns that may violate consent or data rights.
- Dispute resolution: provide travelers with a path to challenge data use or withdraw creator permissions.
