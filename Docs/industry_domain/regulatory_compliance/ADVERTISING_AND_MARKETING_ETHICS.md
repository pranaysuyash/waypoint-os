# Domain Knowledge: Advertising & Marketing Ethics

**Category**: Global Regulatory & Compliance  
**Focus**: Consumer protection, pricing transparency, and social media compliance.

---

## 1. Pricing Transparency (DOT / EU Rules)

The era of "Hidden Fees" is ending. Regulators are aggressive about "Full Price" display.

### The "Drip Pricing" Trap
- **Logic**: Showing a price of $500, but adding taxes, resort fees, and "Processing fees" only at the final checkout.
- **SOP**: All marketing materials (Emails, Ads, Quotes) must show the **"All-in" price** including all non-optional taxes and fees.

---

## 2. "Bait and Switch" Prevention

- **Logic**: Advertising a "Starting at $999" price that is only available for 1 day in 3 years.
- **SOP**: Advertised prices must have "Reasonable Availability." If a deal is sold out, it must be removed from the website/ads immediately.

---

## 3. Testimonial & Influencer Compliance (FTC)

- **Logic**: If an influencer is given a "Free Trip" to promote the agency, they *must* disclose it.
- **SOP**: All sponsored content must include clear disclosures (e.g., #Ad, #Sponsored).

---

## 4. "Urgency" & "Scarcity" Dark Patterns

- **Logic**: "Only 1 room left!" (when there are 50). This is increasingly targeted by consumer protection bureaus.
- **SOP**: Real-time availability data must back up any "Scarcity" claims.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Resort Fee" Complaint**: A client is billed $50/night for "Resort Fees" at the hotel. They claim the agency quote said "All taxes and fees included." The agent must resolve the discrepancy.
- **The "Sold Out" Ad**: An agency runs a Facebook ad for a "Summer Safari." The deal sells out in 10 minutes, but the ad keeps running for 2 days. A client sues for "Deceptive Advertising."
- **Influencer Backlash**: An influencer promotes the agency but fails to disclose they were paid. The agency faces an FTC audit.
- **"Drip" Pricing Audit**: A regulator audits the agency's mobile app and finds that "Service Fees" are only revealed on the last screen.
