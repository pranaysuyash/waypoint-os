# Creator Travel Regulatory Playbook — Japan & Korea

This playbook is written for creator travel products, agent workflows, and partner services in Japan and South Korea. It focuses on regulatory, media, and cultural compliance for creator-led travel, production, and branded experiences.

## 1. Why Japan & Korea matter
- Japan and South Korea are high-profile creator destinations with strong inbound tourism, cultural storytelling, and premium content appeal.
- Both markets have well-defined regulations for visas, commercial filming, influencer marketing, and local partner compliance.
- Creator platforms should treat these markets as premium, regulated corridors where performance depends on precise permit and cultural understanding.

## 2. Regulatory dimensions

### 2.1 Visa and entry requirements
- Japan and South Korea both distinguish between tourist visas and business/media visas for commercial content creation.
- Short-term tourist travel is common, but creators filming sponsored or branded campaigns often need the appropriate business or media entry status.
- Platforms should clearly flag whether a creator itinerary includes commercial filming, product placement, or paid collaborations.
- Transit and multi-country East Asia circuits also require attention to visa validity and re-entry rules.

### 2.2 Media production and filming permits
- Both countries require permits for commercial filming in public spaces, historic sites, and high-traffic tourist zones.
- Japan’s film permit and location approval process can involve municipal government offices and shrine/temple custodians.
- South Korea’s film commission ecosystem supports creators but still requires approvals for drones, large crews, and branded activations.
- Creator travel products should manage permit timelines and support local production partners that specialize in Japan/Korea.

### 2.3 Advertising, influencer marketing, and disclosure
- Japan and South Korea have strong advertising codes around transparency, claims, and influencer disclosure.
- Sponsored travel content must clearly disclose paid relationships and avoid misleading statements about destinations or experiences.
- Korea’s advertising standards and Japan’s Consumer Affairs Agency expectations both prioritize consumer trust and accurate representation.
- Platforms should support local disclosure workflows for creators and destination partners.

### 2.4 Cultural and local partner compliance
- Cultural sensitivities matter in both markets, especially around sacred locations, historic sites, and traditional communities.
- Local partner onboarding should verify cultural liaison experience, location access permissions, and reputation for respectful storytelling.
- Platforms should avoid creating packages that imply unauthorized access to private cultural assets or restricted heritage sites.

### 2.5 Payments, tax, and supplier settlement
- GST or consumption tax rules apply in each market, and local suppliers may require invoice compliance or registered business status.
- Cross-border payments should account for Korean won and Japanese yen settlement processes, as well as creator payout requirements.
- Escrow or staged payment arrangements help protect against supplier no-shows, permit delays, and branded campaign risk.
- Agent workflows should capture local supplier registration, insurance, and payment authorization documentation.

## 3. Product and operational implications

### 3.1 Agent workflows
- Build Japan/Korea-specific compliance checks for visa type, film permit status, location approvals, and partner credentials.
- Provide pre-trip checklists for media approvals, drone permissions, and local cultural advice.
- Offer itinerary guidance for creator circuits such as Tokyo-Kyoto, Seoul-Busan, and regional content hubs like Jeju Island and Osaka.

### 3.2 Partner onboarding
- Require local partners to upload business registration, film commission experience, and proof of approvals for brand campaigns.
- Segment partners by category: production services, hospitality, cultural experiences, and experiential event venues.
- Prioritize partners with strong reputation for handling international media and creator groups.

### 3.3 Creator-facing messaging
- Educate creators on how to safely plan Japan/Korea shoots versus standard tourist travel.
- Provide guidance on local etiquette, disclosure requirements, and destination-specific marketing restrictions.
- Offer destination advice for high-value routes: Tokyo, Kyoto, Seoul, Jeju, Yokohama, and Busan.
