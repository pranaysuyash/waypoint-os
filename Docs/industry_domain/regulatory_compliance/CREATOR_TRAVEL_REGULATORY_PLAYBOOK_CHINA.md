# Creator Travel Regulatory Playbook — China

This playbook is written for creator travel products, partner workflows, and destination packages that involve China. It is designed for creator platforms and agent-facing services that need to manage the country’s unique visa, media, payment, and content compliance risks.

## 1. Why China matters
- China is a major inbound travel market and a high-value destination for creator travel, especially for luxury, cultural, and lifestyle campaigns.
- The market is also highly regulated, so creator travel products must treat compliance as a foundational trust and operational requirement.
- Platforms supporting China need to handle strict visa categories, local censorship and advertising restrictions, payment rails, and data residency rules.

## 2. Regulatory dimensions

### 2.1 Visa and entry restrictions
- China has narrow visa categories for tourism, business, and work; creators engaged in commercial filming or event campaigns may require business visas or explicit permits.
- Visa-free and transit visa programs exist for certain nationalities, but they are limited and do not cover commercial content creation.
- Travel platforms should clearly distinguish between tourist travel and creator-led production travel and provide guidance on the correct visa category.

### 2.2 Content production and media permits
- Commercial content creation in China often requires government permits through local culture, tourism, and media authorities.
- Filming in public places, historic sites, and commercial venues may require location approvals and media shoot permits.
- Drone use is also regulated by the CAAC and often needs separate authorization.
- Even influencer marketing or live-streaming campaigns may need prior approval depending on the location and scale.

### 2.3 Advertising, censorship, and platform compliance
- Chinese advertising regulations are strict about claims, endorsements, and content that could be seen as false, nationalistic, or culturally insensitive.
- Creator platforms should avoid language or destination positioning that touches on sensitive political, religious, or social topics.
- Local partners or distributors may require a compliance review before publishing creator content.

### 2.4 Payment, settlement, and financial controls
- Domestic payment ecosystems such as WeChat Pay and Alipay dominate within China; cross-border settlement requires careful handling.
- Platforms should support RMB-based local supplier settlement and foreign exchange compliance for creator payouts.
- Anti-money laundering and foreign remittance controls may apply to high-value creator campaigns and sponsor payments.

### 2.5 Data and privacy compliance
- China’s data residency and personal information protection rules can affect how traveler/creator data is stored and shared.
- Creator platforms operating in or through China should map where data is collected, whether it is transferred outside the country, and whether local consent is required.
- Sensitive data such as biometric information or location tracking may trigger additional regulatory scrutiny.

## 3. Product and operational implications

### 3.1 Agent workflows
- Build China-specific compliance checks into itinerary creation, including visa requirements, permit needs, and local content restrictions.
- Include pre-trip documentation workflows for media production approvals, location permit filings, and supplier risk screening.
- Provide clear warnings when an itinerary includes China as part of a broader multi-country creator route.

### 3.2 Partner onboarding
- Require local partners to show business registration, tourism/media license credentials, and evidence of experience with international media.
- Surface capabilities for local payment collection, host support, and government permission management.
- Prioritize partners who can support safe, compliant creator campaigns in China’s most popular destinations.

### 3.3 Creator-facing messaging
- Educate creators on the differences between travel content, brand activations, and commercial shoot approvals in China.
- Offer guidance on respectful local behavior, cultural norms, prohibited topics, and the need for compliant approvals.
- Provide destination guides for high-demand creator routes such as Shanghai, Beijing, Chengdu, Guilin, and Hainan.
