# Sustainable Travel and ESG Compliance

## Overview
This document outlines how travel agencies integrate sustainability, environmental, social, and governance (ESG) considerations into travel programs and supplier selection.

## Key ESG dimensions for travel
- **Environmental**: carbon footprint, energy-efficient hotels, green transport options
- **Social**: traveler wellness, community impact, fair labor in supplier services
- **Governance**: transparency in fees, responsible data handling, regulatory compliance

## Sustainable travel practices
- Offer carbon-offset options and low-emission transport choices
- Prioritize suppliers with sustainability certifications and green policies
- Design itineraries that reduce unnecessary air segments and optimize ground logistics
- Communicate sustainability trade-offs clearly to clients

## Compliance and reporting
- Track carbon emissions at the trip level when possible
- Maintain supplier sustainability profiles and evidence of certifications
- Report ESG performance to clients, partners, and internal stakeholders
- Align with local and international standards where applicable (e.g., GDS carbon tagging, UNWTO guidelines)

## Operational implementation
- Include sustainability filters in proposal and supplier selection tools
- Train agents to discuss ESG options without compromising traveler experience
- Use sustainability as a differentiator for premium and corporate clients
- Monitor bookings for adherence to sustainable program rules

## Why this matters
Sustainability is increasingly part of corporate travel policy and traveler expectations. Agencies that can operationalize ESG consistently build trust with clients and reduce long-term risk.
