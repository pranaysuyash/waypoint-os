# Domain Knowledge: Consumer Rights & Airline Compensation

**Category**: Global Regulatory & Compliance  
**Focus**: EU261, US DOT "Refund Mandates," and Passenger rights.

---

## 1. EU261/2004 Mastery

This is the most powerful passenger right law in the world (applies to all flights departing EU/UK or arriving in EU/UK on an EU/UK carrier).

### Compensation Tiers
- **Short-haul (<1500km)**: €250.
- **Medium-haul (1500-3500km)**: €400.
- **Long-haul (>3500km)**: €600.
- **Trigger**: Delay >3 hours at final destination (if not due to "Extraordinary Circumstances").

---

## 2. The "Extraordinary Circumstances" Fight

- **Logic**: Airlines will claim "Weather" or "ATC" even if it was a "Mechanical Failure" (which is compensable).
- **SOP**: The agent must check **METAR data** and **FlightRadar24** to verify if other flights on the same route were flying. If they were, the agent must challenge the "Extraordinary" claim.

---

## 3. US DOT "Automatic Refund" Mandate (2024/2025)

- **Logic**: New US rules require airlines to refund the full ticket price (not just a voucher) if a flight is significantly delayed (>3 hours domestic, >6 hours international) or cancelled.
- **SOP**: The agent must proactively file for the "Automatic Refund" if the traveler chooses not to fly.

---

## 4. "Right to Care" SOP

- **Logic**: During a delay, the airline *must* provide meals, communication, and overnight accommodation (if needed), regardless of the *cause* of the delay.
- **SOP**: If the airline refuses, the agent must advise the client to "Pay and Reclaim" and keep all receipts.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Weather" Lie**: An airline denies compensation for a 5-hour delay, citing "Bad Weather." The agent proves that every other airline was flying and forces the €600 payment.
- **Denied Boarding (Bumping)**: A client is bumped from an overbooked flight. The agent must ensure the client receives "Instant Cash Compensation" at the airport before accepting a new flight.
- **The "Connecting Flight" Trap**: A passenger misses a connection in Frankfurt due to a 45-minute delay on the first leg. The total delay at destination is 10 hours. The agent must calculate the compensation based on the *total* delay.
- **Missed "Duty of Care"**: A traveler is stranded overnight in Chicago; the airline provides no hotel. The agent books a luxury hotel for the client and files a "Right to Care" reimbursement claim.
