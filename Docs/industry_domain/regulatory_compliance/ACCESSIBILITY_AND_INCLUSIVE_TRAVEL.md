# Domain Knowledge: Accessibility & Inclusive Travel

**Category**: Global Regulatory & Compliance  
**Focus**: Disability rights, mobility logistics, and inclusive service design.

---

## 1. The "Special Service Request" (SSR)

In the GDS, accessibility needs are managed via **SSR Codes**.

### Key SSR Codes
- **WCHR**: Wheelchair (can climb stairs).
- **WCHS**: Wheelchair (cannot climb stairs, can walk to seat).
- **WCHC**: Wheelchair (completely immobile, needs "aisle chair").
- **BLND/DEAF**: Blind/Deaf passengers.
- **DPNA**: Disabled Passenger Needing Assistance (Intellectual/Developmental).

---

## 2. Mobility Equipment SOP

### The "Battery" Trap
- **Logic**: Electric wheelchairs use Lithium or Wet-cell batteries, which are "Dangerous Goods."
- **SOP**: The agent must obtain the **Battery Type, Watt-hours, and Weight** of the chair at the time of booking and get "Airline Approval" 48 hours before departure.

---

## 3. Accessible Housing (Beyond the "Ramp")

### The "Room Audit"
- **Logic**: A hotel saying "Accessible" might just mean "Elevator."
- **SOP**: Verify specific details:
    - **Roll-in Shower**: No lip/step.
    - **Grab Bars**: Positioned correctly.
    - **Bed Height**: Low enough for a transfer from a chair.
    - **Visual/Tactile Alerts**: For deaf/blind guests.

---

## 4. Neurodiversity & "Hidden" Disabilities

### Sensory-Friendly Travel
- **Logic**: For travelers with Autism, airports can be overwhelming.
- **SOP**: Request the **"Sunflower Lanyard"** program or "Quiet Room" access at major hubs.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Broken Chair"**: An airline damages a $30,000 custom electric wheelchair during transit. The agent must source a "Rental Loaner" and manage the insurance claim.
- **Battery Denied**: A passenger is denied boarding because their wheelchair battery wasn't "Declared" properly.
- **Hotel "Bait & Switch"**: A guest arrives at an "Accessible" room to find it has a bathtub (impossible for them). The agent must find a nearby hotel with a "Roll-in" shower.
- **Assistance Failure**: An elderly passenger is left at the gate because the "Wheelchair Pusher" didn't arrive.
