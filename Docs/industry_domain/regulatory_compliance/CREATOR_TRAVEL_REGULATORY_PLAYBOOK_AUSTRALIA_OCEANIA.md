# Creator Travel Regulatory Playbook — Australia & Oceania

This playbook is written for creator travel products, agent workflows, and partner experiences in Australia and Oceania, including Australia, New Zealand, Fiji, and Pacific Island destinations.

## 1. Why Australia & Oceania matters
- Australia and Oceania are premium creator travel destinations with strong storytelling appeal, natural scenery, adventure tourism, and high-value event production.
- The region has clear regulatory frameworks, which makes it a good fit for creator products once compliance is understood.
- Creator platforms need to manage visa categories, media production permits, insurance, environmental approvals, and Indigenous partner protocols.

## 2. Regulatory dimensions

### 2.1 Visa and entry requirements
- Australia and New Zealand have strict visa regimes; creators engaged in commercial filming or paid collaborations may require business, short-term work, or media visas.
- Tourist visas are not sufficient for commercial content creation, influencer campaigns, or crew-supported shoots.
- Pacific Island destinations may offer visa-free or visa-on-arrival access, but commercial filming and promotional activities often still require local permits.
- Creator itinerary builders should clearly flag whether a booking is leisure travel, promotional content creation, or production-related.

### 2.2 Media production and filming permits
- Film and media permits are required for commercial shoots in public places, national parks, and tourist attractions.
- Australia has state-based screen agencies, while New Zealand uses the New Zealand Film Commission for approvals and location support.
- Pacific Island nations typically require cultural clearances and community consent for filming in villages, marine areas, and heritage sites.
- Drone operations are regulated by local civil aviation authorities and often need special authorizations.

### 2.3 Environmental and Indigenous compliance
- Protected areas, marine parks, and Indigenous lands require special permits, environmental approvals, and cultural liaison.
- Australia’s Great Barrier Reef, national parks, and sacred Indigenous sites have strict access and filming rules.
- New Zealand also requires Maori partnership and cultural sensitivity for many locations.
- Creator travel products should surface sustainability and community impact requirements for these destinations.

### 2.4 Advertising, sponsorship, and local marketing
- Advertising and sponsorship disclosure rules apply in Australia and New Zealand, especially for paid influencer content and branded tourism campaigns.
- Platforms should support clear disclosure for sponsored stays, gifted experiences, and affiliate promotions.
- Local marketing must avoid misleading claims about environmental impact, Indigenous culture, or event sponsorship.

### 2.5 Payments, tax, and supplier compliance
- GST applies in Australia and New Zealand to travel services and digital agency fees.
- Cross-border payouts to creators and local suppliers should account for FX, withholding tax, and local supplier registration.
- Supplier onboarding should verify tourism business registration, insurance, and production support capabilities.
- Escrow or staged payments help protect high-value creator campaigns and ensure supplier performance.

## 3. Product and operational implications

### 3.1 Agent workflows
- Create Australia & Oceania-specific compliance checks for visa type, production permit status, environmental approvals, and supplier credentials.
- Provide pre-trip checklists for film permit applications, drone authorizations, Indigenous liaison, and insurance coverage.
- Offer itinerary guidance for common creator circuits like Sydney/Melbourne, New Zealand adventure routes, and Pacific Island experiential travel.

### 3.2 Partner onboarding
- Require local partners to upload business licenses, screen agency approvals, tourism operator credentials, and evidence of international production support.
- Segment partners into categories: experiential hospitality, event venues, local production services, Indigenous cultural hosts, and eco-tourism providers.
- Prioritize partners with strong sustainability credentials and experience working with international creators.

### 3.3 Creator-facing messaging
- Educate creators on the difference between tourist travel and commercial content production in Australia and New Zealand.
- Provide guidance on local customs, environmental best practices, and how to work respectfully with Indigenous partners.
- Offer destination advice for core creator hubs: Sydney, Auckland, Queenstown, Fiji, Tahiti, and other Pacific Islands.
