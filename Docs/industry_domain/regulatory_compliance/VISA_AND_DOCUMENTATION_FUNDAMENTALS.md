# Visa and Documentation Fundamentals

Managing travel documentation is a high-risk, low-margin activity that can lead to catastrophic trip failure if mishandled.

---

## 1. Core Documents

### Passports
- **Validity Rule**: Most countries require at least **6 months validity** from the date of return.
- **Blank Pages**: Many countries require at least **2 blank pages**.
- **Condition**: Damaged passports (torn pages, water damage) are often rejected at immigration.

### Visas (The "Hard Blocker")
- **Types**: E-Visa, Visa on Arrival (VoA), Sticker Visa (Console), Transit Visa.
- **Processing Times**: Range from 2 hours (E-Visa) to 30+ days (Sticker Visa).
- **Risk**: Last-minute policy changes (e.g., reciprocity rules, biosecurity bans).

### Identity & Financial Docs
- **PAN Card**: Required for international remittances in certain jurisdictions (e.g., India LRS).
- **Aadhar / National ID**: Required for domestic flights and hotel check-ins.
- **ITR (Income Tax Returns)**: Often required for sticker visa applications (Schengen, USA, UK).

---

## 2. Transit Risks

One of the most common "blind spots" for junior agents.
- **TWOV (Transit Without Visa)**: Rules vary by airport, airline, and layover duration.
- **Terminal Changes**: In airports like Heathrow or Dubai, changing terminals may require clearing immigration (and thus a visa).
- **Self-Transfer**: Booking two separate tickets (e.g., Indigo to Dubai, then Emirates to London) almost always requires a visa to collect bags and re-check.

---

## 3. Insurance Requirements

- **Mandatory**: Some countries (e.g., Schengen area, Cuba) require proof of travel insurance with specific coverage limits.
- **Pre-existing Conditions**: Critical for senior travelers (S2 persona parents).
- **Adventure Sports**: Standard insurance often excludes activities like skydiving or scuba diving.
