# Creator Contracts, Terms, and Disclosures

This document explains the contractual and disclosure requirements for creator partnerships on travel platforms.

## Overview

- Creator partnerships require clear agreements covering liabilities, payment terms, content rights, and traveler protections.
- Disclosures are critical when creator products are sold through the platform, especially for sponsored or affiliate arrangements.
- Strong contract governance reduces risk and supports regulatory compliance.

## Contract Elements

- Scope of services: define what creators and partners deliver, including content, events, and traveler support.
- Compensation: spell out commission structures, payout timing, and revenue share details.
- Intellectual property: clarify ownership, licensing, and usage rights for creator content and branded assets.
- Liability: assign responsibility for cancellations, traveler incidents, and product quality.

## Disclosure Requirements

- Promotional transparency: disclose when creator content is sponsored, affiliate-linked, or platform-promoted.
- Financial relationships: surface creator compensation models where required by advertising and consumer laws.
- Terms visibility: make partnership and product terms accessible to travelers and creators.
- Compliance notices: include disclosures for regulated travel products like package tours, insurance, and health-related experiences.

## Governance Practices

- Standardized templates: use contract templates to accelerate creator onboarding and reduce negotiation risk.
- Review processes: establish legal and compliance reviews for high-risk creator agreements.
- Renewal controls: refresh terms regularly as creator programs evolve.
- Audit readiness: keep records of creator agreements, disclosures, and approval sign-offs.
