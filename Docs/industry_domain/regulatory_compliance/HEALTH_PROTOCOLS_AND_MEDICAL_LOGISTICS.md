# Health Protocols and Medical Logistics

## Overview
This document covers health-related travel requirements, medical logistics, and the agency's role in keeping travelers safe.

## Common health-related requirements
- Vaccination certificates and health declarations
- Pre-travel testing requirements (PCR, antigen)
- Fit-to-fly or medical clearance letters
- Destination-specific health advisories and entry restrictions

## Medical logistics for travel
- Arrange medical escorts for vulnerable travelers or those with special needs
- Coordinate compassionate or medical repatriation when required
- Book accommodations near medical facilities for travelers with chronic conditions
- Provide access to telemedicine or local medical assistance partners

## Agency responsibilities
- Keep requirements current for each destination and traveler profile
- Collect and verify documentation before ticketing and departure
- Advise travelers on local healthcare access and emergency contacts
- Escalate medical or health risks to duty-of-care teams immediately

## Special scenarios
- Evacuation from a destination with limited medical infrastructure
- Travel for patients undergoing treatment, requiring medication continuity
- Travelling with medical equipment, oxygen, or mobility aids
- Traveling during disease outbreaks or special quarantine regimes

## Why this matters
Health requirements can be a trip stopper. Agencies that manage medical logistics proactively reduce risk, build trust, and ensure travelers arrive prepared.
