# Crisis, Contingency, and Disruption Response

## Overview
Travel agencies must have documented processes for handling crises, service disruptions, and emergency travel changes.

## Types of disruptions
- Flight cancellations and re-accommodations
- Natural disasters, strikes, and weather events
- Health or security incidents affecting travelers
- Visa denials and border-entry changes
- Supplier insolvency or sudden contract termination

## Response framework
- Detect: identify the disruption as early as possible
- Assess: determine impacted travelers and severity
- Communicate: inform customers and internal teams clearly
- Act: rebook, reroute, or arrange support as needed
- Review: capture lessons learned and update playbooks

## Critical response elements
- Emergency contact details for travelers and suppliers
- Predefined rerouting and continuity options
- Access to local support partners and ground vendors
- Clear authority matrix for approvals and spend limits
- Communication templates for different disruption types

## Prevention and preparedness
- Maintain backup supplier options in each major region
- Track regulatory and travel advisories for key destinations
- Ensure traveler documentation and insurance coverage is current
- Include disruption resilience in supplier selection and contract terms

## Why this matters
A travel agency's ability to recover quickly from disruptions is a core part of its value proposition, especially for high-touch and corporate customers.
