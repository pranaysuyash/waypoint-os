# Creator Health, Safety Inspections, and Destination Compliance

This document explains health, safety, and destination compliance for creator-led experiences.

## Overview

- Creator travel products must meet health and safety standards across activities, accommodations, and local operations.
- Inspection regimes and destination compliance checks protect travelers and support brand trust.
- Creator programs should make these standards visible in product design and traveler communication.

## Health and Safety Inspections

- Certification: require creators and suppliers to submit evidence of health and safety inspections.
- Audit process: schedule regular checks for venues, guides, and experience operators.
- Incident prevention: monitor health compliance around food safety, activity risk, and emergency readiness.
- Documentation: record inspection results, correction plans, and approval certificates.

## Destination Compliance

- Local regulation: ensure creator experiences follow destination rules for tours, events, and hospitality.
- Permits: verify that all necessary permits are secured before creator events launch.
- Community impact: assess whether creator activities conform to destination preservation and cultural expectations.
- Traveler protection: disclose destination-specific risks and compliance status in product materials.

## Product and Operations

- Safety labeling: surface compliance badges, inspection scores, and permit status in creator product pages.
- Response planning: build protocols for addressing health or compliance failures in creator experiences.
- Supplier coordination: work with local operators to maintain destination compliance documentation.
- Continuous improvement: use inspection data to upgrade creator program standards over time.
