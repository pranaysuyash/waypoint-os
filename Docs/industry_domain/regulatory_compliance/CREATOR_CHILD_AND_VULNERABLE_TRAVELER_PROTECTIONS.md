# Creator Child and Vulnerable Traveler Protections

This document describes protections needed when creator content involves children or vulnerable traveler populations.

## Overview
- Creator content may involve family travel, youth programs, or experiences for vulnerable groups.
- Travel apps must apply additional safeguards for these categories to ensure safety and legal compliance.
- Responsible platforms protect travelers, creators, and partner suppliers.

## Protection Principles
- Special care: treat children, seniors, disabled travelers, and other vulnerable populations as protected segments.
- Accurate representation: avoid marketing experiences that overstate suitability or understate necessary accommodations.
- Privacy: safeguard personal data and consent when recording or sharing creator content involving minors.
- Accessibility: ensure creator-led experiences disclose accessibility features and support services.

## Compliance Controls
- Eligibility checks: require proof of age, guardianship, or medical support when needed.
- Disclosure requirements: clearly label experiences intended for or unsuitable for vulnerable groups.
- Supplier vetting: verify that partners can safely serve specialized traveler needs.
- Emergency support: provide direct assistance channels for vulnerable travelers during creator trips.

## App Design Implications
- Create traveler profiles that capture safety, accessibility, and support preferences.
- Use trust signals to surface creators experienced in responsible family and accessible travel.
- Prevent inappropriate targeting of vulnerable segments with generalized creator content.
- Offer educational content to help travelers understand what to expect from creator-led experiences.
