# Creator Sustainability Certification and Destination Disclosure

This document explains regulatory and compliance concerns for creator travel experiences tied to sustainability claims and destination disclosures.

## Overview

- Creators often promote sustainable travel, but claims may trigger regulatory scrutiny if not backed by certification.
- Platforms should govern wellness and sustainability claims for creator products to prevent greenwashing.
- Disclosure requirements may also apply to local impact, destination risk, and cultural sensitivity.

## Certification & Claims

- Sustainable credentials: verify certifications for eco-lodges, carbon offsets, and regenerative tourism claims.
- Creator claims: require creators to disclose whether an experience is certified, carbon neutral, or community-led.
- Third-party validation: use recognized sustainability standards and auditors for claims on the platform.
- Labeling: separate certified sustainable creator products from aspirational or non-certified offerings.

## Destination Disclosure

- Local risk disclosures: inform travelers about destination-specific safety, health, and cultural guidelines.
- Impact transparency: explain how creator experiences affect local communities, resources, and conservation.
- Permitted use disclosure: clarify whether activities are authorized, restricted, or permitted within a destination.
- Traveler rights: provide clear cancellation, refund, and grievance procedures for creator-backed sustainable travel.

## Platform Governance

- Maintain a registry of certified creator suppliers and sustainable partner programs.
- Monitor creator content for unsupported sustainability claims.
- Build workflows for destination risk review and stakeholder approvals.
- Link disclosures to booking flows so travelers can make informed decisions before checkout.
