# Passwordless Travel Identity and Credentials

This document explores passwordless identity systems and credential management for travel.

## Overview
- Passwordless identity reduces friction for traveler access and authentication.
- Credentials can include mobile IDs, biometric passes, and tokenized access rights.
- Secure, decentralized credential systems improve travel flow and data protection.

## Technology Architecture
- Passwordless authentication using device-bound keys and biometric verification.
- Credential issuance and lifecycle management for travel documents.
- Integration with border control, loyalty, and supplier systems.

## Operational Implications
- Traveler enrollment, consent management, and recovery processes.
- Interoperability between identity domains, carriers, and governments.
- Incident response for lost credentials and identity fraud.

## Commercial Impact
- Smoother check-in, boarding, and access to experiences.
- Reduced support costs and faster identity validation.
- New value propositions for premium and secure travel services.

## Future Trends
- Digital travel credentials as universal access keys.
- Cross-border passwordless identity federation.
- Traveler-controlled identity data with selective disclosure.
