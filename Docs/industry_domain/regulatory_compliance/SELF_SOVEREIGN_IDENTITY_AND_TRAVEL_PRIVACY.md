# Self-Sovereign Identity and Travel Privacy

This document explores the privacy, compliance, and travel operations implications of self-sovereign identity for travelers.

## Overview
- The concept of self-sovereign identity (SSI) in travel.
- Traveler control, consent, and decentralized identity wallets.
- Impacts on border crossing, ticketing, and passenger data exchange.

## Regulatory Landscape
- Privacy-by-design principles for SSI.
- Data minimization, portability, and consent frameworks.
- Interaction with existing national ID, passport, and biometric regimes.

## Operational Implications
- Enrollment and identity verification workflows.
- Traveller data sharing between airlines, hotels, and authorities.
- Revocation, proof presentation, and credential validation.

## Trust and Governance
- Standards bodies, identity trust networks, and issuers.
- Interoperability challenges and cross-border acceptance.
- Consumer trust, transparency, and fraud prevention.

## Future Trends
- SSI-enabled seamless border experiences.
- Privacy-preserving travel tokens and travel credentials.
- Business model evolution for identity-as-a-service in travel.
