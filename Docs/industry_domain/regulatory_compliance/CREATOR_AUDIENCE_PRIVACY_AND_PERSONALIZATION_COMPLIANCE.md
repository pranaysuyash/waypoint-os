# Creator Audience Privacy and Personalization Compliance

Creator-centric travel experiences depend on audience data, but privacy and personalization must be balanced. This document defines the compliance guardrails for creator audience targeting, personalization, and profile-based travel recommendations.

## 1. Privacy boundaries

- Data minimization: collect only what is needed for personalization, with explicit consent.
- Purpose limitation: separate audience targeting data from content licensing, commerce, and analytics.
- Anonymization and pseudonymization: protect traveler identity while enabling creator-driven recommendations.

## 2. Personalization controls

- Consent-first personalization: opt-in experiences, preference hubs, and creative content personalization toggles.
- Transparency: explain how audience signals are used to personalize creator experiences and booking recommendations.
- Portability: allow travelers to move their preference data between apps, creator communities, and loyalty programs.

## 3. Regulatory alignment

- Cross-jurisdictional rules: GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, LGPD, and emerging AI personalization regulations.
- Behavioral targeting disclosure: clear labeling of creator-promoted recommendations and sponsored personalization.
- Automated decision safeguards: human review of high-impact personalization, appeal pathways, and fairness checks.

## 4. Governance and audit

- Preference management: central traveler preference hubs, creator consent logs, and content signal opt-outs.
- Data stewardship: audience data access controls, audit trails, and vendor assessments.
- Policy enforcement: in-app privacy prompts, creator education, and regulatory reporting for personalization practices.
