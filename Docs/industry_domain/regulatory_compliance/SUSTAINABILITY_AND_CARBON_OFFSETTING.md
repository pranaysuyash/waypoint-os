# Domain Knowledge: Sustainability & Carbon Offsetting

**Category**: Global Regulatory & Compliance  
**Focus**: ESG reporting, carbon footprints, and "Green" certifications.

---

## 1. The "Green Mandate" (ESG)

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) criteria are now a "Must-have" for corporate travel.

### Corporate "Carbon Budgets"
- **Logic**: Companies have a "CO2 Budget" alongside their "Financial Budget."
- **SOP**: The agency must provide a **"Carbon Audit"** in the Phase 2 itinerary, showing the CO2 impact of each flight/hotel choice.

---

## 2. Carbon Calculation Standards

### ICAO vs. Google Travel vs. Travalyst
- **Logic**: Different models use different math (e.g., radiative forcing).
- **SOP**: Standardize on the **IATA CO2 Connect** or **Travalyst** framework for consistency.

---

## 3. Offsetting vs. Insetting

### Offsetting
- **Logic**: Buying "Carbon Credits" (e.g., planting trees).
- **Risk**: "Greenwashing" — many offsets are low-quality or non-permanent.

### SAF (Sustainable Aviation Fuel) "Insetting"
- **Logic**: Buying SAF for the airline. This is the "Gold Standard" as it reduces emissions *within* the industry.
- **SOP**: Offer travelers the option to "Upgrade to SAF" during the booking flow.

---

## 4. "Green" Hotel Certifications

### The "Label" Maze
- **Examples**: LEED, Green Key, EarthCheck, GSTC (Global Sustainable Tourism Council).
- **SOP**: Prioritize **GSTC-Accredited** hotels for travelers with "Eco-conscious" profiles.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "High-Emission" Alert**: A corporate traveler's itinerary exceeds their "Carbon Budget." The agent must suggest a "Rail alternative" or a "Non-stop flight" (which is more efficient than a layover).
- **Greenwashing Accusation**: A client complains that the "Eco-lodge" booked by the agent uses plastic bottles and has no recycling. The agent must verify the "Green Claim."
- **SAF Billing Error**: A client pays for SAF "Insetting," but the airline doesn't provide the "Certificate of Usage."
