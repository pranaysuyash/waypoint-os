# Creator Refund Policy and Consumer Protection

This document explains refund policy design and consumer protection for creator-led travel products.

## Overview

- Creator-backed travel experiences can create complexity for refunds, cancellations, and service disruptions.
- Platforms must protect consumers while preserving creator incentives and supplier agreements.
- Clear policies reduce disputes and support trust in creator commerce.

## Policy Design

- Product taxonomy: define refunds for physical travel, events, digital content, and hybrid creator experiences.
- Cancellation rules: set transparent deadlines, penalty tiers, and substitution options.
- Force majeure: distinguish normal creator changes from destination closures, regulatory orders, and emergencies.
- Refund triggers: specify conditions that automatically qualify for traveler refunds or credits.

## Consumer Protection

- Clear disclosure: surface refund rights at checkout and in creator product descriptions.
- Escalation pathways: provide travelers with a fast resolution process for creator-related issues.
- Holdbacks and insurance: use insurance products or escrow mechanisms to protect traveler payments.
- Compliance: align policies with local consumer protection laws and package travel regulations.

## Platform Practices

- Standardize refund workflows across creator and non-creator products.
- Track refund drivers to identify recurring creator supplier or event issues.
- Communicate proactively when creator experiences change or are rebooked.
- Provide travel credits or loyalty compensation when refunds are delayed.
