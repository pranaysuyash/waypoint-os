# Creator Visa and Local Labor Compliance

This document investigates the intersection of creator travel, work authorization, and local labor compliance.

## 1. Visa vs work permit

- Travel visa: permission to enter a destination for tourism, hospitality, or non-work purposes.
- Work permit: permission to engage in income-generating activity, paid appearances, or production work.
- Creator-specific edge cases: paid live events, sponsored content creation, brand appearances, and affiliate travel facilitation.

## 2. Content production as cross-border work

- Appearance rights: when a creator is contracted to appear, host, or promote an event.
- Production permits: local film, photography, and event production permissions needed for creator projects.
- Customs and equipment: import/export of camera gear, promotional materials, merchandise, and studio equipment.

## 3. Contract and compliance models

- Supplier-obligated compliance: local destination partners may be required to secure permits and labor clearances.
- Creator contracting: explicit clauses for travel purpose, deliverables, payment terms, and compliance responsibility.
- Insurance and indemnity: coverage for creator performance, local labor disputes, and permit violations.

## 4. Operational signals

- Work intent capture: classifying a trip as tourism vs creator production within booking systems.
- Border documentation: evidence packs for immigration and customs to justify creator itineraries.
- Local partner coordination: aligning creator schedules with permit windows, event licenses, and local regulations.

## 5. Research questions

- How can creator platforms automatically detect when a creator trip requires work authorization versus a standard travel visa?
- What destination types have the highest friction for creator production and appearance compliance?
- Which compliance workflows can be automated without creating new border risks?
- How should platforms reconcile creator intellectual property rights with local production and labor rules?
