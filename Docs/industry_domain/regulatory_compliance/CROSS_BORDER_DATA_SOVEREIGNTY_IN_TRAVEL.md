# Cross-Border Data Sovereignty in Travel

This document examines how travel systems manage personal data across jurisdictions while respecting data sovereignty and local residency requirements.

## Overview
- Travel data flows that cross borders: PNRs, biometrics, health credentials, and payment records.
- Data sovereignty as a core compliance challenge for global travel businesses.
- Distinct regulatory expectations for different countries and regions.

## Legal Frameworks
- GDPR, APPI, PDPA, LGPD, and emerging localization mandates.
- Travel-specific guidance for PNR sharing and border management.
- Risk of enforcement actions when traveler data is processed outside permitted territories.

## Operational Design
- Data segmentation, regional storage, and privacy-by-design architectures.
- Edge computing and in-country processing for sensitive travel workflows.
- Data flow mapping across reservations, CRM, seat maps, and passenger support.

## Supplier & Partner Management
- Contractual safeguards for third-party systems handling travel data.
- Audit-ready provenance for cross-border transfers and consent mechanisms.
- Balancing interoperability with sovereign controls.

## Future Trends
- Distributed travel data platforms that respect national residency rules.
- Privacy-preserving analytics for global travel intelligence.
- Convergence of travel data meshes and national data sovereignty frameworks.
