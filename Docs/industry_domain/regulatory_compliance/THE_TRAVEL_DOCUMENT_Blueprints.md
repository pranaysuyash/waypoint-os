# Travel Document Blueprints

## Overview
This document describes the critical documents and artifacts that agencies must manage across the travel lifecycle.

## Core document types
- **PNR / Booking Record**: The central travel record containing passenger names, segments, ticketing data, and service requests.
- **Ticket / E-Ticket Receipt**: Proof of paid travel and the fare details, including fare rules.
- **Voucher / Confirmation**: Supplier-confirmed accommodation, transfers, tours, and ancillary services.
- **Invoice / Receipt**: The client-facing financial record showing costs, fees, taxes, and service charges.

## Document roles
- **Operational control**: The PNR is the agency's single source of truth for booking and disruption management.
- **Financial reconciliation**: Invoices and receipts are used to reconcile client collections and supplier payments.
- **Compliance**: Visas, passports, health documents, and insurance policies are required for regulatory entry and duty of care.
- **Customer experience**: Confirmations and mobile vouchers keep travelers informed and reduce anxiety.

## Document lifecycle
1. **Creation**: Generate the booking record when the itinerary is agreed.
2. **Verification**: Validate passport, visa, and health documents before ticketing.
3. **Fulfillment**: Issue tickets and confirm supplier services.
4. **Monitoring**: Update documents when schedule changes, cancellations, or upgrades occur.
5. **Completion**: Archive the trip file and retain records according to policy.

## Best practices
- Keep a canonical digital file for each trip with all associated documents.
- Use version control or audit logs for any changes to PNRs and financial records.
- Align document naming and metadata across systems to avoid duplication and data loss.
- Provide travelers with a single accessible itinerary package that includes all relevant confirmations and instructions.

## Why this matters
Travel document management is the scaffolding beneath every successful trip. When it works, the traveler feels supported; when it fails, operational risk and customer complaints multiply.
