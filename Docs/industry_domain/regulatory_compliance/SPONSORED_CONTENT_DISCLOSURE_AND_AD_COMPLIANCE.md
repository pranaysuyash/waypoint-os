# Sponsored Content Disclosure and Ad Compliance

This document covers regulatory and platform requirements for sponsored creator content in travel products.

## Overview
- Sponsored creator content is subject to advertising laws, platform policies, and travel-specific disclosure requirements.
- Travel apps must ensure that creator content is clearly labeled, accurate, and not misleading.
- Compliance is critical for preserving trust and avoiding fines, reputation damage, or platform enforcement.

## Key Requirements
- Disclosure labels: clearly mark sponsored content, affiliate links, and paid partnerships.
- Truth-in-advertising: creator claims about price, availability, safety, and sustainability must be substantiated.
- Platform compliance: follow rules from app stores, advertising networks, social platforms, and payment channels.
- Travel-specific rules: comply with package travel regulations, health disclaimers, and local licensing requirements.

## Product Controls
- Native labels: include ``Sponsored``, ``Partnered``, or ``Affiliate`` tags on every creator-led product card.
- Content reviews: validate that creator posts do not misstate cancellation terms, health requirements, or inclusions.
- Transparency copy: explain how the creator is compensated and whether the product carries extra fees.
- Opt-out flows: let travelers filter or ignore sponsored creator content if they prefer purely editorial recommendations.

## Enforcement Mechanisms
- Automated checks: scan creator content for disclosure violations, exaggerated claims, or prohibited terms.
- Human review: legal and compliance teams should review branded content before launch, especially high-value offers.
- Remediation process: remove or relabel content immediately when deficiencies are found.
- Feedback loops: track complaints, agency escalations, and regulatory notices tied to creator campaigns.

## App Benefits
- Higher traveler confidence: clear disclosure builds trust in content and booking interactions.
- Lower risk: robust compliance reduces exposure to advertising enforcement actions.
- Better creator partnerships: creators prefer platforms with reliable disclosure processes and fair policies.
- Stronger brand positioning: the app can stand out as a responsible curator of creator-driven travel opportunities.
