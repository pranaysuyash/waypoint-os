# Creator Advertising Retargeting Notice and Consumer Choice

Creator-driven travel ads require clear notice and consumer control over retargeting, personalization, and content-driven commerce. This document defines the regulatory and ethical framework for creator advertising retargeting and consumer choice.

## 1. Notice requirements

- Disclosure: clearly label creator-sponsored retargeting, affiliate promotions, and personalized travel offers.
- Signal transparency: tell users when ads are based on their travel intent, creator interactions, or purchase history.
- Opt-out information: provide easy access to choose-out of creator-driven retargeting.

## 2. Consumer choice architecture

- Preference controls: allow travelers to limit creator personalization, ad categories, and behavioral retargeting.
- Granular opt-outs: separate creator ads from general travel personalization and third-party tracking.
- Data minimization: only use signals needed for the chosen experience and avoid long-term profiling where possible.

## 3. Regulatory alignment

- Digital advertising law: comply with GDPR ePrivacy, CCPA/CPRA, and local ad transparency rules.
- Creator-specific obligations: ensure creators understand retargeting rules in their sponsorship agreements.
- Automated decision safeguards: allow users to appeal or pause automated creator-powered targeting.

## 4. Governance

- Internal policy: define acceptable creator retargeting flows, prohibited practices, and audit checks.
- Review process: subject creator ad products to legal review and privacy impact assessments.
- Reporting: monitor opt-out rates, complaint volumes, and policy exceptions.
