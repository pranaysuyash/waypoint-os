# Creator Travel Regulatory Playbook — India

This playbook is tailored to creator travel products and partner workflows operating in India, including inbound creator experiences, creator-led event production, and agent-facing travel services for creators or creator-sponsored travel.

## 1. Market context
- India is a major destination for creator travel because of its large local creator economy, strong inbound tourism growth, and growing demand for experiential production services.
- Regulatory complexity comes from overlapping central, state, and local rules for tourism, content production, influencer marketing, and payment compliance.
- Creator travel products need to serve both inbound foreign creator groups and domestic Indian creator communities.

## 2. Key regulatory dimensions

### 2.1 Visa, immigration, and production permits
- Foreign creators typically travel on e-Tourist visas, tourist visas, or business visas depending on whether they are shooting content or conducting paid activities.
- Creator shoots, paid collaborations, or production activities may require a specific film shooting permit, particularly for commercial content, drone use, or work in protected areas.
- Local film facilitation offices (FFOs) and state tourism ministries are common permit sources; Mumbai, Goa, Rajasthan, and Kerala each have their own approval workflows.
- For creators arriving with equipment, customs duty rules and temporary importation estimates must be managed carefully.

### 2.2 Local labor and creator pay compliance
- Creator travel services may intersect with local labor and contractor classification if sponsors hire local crew, guides, interpreters, or production support.
- Indian labor rules and GST obligations apply to service providers, influencers, and event vendors if the creator travel product is a paid campaign.
- Platforms should support documentation capture for local contracts, invoices, and GST registration when local suppliers are engaged.

### 2.3 Consumer protection and refund compliance
- India has strong consumer protection statutes for unfair trade practices, and travel services are increasingly subject to these rules.
- Transparent refund, cancellation, and quality disclosure policies are necessary for packages sold to consumers or creators.
- For creator travel packages, clarify the role of the platform vs the agent vs the supplier, because liability may shift across the itinerary.

### 2.4 Advertising, disclosure, and influencer compliance
- India’s advertising code and advertising standards require disclosure of paid partnerships and commercial content.
- Creator travel platforms should embed disclosure guidance for destination campaigns, sponsored stays, and creator-hosted experiences.
- Any local marketing or user-facing content must avoid misleading claims around awards, exclusivity, or guaranteed creator exposure.

### 2.5 Tax, payments, and cross-border settlement
- GST applies to digital services, travel agency services, and event management services in India; the obligations differ for inbound vs domestic travel.
- Cross-border payout flows to creators, agencies, and suppliers should account for RBI regulations, inward remittance reporting, and foreign exchange compliance.
- Escrow or staged payment models are useful to manage disputes, supplier nonperformance, and creator production risk.

### 2.6 Safety, responsible tourism, and local permissions
- Responsible tourism frameworks are important in India’s sensitive cultural and ecological destinations.
- Creator tours in temples, heritage sites, national parks, and tribal areas often need additional community consent, conservation fees, and permit approvals.
- Local suppliers should be vetted for environmental compliance, cultural respect, and community benefit.

## 3. Product and operational implications

### 3.1 Agent workflows and compliance checks
- Build agent-facing verification for creator travel bookings that includes visa type, permit status, supplier registration, and GST eligibility.
- Provide pre-trip compliance checklists for producer visa, customs declaration, drone permits, and state-specific filming approvals.
- Offer a “creator production pack” for India that bundles local permits, content disclosure support, and host location approvals.

### 3.2 Local partner onboarding
- Require vendors to submit GST invoices, local business registration, and specific tourism or event facilitation credentials.
- Support aggregator-style partner onboarding for smaller experiential venues, guide collectives, and creator-facing production services.
- Maintain a partner risk score for categories such as transit, experiential stays, community visits, and production support.

### 3.3 Creator-facing messaging
- Educate creators on India-specific risks: permit requirements, customs for gear, local payment norms, and content disclosure obligations.
- Offer a creator journey guide for India that covers responsible tourism, local cultural norms, and the difference between shoot permits and tourist travel.
- Include country-specific FAQs for popular creator corridors: Goa, Rajasthan, Himachal Pradesh, Kerala, and Delhi/Mumbai.

## 4. Compliance playbook structure
- Market overview: creator corridors, demand segments, and regulatory landscape.
- Compliance radar: visa and permit requirements, payments, advertising disclosure, and consumer law.
- Product checklist: package types, partner controls, and agent workflows.
- Partner fit: supplier categories, onboarding docs, and risk scoring.
- Content hooks: SEO themes for creator travel in India, production permits, and creator-friendly itineraries.
