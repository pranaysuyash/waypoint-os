# Creator Identity Signals and Verification

This document defines the identity and trust signals required to safely use creator content and recommendations inside travel apps.

## Overview
- Travel creator identity is a product signal used to determine authenticity, reach, and reliability.
- Verification is especially important where creators recommend high-cost experiences, niche destinations, or regulated packages.
- The app should treat creator identity as a structured signal, not a simple follower count.

## Verification Criteria
- Identity validation: confirm creator name, business registration, or published social accounts.
- Experience proof: review past itineraries, verified bookings, and traveler feedback.
- Disclosure compliance: ensure creator content carries sponsor labels, affiliate disclosures, or partnership notices.
- Safety credentials: verify certifications for specialized travel content such as adventure guides, wellness influencers, or local insiders.

## Trust Signal Layers
- Platform badges: verified, rising creator, local expert, or partner creator.
- Behavioral metrics: conversion rate, refund rate, traveler satisfaction, and repeat performance.
- Publisher metadata: creator category, geography focus, content format, and publication cadence.
- External reputation: review sites, business directories, and creator networks.

## Product Usage
- Feed sorting: prioritize vetted creators for high-value travel products and emerging destinations.
- Display transparency: show trust icons and verification badges on creator profiles and content cards.
- Access control: limit notification triggers for unverified creators in sensitive categories such as adventure sports or regulated travel.
- Creator onboarding: offer a structured verification flow for creators to submit required credentials.

## Operational Best Practices
- Define a creator risk matrix based on content category and booking complexity.
- Automate low-risk verification tasks and route high-risk cases to human review.
- Continuously update verification status as creators evolve or change focus.
- Keep a rollback path to remove or demote creators when trust or compliance issues emerge.
