# Creator Platform Integration APIs and Partner SDKs

This document explains the platform integration layer for creator content, booking, and partner SDKs.

## Overview

- Travel apps supporting creators need APIs and SDKs that let creators connect products, share promotions, and surface creator data.
- A strong integration layer makes creators feel like partners, not just marketing channels.
- It also enables partner suppliers, venues, and local operators to consume creator signals and act on them.

## API Capabilities

- Creator product ingestion: APIs for creators to upload itineraries, event information, media, and digital assets.
- Booking and availability: endpoints for creator-specific inventory, ticketing, and reservation flow integration.
- Audience and analytics: data feeds for campaign performance, conversion tracking, and traveler engagement.
- Payouts and invoices: partner APIs for creator earnings, commission calculations, and settlement reconciliation.

## SDK and Partner Tools

- Mobile SDKs: let creators embed booking links, event ticketing, and offer discovery in their own apps.
- Widget libraries: reusable components for creator commerce, review cards, and product merchandising.
- Webhooks and callbacks: event notifications for booking status, cancellations, refund triggers, and content moderation.
- Sandbox environments: test creator integrations with realistic sample data and synthetic traveler flows.

## Governance and Security

- API access control: role-based permissions for creators, affiliate partners, suppliers, and internal teams.
- Data privacy: ensure creator and traveler data are segmented and only shared with authorized endpoints.
- Compliance: support audit logging, rate limiting, and secure credential rotation for partner integrations.
- Monitoring: measure API health, partner latency, and failed creator workflows.
