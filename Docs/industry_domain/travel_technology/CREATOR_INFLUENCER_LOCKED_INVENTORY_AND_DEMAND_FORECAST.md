# Creator Influencer Locked Inventory and Demand Forecast

This document describes how travel apps manage locked inventory commitments for creator-led products and forecast demand.

## Overview

- Creator-led offerings often involve inventory that must be reserved or guaranteed in advance.
- Accurate demand forecasting helps the app avoid overcommitting while supporting creator campaigns.
- Locked inventory can be a competitive edge if managed with strong forecasting and contingency planning.

## Inventory Models

- Reserved capacity: prebooked rooms, seats, or experiences specifically for creator campaigns.
- Allocated inventory: supplier blocks held for creator-travel specials or bundles.
- Dynamic hold: inventory that is loosely reserved until booking thresholds are met.
- Drop inventory: limited-time releases tied to creator promotions.

## Forecasting Principles

- Historical data: use prior creator campaign performance to estimate demand elasticity.
- Creator signals: incorporate anticipated creator reach, engagement, and booking intent.
- Inventory risk: model upside and downside scenarios for locked commitments.
- Supplier coordination: align forecast assumptions with partner capacity and pricing.

## Product Execution

- Offer creator products as limited releases with clear availability windows.
- Use demand forecasts to decide how much inventory to whitelist for creator campaigns.
- Provide creator dashboards with visibility into inventory status and sell-through.
- Offer alternative options when locked inventory sells out or supply changes.

## Risk Controls

- Capacity buffers: maintain conservative hold levels for new campaigns or untested creators.
- Cancellation terms: define how creator campaigns handle supplier cancellations or reassignments.
- Performance monitoring: track how closely forecasted demand matched actual bookings.
- Financial alignment: ensure forecasted inventory costs are reflected in creator compensation models.
