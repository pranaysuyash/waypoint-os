# GDS vs NDC vs API Connectivity

## Overview
This document compares the major connectivity models that travel agencies use to access airline and supplier content.

## GDS connectivity
- **What it is**: Traditional distribution through Sabre, Amadeus, Travelport, and other global systems.
- **Strengths**: Broad inventory, established workflows, consolidated global fares.
- **Weaknesses**: Limited merchandising, slower innovation, and legacy message formats.
- **Common use**: Corporate travel, consolidated supplier access, and legacy bookings.

## NDC connectivity
- **What it is**: IATA's New Distribution Capability for richer airline offers and direct retailing.
- **Strengths**: Dynamic bundles, personalized ancillaries, and richer offer content.
- **Weaknesses**: Fragmented adoption, technical complexity, and supplier-specific implementations.
- **Common use**: Premium corporate travel, airline direct retailing, and differentiated luxury offers.

## Direct API connectivity
- **What it is**: Direct supplier or aggregator APIs outside the GDS/NDC ecosystem.
- **Strengths**: Better control, lower fees, and more specialized content.
- **Weaknesses**: Higher integration cost, inconsistent data models, and fewer fallback options.
- **Common use**: Specialist product sourcing, regional carriers, and non-airline suppliers.

## Choosing the right model
- Use GDS when coverage and transactional reliability matter most.
- Use NDC when offer personalization and ancillary merchandising drive value.
- Use direct APIs when the product is specialized and the agency can support integration complexity.

## Why this matters
Knowing the differences enables agencies to balance product breadth, customer experience, and operational risk when designing their technology stack.
