# Creator Feed Relevance and User Intent Signaling

Creator feeds in travel apps must balance creator priority with authentic user intent. This document covers relevance scoring, intent signal capture, and the architecture for surfacing creator content without sacrificing traveler experience.

## 1. Intent signal taxonomy

- Passive signals: search queries, browse history, saved itineraries, destination interests.
- Active signals: booking intent, channel engagement, traveler preference tags, survey responses.
- Creator signals: creator affinity, content freshness, campaign alignment, earned trust.

## 2. Relevance scoring

- Hybrid ranking: combine user intent with creator relevance, performance, and compliance.
- Personalization layers: destination-based intent, trip purpose, mood-based experience preferences.
- Transparency knobs: allow users to tune content flavor, creator vs. editorial balance, and discovery focus.

## 3. Feed quality management

- Noise reduction: filter stale creator content, irrelevant campaign material, and low-value recommendations.
- Diversity enforcement: surface a range of creators, price points, and experience types.
- Experimentation: test intent-signal weighting, feed diversity controls, and creator promotion lift.

## 4. Operational controls

- Flow inspections: reviews for promoted vs. organic creator content, hidden boosts, and paid visibility.
- Privacy compliance: no use of sensitive intent signals without consent, no overly aggressive retargeting.
- User controls: preference centers, opt-outs, and explainers about how creator content is selected.
