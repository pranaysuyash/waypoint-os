# Travel Data Integument and Privacy-Preserving Compute

This document explores data integumentation and privacy-preserving compute in travel systems.

## Overview
- Data integument protects traveler information with secure wrappers and distributed computation.
- Privacy-preserving compute enables analytics without exposing raw personal data.
- These approaches are crucial for travel ecosystems that span jurisdictions and suppliers.

## Architecture
- Secure enclaves, homomorphic encryption, and federated learning for travel data.
- Data wrapping and access controls for PNRs, profiles, and booking histories.
- Compute models that keep sensitive data close to the source.

## Operational Implications
- Supplier collaboration on protected data products.
- Compliance with privacy laws while enabling cross-platform intelligence.
- Monitoring and governance for compute-bound data access.

## Commercial Impact
- New services based on safe data collaboration and analytics.
- Trust differentiation through strong privacy-preserving capabilities.
- Better personalization while minimizing privacy risk.

## Future Trends
- Open travel data networks built on privacy-preserving compute.
- Traveler-owned data models with protected analytics.
- Cross-supplier collaboration for fraud detection, loyalty, and disruption response.
