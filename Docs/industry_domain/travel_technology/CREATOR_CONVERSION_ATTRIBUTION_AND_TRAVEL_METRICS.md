# Creator Conversion Attribution and Travel Metrics

This document defines the metrics and modeling needed to measure creator-driven travel performance. It is intended for product teams, analytics teams, and content operators building creator travel programs and partner economics.

## 1. Overview
- Creator travel bookings require specialized attribution because creators often influence demand before a traveler enters the booking funnel.
- Metrics must reconcile social content, campaign engagement, partner referrals, digital commerce behavior, and offline fulfillment.
- The goal is to measure incremental bookings, creator ROI, and the value of creator-led inventory and partnerships.

## 2. Attribution taxonomy

### 2.1 Direct attribution
- Last-touch attribution credits the final interaction before conversion.
- In travel, this is often the booking page, checkout, or promo code redemption.
- It is simple but underestimates the long-tail influence of creator content.

### 2.2 Multi-touch attribution
- Multi-touch models allocate value across several creator touchpoints: discovery, consideration, retargeting, and booking.
- Weighted models can emphasize creator impressions, content engagement, landing page visits, and retargeted ad exposure.
- For creator travel, a multi-touch model should include both online and offline signals.

### 2.3 Incrementality measurement
- Incrementality compares bookings from creator campaigns against a baseline or control group.
- Examples: geo holdout tests, temporal A/B experiments, and comparative funnel analysis.
- Incrementality is critical for pricing creator commissions and distinguishing organic demand from campaign-driven demand.

### 2.4 Network attribution
- Creator travel often spreads through social graphs and peer referrals.
- Network models assign credit to creators whose audiences produce the highest downstream booking velocity.
- This is especially useful for creator communities, affiliate partner programs, and fan-driven itinerary sharing.

## 3. Travel-specific metric categories

### 3.1 Funnel metrics
- Awareness: creator reach, impressions, video views, and branded content engagement.
- Interest: landing page visits, content dwell time, and creator-specific microsite sessions.
- Intent: booking starts, quote requests, itinerary saves, and supplier inquiry events.
- Conversion: completed bookings, deposit payments, and package purchases.
- Retention: repeat bookings, community program re-enrollment, and loyalty engagement.

### 3.2 Creator campaign metrics
- Creator contribution rate: the share of bookings traceable to creator referrals or campaign signals.
- Creator ROI: revenue generated per creator dollar, including bookstore uplift and net margins.
- Creator activation lift: change in conversion rate for audiences exposed to creator content vs baseline.
- Creator content efficiency: bookings per content asset, cost per creator booking, and engagement-to-booking ratio.

### 3.3 Product metrics
- Creator package attach rate: percentage of bookings that include creator-branded or creator-curated packages.
- Creator upsell lift: additional ancillary revenue generated by creator-driven bookings.
- Partner conversion velocity: time from content exposure to booking for creator-affiliated products.
- Destination engagement index: creator-sourced demand for specific destinations, routes, or event experiences.

### 3.4 Operational metrics
- Attribution latency: time between creator exposure and booking completion, especially important for long-lead travel products.
- Data fidelity score: quality of mappings across creator IDs, tracking sources, and booking records.
- Dispute rate: incidence of attribution disputes with creators, partners, or suppliers.
- Payment reconciliation accuracy: alignment between credited creator commissions and actual settled revenue.

## 4. Data sources and integration
- Web analytics: campaign landing page sessions, referral tags, content source parameters.
- App analytics: in-app itinerary creation, wishlists, saved journeys, and checkout events.
- CRM and booking systems: lead records, booking references, supplier confirmations, and payment status.
- Creator platforms: social content performance, engagement metrics, promo code usage, and partnership agreements.
- Offline channels: call center bookings, email inquiries, and partner referrals tied back to creator campaigns.

## 5. Product and commercial implications

### 5.1 Creator pricing and commissions
- Attribution models should drive creator compensation frameworks that reward incremental revenue and quality bookings.
- Use supported metrics to define commission tiers, bonuses for high-conversion creators, and retention incentives.
- Ensure commission logic is transparent and traceable to reduce disputes.

### 5.2 Portfolio optimization
- Prioritize content formats and creator partnerships that generate the highest booking lift and lowest attribution leakage.
- Map creator performance by destination, package type, and booking channel.
- Allocate marketing spend to creator segments with the strongest incremental conversion signals.

### 5.3 Reporting and governance
- Provide creators with clear dashboards showing bookings, conversion rates, and revenue attribution.
- Build internal governance around attribution models, measurement assumptions, and update cadence.
- Document how creator attribution is calculated and how travel-specific signals are weighted.

## 6. Next research actions
- Define the optimal creator conversion window for travel bookings, especially long-lead and premium packages.
- Audit existing creator travel campaign data sources and identify attribution gaps.
- Build a test plan to compare last-touch, multi-touch, and incrementality measurement in creator travel programs.
