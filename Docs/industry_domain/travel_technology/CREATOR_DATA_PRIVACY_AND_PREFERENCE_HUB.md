# Creator Data Privacy and Preference Hub

This document describes how travel apps can manage creator-related data and traveler preferences in a privacy-first way.

## Overview
- Creator content personalization relies on data, but traveler privacy must remain central.
- A creator preference hub lets travelers control how creator signals and recommendations are used.
- Data handling should separate creator metadata from sensitive traveler profiles.

## Privacy Principles
- Transparency: tell travelers how creator content influences recommendations and marketing.
- Consent: obtain explicit permission for personalized creator matching and notifications.
- Minimization: only use creator-related data that improves the traveler experience.
- Control: allow travelers to opt out of creator-based personalization and tracking.

## Preference Hub Features
- Creator signals: let travelers select favorite creator themes, trusted partners, or avoidance lists.
- Notification settings: control push and email frequency for creator campaigns.
- Privacy settings: manage tracking, personalization, and third-party sharing.
- Feedback options: report unwanted creator content or request removal of specific creator recommendations.

## Product Implementation
- Separate preferences: keep creator preferences distinct from general travel preferences.
- Contextual controls: offer privacy options during booking, discovery, and creator onboarding.
- Default safety: default to conservative personalization unless the traveler opts in.
- Audit logs: record consent changes and preference updates for compliance.

## Travel App Benefits
- Better trust: travelers feel safer when creator recommendations respect their privacy.
- Higher relevance: explicit preferences can improve the quality of creator matches.
- Regulatory readiness: privacy-first design helps with GDPR, CCPA, and global compliance.
- Brand differentiation: transparency around creator personalization can become a competitive advantage.
