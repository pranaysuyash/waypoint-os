# Creator Recommender Explainability and Bias Control

This document explains how travel apps should manage explainability and bias control for creator recommendations.

## Overview

- Creator recommendations are powerful but can suffer from algorithmic bias, unfair amplification, and opaque ranking.
- Explainability builds traveler trust and helps creators understand why their products are surfaced.
- Bias control keeps the creator ecosystem diverse and avoids concentration on a small set of creators.

## Explainability Principles

- Transparent signals: show travelers the key factors behind creator ranking, such as relevance, popularity, and safety.
- Creator attribution: clearly explain when a creator suggestion is based on paid promotion, affinity, or editorial selection.
- Feedback loops: let travelers give input on why creator recommendations were useful or irrelevant.
- Documentation: maintain internal model documentation for creator recommendation logic and evaluation.

## Bias Control

- Diversity objectives: ensure creator recommender models surface a range of voices, destinations, and product types.
- Fairness metrics: track exposure distribution, fulfillment outcomes, and recommendation disparity.
- Debiasing techniques: apply controls for popularity bias, recency bias, and geographic concentration.
- Governance: review model changes for creator fairness and regulatory compliance.
