# Creator Attribution and Conversion Modeling

This document explains how travel apps model attribution and conversion for creator-driven bookings.

## Overview

- Creator-driven traffic has unique attribution challenges due to multi-touch pathways and cross-channel discovery.
- Accurate conversion modeling is essential for pricing creator commissions, measuring ROI, and optimizing campaigns.
- Models must reconcile creator signals with digital commerce, booking funnels, and offline fulfillment.

## Attribution Approaches

- Last-click attribution: simplest but may undervalue creator content that introduces travelers earlier.
- Multi-touch attribution: allocate credits across creator exposures, promotional touchpoints, and product interactions.
- Incrementality tests: measure which bookings occur because of creator campaigns versus baseline demand.
- Network attribution: factor in creator social graphs, community referrals, and influencer amplification.

## Conversion Modeling

- Funnel stages: model awareness, interest, intent, booking, and loyalty for creator-driven travel offers.
- Cross-device tracking: tie creator interactions to bookings across app, web, and offline channels.
- Econometric models: use historical campaign performance to forecast conversion rates and campaign ROI.
- Behavioral signals: incorporate engagement, dwell time, and creator content interaction into predictive models.

## Product Implications

- Budget allocation: direct spend to creators and channels with the strongest conversion lift.
- Commission structure: align creator payouts with demonstrated attribution and incremental bookings.
- Optimization: prioritize creator content formats and placement based on conversion performance.
- Transparency: provide creators and financial stakeholders with clear, data-backed attribution reports.
