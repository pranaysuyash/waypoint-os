# Creator Content Moderation and Trust Signal Engine

This document explains how travel apps should moderate creator content and generate trust signals for creator-driven products.

## Overview

- Creator content introduces new moderation challenges because traveler trust depends on authenticity, safety, and destination accuracy.
- A trust signal engine helps the platform validate creators, detect bad actors, and elevate reliable creator offerings.
- Effective moderation balances creator expression with traveler protection.

## Content Moderation

- Policy framework: define unacceptable claims, prohibited experiences, and local compliance requirements.
- Automated filters: scan creator media, descriptions, and promotions for banned terms, misleading claims, and policy violations.
- Human review: route flagged creator submissions and high-risk products to moderation specialists.
- Community reporting: enable travelers and partners to report creator content issues.

## Trust Signals

- Verification badges: show verified creators, vetted partners, and qualified local experiences.
- Reputation metrics: surface creator ratings, repeat bookings, and dispute history.
- Transparency cues: disclose fact-checked claims, sponsorship status, and certification levels.
- Signal fusion: combine social proof, operational data, and compliance status into a trust score.

## Product Implications

- Search ranking: prioritize creator offerings with stronger trust signals and cleaner moderation records.
- Onboarding: let creators earn trust badges as they demonstrate quality and compliance.
- Disclosure: clearly label moderated content and explain trust criteria to travelers.
- Risk management: use trust scores to decide which creator experiences require additional operational oversight.
