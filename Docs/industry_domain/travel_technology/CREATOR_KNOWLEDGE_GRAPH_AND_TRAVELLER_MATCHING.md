# Creator Knowledge Graph and Traveller Matching

This document explores how travel apps can use creator knowledge graphs to match travelers with the right creators and experiences.

## Overview

- A creator knowledge graph models relationships between creators, destinations, experiences, traveler profiles, and content themes.
- Matching travelers to creators improves relevance, conversion, and long-term engagement.
- Knowledge graphs help the app surface creator-driven recommendations that reflect both traveler intent and creator expertise.

## Knowledge Graph Elements

- Creator nodes: profile, expertise, audience, trust signals, and content style.
- Experience nodes: itineraries, packages, events, and local activities.
- Destination nodes: geography, seasonality, compliance requirements, and community partners.
- Traveler nodes: preferences, past bookings, intent signals, and privacy controls.

## Matching Strategies

- Graph traversal: use weighted connections to find creators with relevant past experiences.
- Semantic similarity: map traveler interests to creator themes using content embeddings and domain taxonomy.
- Hybrid scoring: combine explicit traveler preferences, creator trust, and product availability.
- Feedback loops: update the graph with booking outcomes, traveled experiences, and traveler ratings.

## Product Use Cases

- Creator discovery: recommend creators based on traveler mood, trip type, and destination.
- Itinerary pairing: suggest creator-led bundles that match traveler profile and seasonality.
- Campaign targeting: route creator promotions to travelers with the highest likely affinity.
- Community expansion: identify new creator niches by analyzing traveler-creator-interest gaps.

## Implementation Considerations

- Balance graph complexity with real-time performance.
- Use privacy-safe data models to avoid over-personalization.
- Keep graph signals interpretable for product teams and creator operations.
- Maintain creator metadata accuracy through portal updates and verification workflows.
