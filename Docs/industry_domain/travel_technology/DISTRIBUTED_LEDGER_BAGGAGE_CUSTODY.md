# Distributed Ledger Baggage Custody

This document explores how distributed ledger technology can strengthen baggage chain-of-custody, claims resolution, and operational transparency in travel.

## Overview
- Immutable baggage custody records for airport handlers, carriers, and ground logistics.
- Reducing disputes by recording handoffs, routing updates, and exception events on a shared ledger.
- The role of distributed ledger systems in a multilayer travel ecosystem.

## Architecture
- Shared ledger nodes operated by airlines, ground handling partners, and baggage tracking vendors.
- Integration with RFID/NFC baggage tags, sensors, and existing baggage reconciliation systems.
- Data partitioning and privacy controls for passenger-sensitive information.

## Operational Implications
- Real-time reconciliation across handlers and airports.
- Improved baggage recovery and claims workflows with verifiable custody histories.
- Reduced dependence on manual tags and paper-based transfer records.

## Commercial Impact
- Lower baggage claim costs and faster recovery for misrouted luggage.
- New supplier services around ledger-enabled baggage insurance and premium handling.
- Differentiation through transparent service-level commitments.

## Future Trends
- Tokenized baggage rights and ownership metadata.
- Cross-modal custody tracking for air-ground-port journeys.
- Smart contracts for automatic compensation on delayed or damaged baggage.
