# Creator Sustainable Supply Networks and Destination Stewardship

Creator travel programs should support sustainable destination networks and responsible supplier ecosystems. This document defines the structures for sourcing, governing, and stewarding sustainable supplier networks within creator-led travel experiences.

## 1. Sustainable supply network design

- Partner selection: prioritize suppliers with strong ESG credentials, local stewardship commitments, and low-impact operations.
- Destination-focused sourcing: integrate local sustainability priorities, community benefit, and regenerative tourism practices.
- Creator alignment: ensure creators can authentically promote sustainable experiences with verified impact.

## 2. Stewardship governance

- Supplier commitments: sustainability standards, reporting requirements, and destination stewardship obligations.
- Performance monitoring: carbon intensity, waste reduction, local employment, and community satisfaction.
- Incident escalation: handling greenwashing, supplier non-compliance, and destination harm.

## 3. Product integration

- Sustainable bundles: package creator-led experiences with verified sustainability credentials and impact narratives.
- Transparency: display certification, sustainability claims, and destination stewardship practices in creator content.
- Traveler choice: allow travelers to filter by sustainability attributes, local impact, and destination contributions.

## 4. Operational continuity

- Local stakeholder engagement: coordination with destination DMOs, community councils, and environmental NGOs.
- Renewal and refresh: update supplier networks based on stewardship performance, destination capacity, and emerging risk.
- Reporting: capture impact metrics, sustainability outcomes, and creator program contribution to destination resilience.
