# Domain Knowledge: DMC & Local Operators

**Category**: Supplier Ecosystem  
**Focus**: Destination Management Companies (DMCs), ground logistics, and local expertise.

---

## 1. What is a DMC?

A Destination Management Company (DMC) is a professional services company with local knowledge, expertise, and resources, working on the design and implementation of events, activities, tours, transportation, and program logistics.

### The "B2B" Layer
- **Logic**: DMCs do not usually sell to the public. They sell their expertise to "Outbound" travel agencies.
- **Value**: They provide the "On-the-ground" support that a GDS or Bed Bank cannot.

---

## 2. When to use a DMC?

### Complex Logistics
- **Example**: A 10-day safari in Kenya with multiple small-plane transfers, private guides, and cultural visits.
- **SOP**: The agency sends the "Client Profile" (Phase 1) to the DMC, who builds the "Local Itinerary."

### Niche Access
- **Example**: Private access to the Vatican after hours or a "Meet & Greet" with a local artisan in Kyoto.

---

## 3. Financial & Risk Mechanics

### Net Pricing (The "B2B Rate")
- **Logic**: DMCs provide a "Net Price" for the entire land package. The agency adds its markup (e.g., 15-20%).
- **SOP**: The agency is responsible for paying the DMC, usually 30-60 days before arrival.

### Liability & Insurance
- **Logic**: The DMC must have "Public Liability Insurance" for their local activities (e.g., if a traveler falls off a camel).
- **SOP**: The agency must verify the DMC's insurance certificates during the "Vendor Onboarding" (Phase 1).

---

## 4. The "White Label" Service

DMCs act as the "Silent Partner." When a traveler arrives at an airport, the person holding the sign has the **Agency's Name**, not the DMC's name.

### Duty of Care
- **Logic**: In a crisis, the DMC is the first responder.
- **SOP**: The agency must have the DMC's "Emergency 24/7 Number" in the traveler's itinerary.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **DMC No-show**: A traveler arrives at the airport in Lima, but the DMC's driver is not there. The agent must wake up the DMC manager (time-zone issues).
- **Quality Mismatch**: The "Luxury SUV" provided by the DMC is old and dirty. The agent must negotiate a partial refund or upgrade.
- **Local Crisis**: A strike occurs in Paris. The DMC must re-route all the traveler's transfers and tours in real-time.
