# Creator Remote Experience and Virtual Tour Partnerships

This document covers partnerships between creators and remote/virtual travel experiences.

## Overview

- Remote experiences and virtual tours expand creator travel products beyond physical trips.
- Travel apps can combine creator storytelling with immersive virtual offerings for audiences who cannot travel.
- These partnerships require unique supplier, content, and monetization strategies.

## Product Types

- Live virtual tours: creator-hosted remote walkthroughs of destinations, hotels, or events.
- Interactive workshops: cultural classes, cooking sessions, or language experiences led by creators.
- Hybrid products: virtual previews paired with future physical bookings.
- On-demand content: packaged creator-led virtual experiences for revenue or subscription access.

## Partnership Considerations

- Content quality: ensure creators collaborate with skilled hosts and production partners.
- Technology: support streaming, interactivity, and accessible delivery across devices.
- Monetization: price experiences as standalone products, subscriptions, or promotional funnels.
- Audience fit: match virtual offers to travelers who are scouting future trips or seeking remote enrichment.

## App Benefits

- Reach: remote experiences can engage broader audiences and drive app installs.
- Conversion funnel: virtual tours can warm travelers toward later bookings.
- Creator brand: support creator monetization beyond traditional physical travel.
- Flexibility: virtual products preserve creator relevance during travel disruptions.

## Operational Controls

- Partner vetting: qualify virtual tour hosts, production quality, and platform reliability.
- Scheduling: coordinate creator, host, and traveler availability for live sessions.
- Content rights: define usage and redistribution rights for virtual experiences.
- Feedback: capture attendee ratings and creator performance signals.
