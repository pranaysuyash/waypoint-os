# Creator Curated Destination Inventory Strategy

This document explores how creators can act as a distinct supplier class for destination inventory and experiential travel products.

## 1. Creator-curated inventory types

- Experience bundles: creator-designed itineraries, workshops, meetups, and local immersion journeys.
- Destination collections: themed destination portfolios, brand-aligned locales, and community-curated sites.
- Hybrid products: creator-only access tiers, VIP experiences, and content co-created packages.

## 2. Supplier model distinctions

- Creator as supplier: creators package access, curation, and narrative into inventory that can be booked.
- Creator as distribution layer: creators drive demand for supplier inventory without owning it.
- Mixed models: hybrid arrangements where creators co-own capacity or reserve inventory with partner suppliers.

## 3. Governance and sourcing

- Partner verification: vetting local suppliers for quality, reliability, brand fit, and compliance.
- Curation standards: editorial criteria, destination sustainability, cultural sensitivity, and guest expectations.
- Inventory refresh: publishing cadence, expiration, and path-to-retirement for creator-curated listings.

## 4. Product and experience strategy

- Positioning: how creator inventory differs from standard travel packages and should be marketed.
- Bundling: combining supply, transport, content moments, and event access into a single creator offering.
- Pricing: premium, commission, and incentive structures for creator-curated products.

## 5. Research questions

- What governance mechanisms protect suppliers when creators package their destinations?
- How should platforms manage inventory ownership, attribution, and creator commissions?
- Which creator-curated models are most scalable for travel marketplaces?
- How can curated destination inventory preserve both creator brand and supplier operational reliability?
