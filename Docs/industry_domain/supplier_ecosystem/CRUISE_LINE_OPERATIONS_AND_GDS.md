# Domain Knowledge: Cruise Line Operations & GDS

**Category**: Supplier Ecosystem  
**Focus**: Specialized cruise GDS (Polar/CruisingPower), stateroom categories, and deck plans.

---

## 1. The "Polar" System & Specialized GDS

Cruise lines do not typically use the "Air" GDS (Amadeus/Sabre) for the core booking. They use specialized B2B portals or integrations.

### Key Platforms
- **Polar**: Used by Cunard, Princess, and Holland America.
- **CruisingPower**: Used by Royal Caribbean and Celebrity.
- **Seaview**: Used by MSC Cruises.
- **SOP**: The agent must link the "Cruise Booking ID" into the main "Master Trip ID" in the Mid-Office.

---

## 2. Stateroom Categories & Deck Plans

### The "Category" Maze
- **Logic**: A single ship can have 20+ categories (Inside, Oceanview, Balcony, Mini-Suite, Grand Suite).
- **GTY (Guarantee) Bookings**: The client pays a lower price but the cruise line assigns the specific cabin later.
- **SOP**: For S1/S2 luxury travelers, **Never** book GTY. Always select a specific cabin number to avoid being placed under the "Galley" or "Nightclub" (noise issues).

---

## 3. Financial Mechanics (The "Commission Heavy" Sector)

Cruises are one of the most profitable sectors for agencies.

### Commission Tiers
- **Base**: 10-16% on the "Cruise Fare."
- **NCCF (Non-Commissionable Cruise Fare)**: A portion of the price (Taxes/Port Fees) that is **Not** commissionable.
- **Overrides**: Large agencies get "Back-end" overrides based on annual volume (e.g., an extra 2%).

---

## 4. Shore Excursions & On-board Credit (OBC)

### OBC (On-board Credit)
- **Logic**: Agencies often "gift" OBC (e.g., $250) to the traveler.
- **SOP**: Confirm that the OBC is visible on the traveler's "Cruise Card" upon boarding.

### Private Shore Excursions
- **Logic**: Cruise-sold tours are crowded. DMCs (Phase 1) provide "Private" tours that return to the ship on time.
- **Risk**: If a private tour is late, the ship **Will Not Wait**. If a cruise-sold tour is late, the ship **Must Wait**.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Galley" Nightmare**: A VIP is assigned a room directly under the kitchen. The noise starts at 4 AM. The agent must negotiate a room change mid-ocean.
- **Missed Port**: Due to weather, the ship skips the port where the traveler had a $5,000 private DMC tour booked. The agent must handle the refund/insurance claim.
- **OBC Disappearance**: The traveler arrives on the ship, but the $500 promised OBC is not on their account.
