# Supplier Quality and Contract Management

## Overview
This document explains how travel agencies manage supplier quality, contract terms, and ongoing performance to protect service delivery.

## Supplier quality dimensions
- Reliability and fulfillment accuracy
- Responsiveness and communications
- Pricing transparency and rate integrity
- Product fit for traveler segments
- Compliance with agency standards

## Contract management practices
### Contract negotiation
- Define scope: rooms, transfers, tours, insurance, and special services
- Agree on rates, cancellations, and change policies
- Build in performance incentives or volume discounts

### Contract governance
- Track contract expiry and renewal dates
- Maintain a repository of contract clauses and supplier SLAs
- Monitor compliance with payment terms and cancellation conditions

### Supplier performance reviews
- Collect operational KPIs: delivery accuracy, on-time performance, issue resolution
- Use traveler feedback and dispute data to rate suppliers
- Escalate or remove underperforming suppliers from active use

## Common supply chain risks
- Overreliance on a single provider for critical inventory
- Hidden blackout dates or rate restrictions
- Poor communication during disruption or service failure

## Why this matters
Supplier quality and contract management are essential for consistent travel delivery. Strong supplier governance reduces disruption, improves margins, and protects the agency from reputational risk.
