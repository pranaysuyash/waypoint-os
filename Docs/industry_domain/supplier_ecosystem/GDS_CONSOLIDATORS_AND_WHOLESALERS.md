# Domain Knowledge: GDS Consolidators & Wholesalers

**Category**: Supplier Ecosystem  
**Focus**: Net fares, consolidator desks, and inventory arbitrage.

---

## 1. What is a Consolidator?

A consolidator is a large agency that buys airfare and hotel inventory in bulk and resells it to smaller agencies or independent contractors.

### Why use them?
- **Price**: They have "Net Fares" (Private fares) that are significantly lower than public fares.
- **Ticketing Authority**: Small agencies (Non-IATA) use a consolidator's IATA number to issue tickets.
- **Expertise**: They often have "Complex Itinerary" desks that can manually price "Around the World" (RTW) trips.

---

## 2. Airfare Arbitrage (Net Fares)

### The "Published" vs. "Net" Gap
- **Published**: The price on the airline's website.
- **Net**: The price the consolidator gives to the agent.
- **Action**: The agent can add their own "Markup" on top of the Net fare. The client sees a single "Inclusive" price.

---

## 3. Bed Banks (Hotel Wholesalers)

### Examples: Hotelbeds, WebBeds, TBO Holidays
- **Logic**: These platforms aggregate rates from thousands of hotels.
- **Anomalies**: Sometimes the Bed Bank rate is lower than the hotel's "Direct" rate, but it may have stricter cancellation policies.
- **SOP**: Always compare the "Bed Bank" rate with the "Preferred Partner" rate. Use Bed Banks for price-sensitive clients and Preferred Partners for VIPs (to get amenities).

---

## 4. Consolidator Risk Management

### The "Ticketing Delay"
- **Issue**: Some consolidators take 24-48 hours to issue a ticket after payment. In that time, the fare may change.
- **SOP**: Always check the "Ticketing Deadline" (Phase 2).

### The "Service Hand-off"
- **Logic**: If a flight is cancelled, who handles it? The airline, the consolidator, or the agent?
- **SOP**: For Net Fares, the **Agent** is usually responsible for the disruption recovery, as the airline will tell the traveler to "Contact your travel agent."

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **Consolidator Bankruptcy**: A large consolidator goes bust with $50k of the agency's client funds. The agency must secure the bookings or refund the clients.
- **Fare Jump**: An agent quotes a Net Fare, but by the time the client pays, the consolidator has "sold out" of that fare class.
- **Disruption Loop**: An airline cancels a consolidator ticket. The agent must navigate the "Consolidator Support Queue" while the traveler is stranded.
