# Creator Marketplace Governance and Content Curations

This document covers governance, curation, and supplier management for creator-driven marketplaces.

## Overview

- Creator marketplaces need governance rules to balance creative freedom, quality, and compliance.
- Content curation is essential when creators introduce travel packages, digital goods, and experiential services.
- Marketplace governance protects travelers and preserves supplier trust.

## Governance Rules

- Submission policies: standards for creator products, destination claims, and supporting evidence.
- Eligibility: criteria for creators, supplier partners, and co-branded collaborators.
- Approval workflows: manual and automated review steps before creator content goes live.
- Enforcement: take-down processes, rights management, and appeal mechanisms.

## Content Curation

- Editorial selection: surface premium creator experiences based on quality, risk, and alignment.
- Discovery signals: blend creator popularity with product relevance and destination value.
- Seasonal programming: rotate creator offerings as destinations, demand, and travel windows change.
- Featured collections: bundle creator content into thematic campaigns, local guides, or VIP series.

## Supplier Ecosystem Alignment

- Partner tiers: differentiate digital-first creators from experience suppliers, local operators, and brands.
- Revenue splits: align marketplace fees with creator, supplier, and platform economics.
- Quality assurance: monitor ratings, cancellation rates, and traveler feedback across creator marketplace items.
- Marketplace transparency: clearly disclose creator roles, partner relationships, and service boundaries.
