# Travel Insurance Partners and Assistance Networks

## Overview
This document explains how travel agencies work with insurance carriers, emergency assistance providers, and local support networks.

## Partner types
- **Travel insurance carriers**: provide trip cancellation, interruption, medical, and baggage coverage
- **Assistance networks**: provide 24/7 emergency support, medical evacuation, and security monitoring
- **Local rescue and ground providers**: handle on-the-ground response during crises

## Agency responsibilities
- Present insurance options clearly and explain coverage gaps
- Help clients choose the right plan for destination risk and traveler profile
- Assist with claims documentation and emergency plan activation
- Track partner performance on responsiveness and claims turnaround

## Integration with agency workflows
- Include insurance options at proposal and checkout stages
- Capture policy details in the traveler profile and itinerary notes
- Link policy validity to visa or country entry requirements where applicable
- Provide emergency contact cards with local assistance numbers

## Operational best practices
- Maintain a preferred panel of insurers and assistance partners by region
- Test emergency response handoffs with scenario exercises
- Verify that partner services cover high-risk items such as medical repatriation and security evacuation
- Use partner portals or APIs to automate policy issuance and claims follow-up

## Why this matters
Insurance and assistance are often the last line of defense for travelers. Agencies that manage these partnerships well reduce customer anxiety and recovery time when incidents occur.
