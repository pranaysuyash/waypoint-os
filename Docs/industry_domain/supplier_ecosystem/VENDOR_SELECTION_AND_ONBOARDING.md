# Vendor Selection and Onboarding

## Overview
This document outlines how travel agencies choose new suppliers and bring them into the agency's operating ecosystem.

## Supplier selection criteria
- Product fit: geography, price level, and service type
- Reliability: fulfillment accuracy, cancellation rates, and responsiveness
- Margin potential: commission levels, markups, and fee flexibility
- Contract terms: payment terms, cancellation policy, and liability clauses
- Technology integration: API connectivity, content feeds, and booking tools

## Onboarding process
1. Submit supplier profile and capability questionnaire
2. Validate commercial terms and service level agreements
3. Configure the supplier in agency systems (GDS/PMS/CMS)
4. Train agents on product rules, fare restrictions, and refund policies
5. Perform test bookings and exception handling drills

## Ongoing monitoring
- Track booking success rate and settlement accuracy
- Review supplier performance against scorecards
- Refresh contracts when service quality or market conditions change
- Manage supplier risk through diversification and backup providers

## Why this matters
A strong supplier onboarding process reduces booking errors, minimizes revenue leakage, and ensures agents can quote accurate, compliant travel options to customers.
