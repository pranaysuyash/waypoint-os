# Creator Supplier Credentialing and Risk

This document covers how creator-driven travel products must manage supplier credibility, risk, and compliance.

## Overview

- Creator-recommended travel experiences often rely on non-traditional suppliers, local operators, and small service providers.
- Travel apps should credential these suppliers before promoting them via creator content.
- Risk management is central to protecting travelers, brand reputation, and legal liability.

## Credentialing Criteria

- Legal status: verify supplier business registration, permits, and insurance.
- Operational capability: confirm capacity, safety protocols, and quality controls.
- Financial integrity: assess payment terms, settlement reliability, and refund policies.
- Cultural and environmental fit: ensure local suppliers respect destination rules and sustainability practices.

## Risk Categories

- Safety risk: adventure activities, remote experiences, and wellness services demand extra oversight.
- Regulatory risk: tours, wellness retreats, and local services may require licensing or specific approvals.
- Reputation risk: low-quality or misrepresented suppliers can damage the creator and app brand.
- Service risk: last-minute cancellations, availability mismatches, and supplier no-shows.

## App Product Controls

- Supplier profile: display supplier credentials, certifications, and verified status in creator product cards.
- Risk indicators: mark higher-risk experiences and require explicit traveler acknowledgement.
- Booking contracts: attach supplier terms, cancellation policies, and liability notices to creator-led packages.
- Supplier audits: perform periodic reviews and traveler feedback analysis for creator-referenced suppliers.

## Operational Playbook

- Onboard suppliers with clear creator product acceptance criteria.
- Create a risk scoring model that combines supplier checks, experience type, and creator trust level.
- Use supplier credentialing as a gating mechanism before creator content goes live.
- Maintain a blacklist/watchlist for suppliers with repeated issues.

## Business Outcomes

- Traveler protection: a safer creator network builds stronger long-term trust.
- Product reliability: reliable suppliers increase conversion and reduce refund rates.
- Creator confidence: creators can promote experiences with verified suppliers more confidently.
- Platform differentiation: safe creator commerce becomes a competitive advantage versus informal social bookings.
