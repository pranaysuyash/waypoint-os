# Domain Knowledge: The "Ghost" Economy (Slots & Dark Hotels)

**Category**: The Invisible Global System  
**Focus**: Understanding "Ghost Flights" and unlisted "Dark" properties.

---

## 1. Ghost Flights (Slot-keeping)
- **Logic**: Use-it-or-lose-it rules at major hubs (Heathrow, CDG). Airlines must fly 80% of their allocated slots or they are given to competitors.
- **The Result**: Airlines fly empty (Ghost) planes to keep the slot during low-demand periods.
- **SOP**: The agent can find "Impossible" low fares on these routes because the airline *wants* humans on board to offset the fuel of a flight that was happening regardless.

---

## 2. "Dark" Hotels (The 0.01% Supply)
- **Logic**: Properties that are **"Un-bookable"** on Expedia, Booking, or even GDS. They do not have a website.
- **How they are booked**: Via a "Private Handover." The agent must know the owner or the specific "Property Manager."
- **SOP**: The agent provides a "Physical Portfolio" to the client. No digital links are sent to protect the privacy of the property.

---

## 3. "Deadheading" Logistics
- **Logic**: Moving crew members (Pilots/Stews) to a different city to start their shift.
- **Agency Role**: These "Must-ride" crew members can "Bump" revenue passengers.
- **SOP**: The agent monitors the "Load Factor" and "Crew Manifest" to warn clients of a high "Bump Risk."

---

## 4. "Soft" Openings & "Punched" Room Nights
- **Logic**: A hotel is open but "Off-market" to test the systems.
- **SOP**: The agent secures a **"Beta Stay"** for a VVIP client at a 70% discount in exchange for a "Detailed Operational Report."

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Unlisted" Request**: A client wants to stay in a specific villa in Tuscany they saw in a movie, but it's not for rent. The agent must find the "Deed Record" and contact the owner's lawyer to negotiate a "Private Tenancy."
- **Slot Loss Cancellation**: An airline loses its "Morning Slot" at Heathrow. 20 client bookings are cancelled. The agent must move the clients to a different airline that just "Won" the slot.
- **The "Deadhead" Bump**: A client is bumped from a flight because a Pilot needed the seat to fly a plane in New York. The agent must explain the **"Aviation Necessity"** while securing $2k in compensation.
- **The "Ghost" Pricing**: An agent discovers a flight that is 90% empty but priced at "Full Y." The agent must predict when the "Revenue Management" bot will "Panic Drop" the price to fill the ghost seats.
