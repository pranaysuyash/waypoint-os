# Vendor Partner Relationships in Travel Distribution

## Overview
Vendor partnerships are the foundation of agency delivery. This doc explains the types of partner relationships and how agencies manage them.

## Partner categories
- Airline consolidators
- Hotel wholesalers and bedbanks
- GDS providers
- Ground handlers and DMCs
- Insurance providers
- Experience and activity suppliers

## Relationship models
- Commission-based: agent earns a percentage of supplier revenue
- Markup-based: agency adds a margin on top of supplier cost
- Net rate: agency sells a negotiated low cost with fixed commission
- Preferred partner: volume commitments in exchange for better terms

## Operational considerations
- Contract management and performance monitoring
- Payment terms, invoicing, and reconciliation
- Quality assurance and supplier audits
- Policy alignment for cancellations, refunds, and dispute resolution

## Why this matters
Good partner relationships determine inventory quality, margin stability, and the agency's ability to handle complex or bespoke travel requests.
