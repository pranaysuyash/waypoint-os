# Domain Knowledge: Boutique & Independent Hotel Retailing

**Category**: Supplier Ecosystem  
**Focus**: Non-GDS hotels, direct contracting, and channel manager integration.

---

## 1. The "Off-GDS" Challenge

Many of the world's most exclusive boutique hotels (e.g., Ryokans in Japan, Safari Camps in Botswana) are not in Amadeus or Sabre.

### Sourcing Strategies
- **Wholesale Aggregators**: Using platforms like *Hotelbeds* or *WebBeds* that specialize in boutique inventory.
- **Direct Contracts**: The agency signs a "Preferred Partner" agreement directly with the hotel.
- **Channel Managers**: Using tech like *Siteminder* or *STAAH* to see live inventory for small hotels.

---

## 2. The "Soft" Brand Networks

- **Logic**: Independent hotels often join "Soft Brands" to get global visibility without losing their identity.
- **Examples**: *Leading Hotels of the World (LHW)*, *Small Luxury Hotels (SLH)*, *Relais & Châteaux*.
- **SOP**: The agent must check if the agency is a member of the brand's "Agent Program" (e.g., LHW VITA) to secure perks (Upgrades, Breakfast, $100 Credit).

---

## 3. Direct Booking SOP (The "Confirm & Re-confirm")

- **Logic**: Small hotels are more prone to "lost" reservations from third-party systems.
- **SOP**:
    - **T-7 Re-confirmation**: 7 days before arrival, the agent must send a direct email/call to the hotel front desk with the client's name and specific arrival time.
    - **VIP Note**: Ensuring the "Hotel Manager" knows a high-value (S1) client is arriving.

---

## 4. Payment Challenges (Net vs. Gross)

- **Net Rates**: The agency pays the hotel a discounted rate and keeps the markup. (Better for cash flow).
- **Gross Rates**: The client pays the hotel, and the hotel pays the agency a commission (High risk of non-payment).

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Missing Booking"**: A traveler arrives at a remote Ryokan in Kyoto at 9 PM; the hotel says "No reservation." The agent must provide proof of the "Channel Manager" confirmation and negotiate a room.
- **The "Perk Failure"**: A client is booked at an SLH hotel via the agency, but the hotel refuses the "Free Breakfast" perk.
- **Inventory Discrepancy**: A boutique hotel shows "Sold Out" in the GDS but "Available" on their direct website. The agent must call to "Force-open" the GDS inventory.
- **Payment Dispute**: A small hotel in Greece demands a "Bank Transfer" for a $20,000 villa booking instead of a credit card. The agent must manage the secure transfer.
