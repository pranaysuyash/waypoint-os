# Supplier Performance Scorecards and Monitoring

## Overview
A supplier performance scorecard helps agencies evaluate suppliers on reliability, service quality, and commercial value.

## Key performance dimensions
- Fulfillment accuracy and ticketing correctness
- On-time confirmation and change handling
- Cancellation and refund responsiveness
- Service quality and traveler satisfaction
- Contract compliance and settlement accuracy

## Metrics to track
- Booking success rate
- Refund / void processing time
- Error rate by travel product type
- Supplier escalation incidence
- Commission and invoicing discrepancies

## Monitoring process
1. Define scorecard metrics and acceptable thresholds
2. Collect data from booking, support, and settlement systems
3. Review performance on a regular cadence (weekly / monthly)
4. Flag low performers and identify root causes
5. Adjust sourcing, contract terms, or volume allocation accordingly

## Using scorecards strategically
- Prioritize resilient suppliers for high-risk and premium itineraries
- Reward strong supplier partners with more volume or preferred placement
- Use scorecards as evidence in contract negotiations and renewal discussions

## Why this matters
Proactive supplier monitoring protects agencies from hidden operational costs and strengthens the supplier network over time.
