# Domain Knowledge: Hotel Programs & Preferred Partner Revenue

**Category**: Supplier Ecosystem  
**Focus**: Negotiated rates, luxury amenities, and commission optimization.

---

## 1. Preferred Partner Programs (The "Invite-Only" Networks)

Large agencies gain access to "Consortia" or "Preferred Partner" programs that provide exclusive benefits.

### Virtuoso / Signature / Traveler Made
- **Benefits**: $100 Resort Credit, Free Breakfast, Room Upgrades (Subject to Availability), Early Check-in/Late Check-out.
- **SOP**: Prioritize Virtuoso-affiliated hotels for S1/S2 luxury travelers.

### Hotel Direct Programs
- **Examples**: Four Seasons "Preferred Partner," Rosewood "Elite," Hyatt "Privé."
- **Logic**: These direct programs often offer *better* benefits and higher "Upgrade Priority" than general consortia.
- **System Action**: Mapping the "Traveler Rank" to the most advantageous program for each booking.

---

## 2. Negotiated Corporate Rates (LNRs)

### Local Negotiated Rates (LNR)
- **Logic**: If an agency sends 500 room-nights a year to a specific hotel (e.g., The Taj Mahal Palace, Mumbai), they negotiate a fixed, lower rate for their clients.
- **SOP**: Use the "Agency Code" (e.g., `CORP123`) in the GDS during booking to access these exclusive rates.

---

## 3. Commission Management (The "Long Tail" Revenue)

Hotels pay commissions (usually 10%) on the "Pre-tax" room rate.

### Commission Anomalies
- **Net Rates**: Some rates are "Net" (No commission), but have a lower price.
- **Commissionable Markups**: Adding a hidden markup on top of a net rate.
- **Logic**: For corporate clients, use "Negotiated Net Rates" with a transparent management fee. For luxury clients, use "Commissionable Rates" with added amenities.

---

## 4. Operational SOP: The "VIP Amenities" Flow
1. **The Request**: During Phase 2, the agent must email the "Hotel Sales Manager" or "Concierge" to confirm the VIP arrival.
2. **The "Special Request"**: Mention the traveler's preferences (extracted in Phase 1) — e.g., "Non-feather pillows" or "Favorite brand of sparkling water."
3. **The Verification**: Confirm that the $100 credit is loaded on the traveler's folio *before* they check in.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **Upgrade Failure**: A Virtuoso traveler is not given an upgrade despite availability. The agent must call the GM (General Manager) to resolve.
- **Commission Dispute**: A hotel claims the booking was "Direct" and refuses to pay the agency's 10% commission.
- **Amenity Confusion**: The traveler is charged for breakfast that was supposed to be "Complimentary" under the Privé program.
