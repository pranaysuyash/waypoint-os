# Creator Local Ops and Community-Based Supply

This document explains supplier ecosystem design for creator-powered local operations and community-based supply.

## Overview

- Creator-led travel experiences often depend on local operators and community partners for delivery.
- Community-based supply helps scale creator products while preserving authenticity and local value.
- Apps must manage these partnerships with governance, quality controls, and ecosystem curation.

## Local Operations

- Local partner roles: guide hosts, venue operators, activity producers, and event facilitators.
- Partnership models: revenue-sharing, contractor agreements, and community-led collectives.
- Operational handoff: align creator briefs with local execution plans and traveler touchpoints.
- Capacity planning: build local supply flexibility for creator products that can vary by season and demand.

## Community Supply

- Community sourcing: invite local creators, artisans, and hosts to contribute experiences.
- Curation standards: verify local partners for safety, authenticity, and legal status.
- Community economics: compensate local providers fairly while keeping creator products viable.
- Feedback loops: use traveler and creator ratings to refine local community supplier selection.

## Product Integration

- Highlight local stories and community impact in creator product descriptions.
- Use local partner profiles to build traveler trust in creator experiences.
- Measure the health of community supply through availability, cancellations, and quality ratings.
- Support resilience by maintaining geographic diversity in local operators.
