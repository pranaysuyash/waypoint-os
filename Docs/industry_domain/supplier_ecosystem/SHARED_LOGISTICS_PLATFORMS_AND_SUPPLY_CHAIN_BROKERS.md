# Shared Logistics Platforms and Supply Chain Brokers

This document examines shared logistics platforms, supply chain brokerage, and collaborative supplier orchestration.

## Overview
- The emergence of shared logistics platforms in travel.
- Supply chain brokers as intermediaries for complex travel products.
- Collaboration models between carriers, suppliers, and agencies.

## Platform Design
- Multi-supplier aggregation, catalogue orchestration, and offer distribution.
- Inventory sharing, dynamic supplier matching, and real-time fulfillment.
- Commissioning, service-level guarantees, and platform fees.

## Operational Implications
- Brokerage workflows for capacity sourcing and contract activation.
- Resilience through multi-supplier fallback and shared inventory.
- Supplier performance monitoring and dispute handling.

## Commercial Impacts
- Platform economics versus direct procurement.
- Margin layering, transparency, and supplier incentives.
- Marketplace governance for travel supply networks.

## Future Trends
- Autonomous supply orchestration and programmatic sourcing.
- Shared logistics platforms as travel ecosystem infrastructure.
- Broker-led innovation in atypical travel products.
