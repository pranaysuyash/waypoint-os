# Programmatic Supply Pathing and Resilience

This document explores programmatic supply pathing for travel inventory, enabling resilience through dynamic routing, fallback paths, and real-time supplier selection.

## Overview
- Supply pathing defines how travel products are sourced across architectures and partners.
- Programmatic pathing dynamically chooses suppliers, offers, and routes based on availability, cost, and policy.
- Resilience is achieved through fallback catalogs, alternate flows, and automated reconciliation.

## Architecture
- Supply path engines that map demand to the best supplier path.
- Rules for routing requests across GDS, NDC, APIs, and bespoke providers.
- Real-time evaluation of price, inventory certainty, and fulfillment risk.

## Operational Implications
- Automated re-routing when preferred suppliers fail or are unavailable.
- Supplier health monitoring and path redundancy.
- Governance models for path prioritization and profit allocation.

## Commercial Impact
- Better conversion through smarter supplier selection.
- Reduced dependency on single-source inventory.
- New revenue opportunities from premium guaranteed paths and resiliency services.

## Future Trends
- Autonomous supply pathing powered by AI and intent-aware optimization.
- Cross-network orchestration across travel, hospitality, and ground transport.
- Path-level analytics for availability, latency, and supplier risk.
