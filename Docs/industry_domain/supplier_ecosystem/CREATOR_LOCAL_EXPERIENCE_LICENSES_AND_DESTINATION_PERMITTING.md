# Creator Local Experience Licenses and Destination Permitting

Creator-led local experiences require the same legal and operational discipline as any supplier-managed tour. This document explains the licensing, permitting, and destination-level approvals needed to activate creator-curated experiences.

## 1. Permit landscape

- Local activity permits: performance, filming, hospitality, transportation, public space use.
- Destination permissions: cultural sites, conservation zones, city events, hospitality takeovers.
- Temporary and pop-up use cases: one-off experiences, flash events, immersive dining.

## 2. Supplier compliance

- Partner credentials: local operator licenses, supplier insurance, safety certifications.
- Content-led liability: creator obligation to vet local partners, verify credentials, and disclose risk.
- Shared governance: app/platform audit rights, supplier compliance scorecards, corrective action.

## 3. Cultural and community safeguards

- Community impact review: local economic benefit, crowd control, neighborhood sustainability.
- Sensitive site protocols: heritage sites, indigenous land, protected areas, documentary filming.
- Host community liaison roles: local guides, destination ambassadors, liaison officers.

## 4. Integration with travel sourcing

- Experience bundling: licensed creator experiences as formal supplier inventory.
- Permit-aware pricing: inclusion of licensing costs, permit fees, and destination surcharges.
- Renewal and seasonal cycles: permit reapplication, destination event windows, regulatory seasonality.
