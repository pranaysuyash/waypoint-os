# Creator Partner Marketplace GTM and Supplier Onboarding

This document defines a go-to-market and supplier onboarding playbook for creator-facing travel marketplaces. It is intended to help product, operations, and partnership teams design a launch-ready ecosystem.

## 1. Strategic thesis

- Creator travel marketplaces require partner trust, clear onboarding, and a strong value proposition for suppliers.
- The GTM motion must align creator curation with supplier performance, compliance, and marketplace governance.
- Supplier onboarding should be designed as a repeatable funnel with risk gating, partner enablement, and co-marketing pathways.

## 2. Partner personas

- Local experience operators: tours, transfers, lounges, short-stay hospitality, and guided city experiences.
- Accommodation hosts: boutique hotels, short-stay properties, and creator-ready residency spaces.
- Travel service providers: transfer networks, insurance, visa support, and logistic operators.
- Creator-curated suppliers: influencer-approved venues, pop-up experiences, and destination producers.

## 3. GTM value propositions

- For suppliers: new demand from creator audiences, co-branded distribution, premium visibility, and operational support.
- For creators: trusted supplier networks, revenue share certainty, risk-managed production, and compliance-ready partners.
- For customers: curated experiences, transparent packaging, seamless booking flows, and clear layover or event-ready itineraries.

## 4. Onboarding and supply pathing

- Entry criteria: regulatory eligibility, quality standards, content fit, and creator alignment.
- Tiered onboarding: pilot partners, verified partner programs, and scaled supplier networks.
- Risk controls: partner credentialing, contract terms, insurance requirements, and compliance checklists.
- Enablement materials: partner portal guides, onboarding templates, supplier playbooks, and quality scorecards.

## 5. Marketplace mechanics

- Offer taxonomy: creator bundles, stopover-friendly packages, partner-only inventories, and flexible fulfillment options.
- Commission and payout models: creator revenue share, supplier margin transparency, and performance tier incentives.
- Feedback loops: partner performance dashboards, creator reviews, traveler satisfaction signals, and dispute resolution.
- Co-marketing: joint campaigns, creator-published itineraries, supplier stories, and destination promotions.

## 6. Operational readiness

- Launch checklist: partner set-up, product rules, pricing governance, claims handling, and support escalation.
- Compliance guardrails: visa/labor risk flagged in supplier intake, local authorization checks, and brand safety controls.
- Scale signals: partner pipeline health, onboarding velocity, marketplace fill rates, and supplier performance segmentation.

## 7. Next research actions

- Map the high-value supplier categories for creator layovers, stopovers, and creator-led product bundles.
- Define a marketplace readiness score for partners across quality, compliance, and creator-fit.
- Identify the best partner onboarding formats for creator marketplaces: self-service, curated outreach, or agency-assisted onboarding.
