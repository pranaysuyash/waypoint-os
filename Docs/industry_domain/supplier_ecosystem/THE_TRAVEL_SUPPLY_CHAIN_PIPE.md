# The Travel Supply Chain Pipe

## Overview
This document maps the end-to-end supply chain that enables a travel agency to source, price, book, and service travel products.

## Supply chain layers
- **Global suppliers**: airlines, hotel chains, tour operators, and insurance carriers
- **Distributors**: GDS, bedbanks, consolidators, and wholesalers
- **Agents and agencies**: front-office sellers, mid-office ticketing, and back-office settlement teams
- **Customer touchpoints**: travel portals, mobile apps, and direct agent communication

## Flow of value
- Supplier content flows into distribution platforms
- Distribution platforms normalize and aggregate content for agencies
- Agencies source products, build itineraries, and add service value
- Travelers receive a packaged trip enabled by agency expertise, support, and risk management

## Critical handoffs
- Product selection → pricing and quoting
- Booking confirmation → operations readiness
- Document verification → pre-trip clearance
- Disruption detection → recovery and communication
- Travel completion → feedback and loyalty

## Why this matters
A clear supply chain model helps agencies understand where friction exists, where technology should connect, and where service value is created or lost.
