# Tax Compliance, GST, and TCS for Travel Agencies

## Overview
Travel agencies operate at the intersection of service fees, supplier commissions, and regulated tax systems. This doc explains the common tax frameworks that affect travel agency pricing and accounting.

## Key concepts
- GST: Goods and Services Tax, applied to agency service fees and often to supplier markups
- TCS: Tax Collected at Source, a collection mechanism for certain outbound and international travel receipts
- Withholding requirements: regulatory obligations to retain tax on vendor or partner payments

## Practical impacts for agencies
- Pricing models must include tax-inclusive and tax-exclusive views
- Commission structures should account for GST on fees and TCS on international collections
- Booking systems need to keep audit-ready tax records for suppliers and customers

## Common agency practices
- Separate service fee from supplier cost in invoices
- Display GST rates clearly for corporate and leisure clients
- Avoid underwriting tax risk by matching ticketing and invoicing rules to local regulations

## Why this matters
Poor tax compliance creates financial risk, disrupts vendor relationships, and can invalidate customer invoices. Travel agents must treat tax policy as an operational input, not a backend afterthought.
