# Domain Knowledge: Financial Arbitration (ADM / ACM / Chargebacks)

**Category**: Advanced Financial  
**Focus**: The legal and financial "Battleground" between agencies, airlines, and banks.

---

## 1. ADM vs. ACM
- **ADM (Agency Debit Memo)**: The airline bills the agency (e.g., "You undercharged the tax by $10. Pay us now").
- **ACM (Agency Credit Memo)**: The airline owes the agency money (e.g., "We overcharged your BSP account. Here is a credit").
- **SOP**: The agency has **14–30 days** to "Dispute" an ADM via the **BSPlink** portal. If they don't dispute, the money is automatically taken from their bank account.

---

## 2. Chargeback Arbitration
- **Logic**: A traveler disputes a charge on their credit card (e.g., "I never took this trip" or "The flight was cancelled and I want my money back").
- **Agency Role**: The agency must provide the **"Evidence Package"**:
    - Signed T&Cs.
    - PNR history showing the traveler took the flight.
    - Emails showing the traveler was notified of the cancellation policy.

---

## 3. The "Involuntary" Refund Defense
- **Logic**: If an airline cancels a flight, the traveler is legally entitled to a refund. If the airline refuses and the traveler chargebacks, the bank will side with the traveler.
- **SOP**: The agency must fight the airline for the refund to "Cover" the chargeback.

---

## 4. Double-Jeopardy
- **Logic**: The traveler gets a refund from the airline AND a chargeback from the bank. The agency is out-of-pocket for double the amount.
- **SOP**: The agent must monitor the **"Chargeback Queue"** daily to prevent "Double Payouts."

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Massive" ADM**: An agency receives a $50,000 ADM because a junior agent used a "Corporate Net Fare" for 20 leisure passengers. The agency must prove "Administrative Error" to reduce the penalty.
- **Fraudulent Chargeback**: A traveler takes a $10,000 First Class trip and then claims "Fraud" on their card. The agent must produce the **"Check-in Manifest"** and the **"Passport Scan"** provided at booking.
- **Airline Bankruptcy Chargeback**: An airline goes bankrupt. 1,000 clients chargeback. The agency's merchant account is "Frozen" due to high risk. The agency must negotiate a "Reserve Fund" with the bank.
- **The "Lost" Dispute**: An agent misses the 14-day window to dispute a $500 ADM for a "Missing Tax." The agency must now "Pay" and can only try to recover it via a manual "Correspondence" with the airline.
