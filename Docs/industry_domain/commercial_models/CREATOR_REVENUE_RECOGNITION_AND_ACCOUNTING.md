# Creator Revenue Recognition and Accounting

This document explains accounting rules for creator monetization in travel apps.

## Overview

- Creator programs can involve complex revenue recognition because of referral fees, subscriptions, and co-developed products.
- Travel apps need clear accounting rules for creator-related revenue and cost attribution.
- Reliable accounting supports investor confidence, operating decisions, and creator payouts.

## Revenue Types

- Referral commissions: earned when traveler bookings close from creator referrals.
- Subscription revenue: recurring income from creator membership or travel club products.
- Creator share of product revenue: revenue splits tied to co-developed or branded experiences.
- Bonus and incentive adjustments: performance-based payouts that may affect revenue margins.

## Recognition Principles

- Timing: recognize revenue when travel services are delivered or when subscription obligations are fulfilled.
- Matching: align creator commission costs with the revenue they helped generate.
- Refund liability: account for anticipated returns, cancellations, and disputes tied to creator referrals.
- Deferred revenue: handle prepaid creator subscriptions and membership fees appropriately.

## Accounting Controls

- Track attribution: maintain robust data linking creator contributions to recognized revenue.
- Reserve management: hold back amounts for refunds and disputed creator commissions.
- Disclosure: report creator program economics clearly in financial statements and partner dashboards.
- Audit-ready records: keep documentation for creator contracts, payout schedules, and product performance.
