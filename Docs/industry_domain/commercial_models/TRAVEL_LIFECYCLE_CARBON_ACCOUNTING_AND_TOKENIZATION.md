# Travel Lifecycle Carbon Accounting and Tokenization

This document explores carbon accounting throughout the travel lifecycle and the tokenization of carbon impacts.

## Overview
- Travel lifecycle accounting measures emissions from booking to return.
- Tokenized carbon units provide traceable mitigation and consumer transparency.
- Accounting models cover transport, accommodation, activities, and ground mobility.

## Architecture
- Emissions data integration from suppliers, itineraries, and behaviors.
- Token layers that represent carbon offsets, avoidance, and reduction credits.
- Interoperability with loyalty, finance, and regulatory reporting systems.

## Operational Implications
- Supplier data collection for carbon intensity and travel footprint.
- Traveler-facing carbon summaries and offset product integration.
- Auditability and verification of carbon claims across the itinerary.

## Commercial Impact
- New products for carbon-aware travel purchase and offset augmentation.
- Loyalty incentives tied to lower carbon travel choices.
- Market differentiation with transparent carbon lifecycle reporting.

## Future Trends
- Carbon-backed travel tokens that settle in real time.
- Destination-level carbon passports and sustainable travel profiles.
- Embedded carbon accounting in travel planning and supplier sourcing.
