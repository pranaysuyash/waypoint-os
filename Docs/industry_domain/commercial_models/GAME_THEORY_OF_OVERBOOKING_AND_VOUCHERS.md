# Domain Knowledge: Game Theory of Overbooking & Vouchers

**Category**: Behavioral Engineering  
**Focus**: Calculating the "Optimal Acceptance" for Involuntary Denied Boarding (IDB).

---

## 1. Why Airlines Overbook
- **Logic**: No-show rates are predictable. Airlines sell 110% of capacity to maximize revenue.
- **The Gamble**: If everyone shows up, the airline must "Buy Back" seats from passengers.

---

## 2. The "Volunteer" Auction
- **Logic**: The airline starts low (e.g., $200 voucher) and increases until enough people move.
- **SOP**: The agent advises the client on the **"Voucher Floor"**. 
    - "Don't accept the $200. The law (EU261/US DOT) requires $600+ cash if they bump you. Wait for the airline to reach the cash-equivalent floor."

---

## 3. The Calculus of Delay
- **Formula**: `Voucher Value > (Value of Time + Cost of Missed Activity + Stress Factor)`.
- **SOP**: If the client has a $1,000/night hotel booked in the destination, a $500 voucher is a "Rational Loss." The agent must tell the client to **"Refuse and Board."**

---

## 4. "Hidden" Compensation
- **Logic**: Vouchers expire. Cash doesn't.
- **SOP**: Force the airline to provide a **"Statement of Involuntary Denial"** to trigger the travel insurance "Travel Delay" claim *in addition* to the airline voucher.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "High-Stakes" Bump**: A client is on their way to a wedding. The airline offers $1,500 to take a flight 6 hours later. The agent must calculate if the client will make the ceremony.
- **The "Voucher" Expiry Trap**: A client accepts a $2,000 voucher but finds it's only valid for 6 months on "Base Fare" only. The agent must "Negotiate" for a travel credit that covers "All Taxes and Fees."
- **Involuntary Denied Boarding (IDB)**: The airline "Forces" a client off the plane. The agent must immediately file the **"Legal Demand"** for the maximum statutory compensation (e.g., €600) before the client even leaves the airport.
- **The "Hotel" Double-Dip**: A client is bumped and given a hotel. The agent must ensure the client "Checks-in" to the hotel to trigger the **"Duty of Care"** obligation, while still pursuing the cash compensation.
