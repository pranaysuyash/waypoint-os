# Domain Knowledge: White-Label & B2B2C Partnerships

**Category**: Commercial Models  
**Focus**: Partner-led growth, API-first travel, and loyalty integrations.

---

## 1. The "Travel-as-a-Service" (TaaS) Model

Many non-travel brands (Banks, Lifestyle apps, HR platforms) want to offer travel to their users without becoming an IATA agency.

### The "White-Label" SOP
- **Logic**: The agency provides the tech and fulfillment, but the brand (e.g., "Amex Travel") is on the front end.
- **SOP**: The agency must maintain **Brand Voice Guidelines** for every partner interaction.

---

## 2. Loyalty Point Integrations (Burn & Earn)

### The "Points-to-Cash" Bridge
- **Logic**: Allowing a user to pay for a flight using "Bank Points."
- **SOP**: The agent must manage the "Real-time Valuation" of points vs. the GDS cash price.

---

## 3. The "Employee Benefit" Segment

- **Logic**: Companies offering "Travel Credits" as a bonus for employees.
- **SOP**: The agent must manage the "Corporate Subsidy" logic (e.g., "The company pays up to $1000, the employee pays the rest").

---

## 4. API-First Fulfillment

- **Logic**: A startup builds a "Beach Finder" app and needs the agency to handle the actual booking via API.
- **SOP**: The agency must provide a stable **Fulfillment API** that translates "App Actions" into "GDS Commands."

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Points Mismatch"**: A client tries to book a $2,000 trip using 100,000 points, but the "Points Engine" says they only have $1,500 value. The agent must explain the "Point Valuation" logic.
- **Brand Voice Failure**: An agent speaking on behalf of a "Luxury Bank" uses casual/slang language. The bank cancels the contract.
- **The "Subsidy" Error**: An employee books a trip that costs $1,200. The system accidentally bills the company for the full amount instead of just the $1,000 subsidy.
- **API Timeout**: A partner's app times out during a "Last-minute" flight booking. The flight sells out. The agent must find a way to "Force-book" the original price to save the partnership.
