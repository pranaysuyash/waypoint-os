# Domain Knowledge: Chargeback Protection & Fraud

**Category**: Commercial Models  
**Focus**: Credit card fraud, chargeback defense, and secure payments.

---

## 1. The "Travel Fraud" Landscape

Travel is a high-ticket, high-risk industry for "Friendly Fraud" and "Third-Party Fraud."

### Fraud Archetypes
- **The "First-Party" Chargeback**: The client goes on the trip, comes home, and disputes the charge with their bank saying "I didn't authorize this" or "The service was poor."
- **The "Stolen Card" Booking**: A scammer books a last-minute flight (e.g., London to Dubai) using a stolen card. By the time the bank detects it, the flight is gone.

---

## 2. Chargeback Defense SOP

### The "Golden Evidence"
- **Logic**: To win a dispute, you must prove the client authorized the transaction.
- **SOP**: Collect a **Signed Credit Card Authorization Form (CCAF)** for every booking.
- **Requirement**: The form must include a copy of the cardholder's ID and a clear description of the "Non-refundable" policy.

---

## 3. Fraud Detection (Red Flags)

### Indicators of Risk
- **Last-minute high-value bookings** (e.g., First class flight departing in 4 hours).
- **Inconsistent location**: Card is from Brazil, traveler is in Japan, booking is for a trip in Paris.
- **Email anomalies**: `johndoe1234567@gmail.com` with no prior history.

---

## 4. Secure Payment Technology

### 3D Secure (3DS)
- **Logic**: Forcing the user to enter a code from their banking app.
- **Benefit**: This "Shifts the Liability" from the agency to the bank.
- **SOP**: Use "3DS Mandatory" links for all new or high-risk clients.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Post-Trip Dispute"**: A client disputes a $20k safari charge, claiming "The guide was rude." The agent must assemble the "Evidence Package" (Signed contract, hotel check-in logs, email confirmations).
- **The "Scammer Trap"**: A new client wants to book 10 First-Class tickets to Lagos today. The agent must verify the identity using a "Video Call" or "ID-Verification" tool.
- **Authorization Error**: An agent processes a payment over the phone but forgets to send the electronic authorization form. The payment is disputed, and the agency loses the funds.
