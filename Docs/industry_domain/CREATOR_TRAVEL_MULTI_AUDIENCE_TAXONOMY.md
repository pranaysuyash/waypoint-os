# Creator Travel Multi-Audience Taxonomy

This document defines audience categories, content taxonomies, and product framing for creator travel programs.
It helps research, content, and product teams build consistent messaging for travelers, creators, vendors, agents, and partners.

## 1. Audience dimensions

- Travelers: consumers looking for itineraries, inspiration, convenience, safety, and creator-led experiences.
- Creators: talent, influencers, content producers, hosts, and brand partners who drive demand or curate travel.
- Vendors: suppliers, tour operators, hotels, venues, and local service providers who fulfill creator-enabled products.
- Agents: travel advisors, booking support teams, operations managers, and compliance specialists.
- Partners: distribution channels, affiliate networks, airports, lounge operators, and destination marketing organizations.
- Analysts and regulators: market analysts, policy teams, compliance reviewers, and legal stakeholders.

## 2. Taxonomy axes

- Intent type:
  - informational: guides, research, explainers
  - commercial: product pages, partner offers, booking entry
  - operational: checklists, playbooks, partner enablement
- Audience orientation:
  - user-facing: traveler advice, creator profile, destination guides
  - partner-facing: supplier onboarding, affiliate programs, lounge partnerships
  - agent-facing: compliance workflows, booking operations, risk mitigation
- Content form:
  - pages: landing pages, location pages, partner pages
  - assets: checklists, contract templates, playbooks, regulatory summaries
  - signals: keywords, snippets, internal links, CTAs
- Product layer:
  - experience: creator itineraries, layover bundles, event travel
  - commerce: affiliate offers, commissions, bundle pricing
  - compliance: visa rules, disclosure requirements, labor permits
  - platform: APIs, inventory models, partner integration guides

## 3. Page and doc archetypes

- Traveler content:
  - layover planning guides
  - destination safety and visa advice
  - creator-hosted experience descriptions
- Creator content:
  - creator product playbooks
  - monetization guidance
  - creator production and logistics checklists
- Vendor content:
  - creator-ready supplier onboarding
  - commission and payout models
  - local partner compliance requirements
- Agent content:
  - booking workflow templates
  - risk and permit checklists
  - cross-border coordination guides
- Partner content:
  - airport and lounge partner opportunity briefs
  - affiliate and promo program overviews
  - distributor enablement guides

## 4. Messaging and search intent mapping

- Traveler search intent:
  - “creator travel itinerary”
  - “layover travel ideas”
  - “creator travel visa rules”
- Creator search intent:
  - “how to monetize travel content”
  - “creator travel production logistics”
  - “creator travel partnership terms”
- Vendor search intent:
  - “creator-ready hotel programs”
  - “affiliate travel partner model”
  - “local supplier compliance for influencer shoots”
- Agent search intent:
  - “booking creator event travel”
  - “creator work permit checklist”
  - “travel agency creator program operations”

## 5. Product strategy alignment

- Design content and product experiences using the same taxonomy to avoid audience confusion.
- Surface the right persona at the right moment: traveler content for demand capture, creator content for partner education, agent content for operations.
- Use multi-intent clusters to link traveler and partner pages with regulatory and operational intelligence.

## 6. Research questions

- What audience segments are most likely to convert on creator-led travel offerings?
- Which taxonomy dimension should be central to creator travel landing pages: experience, compliance, or partnership?
- How can we align content clusters with both search intent and internal product motions?
- Where do agents, vendors, and creators need the most dedicated content versus shared reference pages?
