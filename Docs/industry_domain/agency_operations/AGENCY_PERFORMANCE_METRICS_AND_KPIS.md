# Agency Performance Metrics & KPIs

## Overview
This document defines the performance metrics that travel agencies track to evaluate operations, sales effectiveness, service quality, and financial health.

## Core KPI categories
### 1. Revenue and profitability
- **Gross margin**: total revenue minus supplier costs, before operating expenses.
- **Net margin**: profit after deducting operating costs and overhead.
- **Average transaction value**: total revenue divided by the number of bookings.

### 2. Operational efficiency
- **Bookings per agent**: number of completed bookings per agent per month.
- **Average handling time**: time spent from initial inquiry to confirmed booking.
- **Cycle time**: time from inquiry to ticket issuance or service confirmation.

### 3. Customer experience
- **Customer satisfaction (CSAT)**: survey score after each trip.
- **Net promoter score (NPS)**: likelihood that clients recommend the agency.
- **On-time response rate**: percent of inquiries responded to within SLA.

### 4. Risk and compliance
- **Documentation completeness rate**: percent of trips with passports, visas, and health documents verified before cut-off.
- **Disruption recovery time**: average time to resolve ticketing or itinerary disruptions.
- **Compliance audit pass rate**: percent of files that pass internal policy reviews.

## Measurement principles
- Define a clear owner for each KPI and a reporting cadence.
- Use automated dashboards wherever possible to avoid manual data entry.
- Segment metrics by service tier, client type, and product line to identify areas of improvement.
- Track leading indicators as well as lagging indicators (e.g., inquiry conversion rate vs. revenue growth).

## Common dashboard groupings
- **Sales dashboard**: revenue, pipeline, conversion rate, average deal size
- **Operations dashboard**: workload by agent, booking velocity, disruption volume
- **Quality dashboard**: CSAT, NPS, complaint rates, refund rates
- **Finance dashboard**: margin by product, cashflow status, supplier settlement variance

## Why this matters
Well-defined KPIs help agencies make fact-based decisions, align teams around shared goals, and identify when operational or commercial practices need adjustment.
