# Creator Product Launch Strategy and Feedback Cycle

This document explains launch strategy and continuous feedback for creator-led travel products.

## Overview

- Creator products often launch in waves tied to marketing moments, creator campaigns, and seasonal demand.
- A structured launch strategy helps the app coordinate support, inventory, pricing, and community momentum.
- Continuous feedback loops allow creator products to improve after initial release.

## Launch Strategy

- Go-to-market timing: align creator launches with demand windows, festivals, and platform promotions.
- Product readiness: validate supply, pricing, regulatory approvals, and creator content before release.
- Launch orchestration: coordinate creator communications, app discovery, and marketplace placement.
- Incentives: use early-bird pricing, limited access, or creator-led events to drive urgency.

## Feedback Cycle

- Traveler feedback: collect surveys, reviews, and experience ratings after bookings.
- Creator debriefs: gather creator insights on traveler expectations, venue performance, and operational gaps.
- Metrics review: analyze conversion, refund, and retention data for each launch.
- Iteration: adjust pricing, product descriptions, capacity, and creator support based on feedback.

## Product and Ops

- Feedback dashboards: give creators and product owners visibility into launch performance.
- Issue tracking: escalate frequent problems discovered during creator launches.
- Learning loops: apply launch learnings to future creator product development.
- Success criteria: build clear launch KPIs such as sell-through, satisfaction, and repeat bookings.
