# Creator Partner Portal and Self-Service Dashboards

This document describes the portal infrastructure required to onboard, manage, and scale creator partnerships for travel apps.

## Overview

- A creator portal is the operational backbone for creator relationships, performance tracking, and self-service collaboration.
- Travel apps need partner dashboards to reduce manual coordination, accelerate approvals, and keep creators aligned with brand, compliance, and product requirements.
- Portals are especially valuable for large creator networks, affiliate programs, and destination ambassador programs.

## Portal Capabilities

- Onboarding workflows: creator identity verification, contract acceptance, and credential submission.
- Content briefings: campaign guidelines, asset templates, and launch calendars.
- Performance dashboards: bookings, revenue, conversion rates, and traveler satisfaction metrics.
- Payment statements: commission calculations, payout schedules, and deduction explanations.
- Compliance controls: disclosure requirements, approved content formats, and local travel regulations.

## Self-Service Features

- Asset upload: creators submit stories, itineraries, photos, videos, and metadata directly.
- Campaign planning: creators can request campaign windows, destination focus, or product bundles.
- Feedback loops: creators receive reviews, issue flags, and optimization suggestions.
- Contract management: manage exclusive deals, non-compete clauses, and geo restrictions in one place.

## Operational Benefits

- Scale: reduce the burden on partnership managers as creator volume grows.
- Transparency: creators understand how performance is measured and why content is approved or rejected.
- Quality: automated checks enforce brand consistency before content goes live.
- Speed: quicker turnarounds from creator briefing to app publication.

## App Integration

- Embed portal signals into the app: verified creators, active campaigns, and creator recommendations.
- Use portal metadata to power in-app creator discovery and content personalization.
- Align creator portfolio decisions with product launch calendars and supplier inventory.

## Risks and Mitigations

- Data silos: synchronize portal data with the app and marketing systems to avoid stale creator status.
- Overpromise: clearly define supported product categories to prevent creators from promoting unavailable offerings.
- Privacy: protect creator PII and comply with local data protection rules.
- Governance: enforce portal policies consistently to avoid unfair creator disputes.
