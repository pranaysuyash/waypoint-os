# Travel Agency Technology and Operations

## Overview
Travel agencies rely on a mix of systems to manage inventory, bookings, customer data, supplier contracts, and operational workflows.

## Core technology categories
- Booking and distribution systems (GDS, bedbanks, direct supplier APIs)
- Customer relationship management (CRM)
- Operations management / itinerary management systems
- Document and compliance trackers
- Accounting, settlement, and commission reporting tools

## Operational integration priorities
- Ensure itinerary data is synchronized across booking and support platforms
- Maintain a single traveler profile that includes documentation status and preferences
- Link supplier terms, cancellation rules, and fare restrictions to quotation tools
- Automate notification and disruption workflows where possible

## Operational risks with technology
- Fragmented data across systems creates handoff and visibility gaps
- Manual updates increase the risk of mismatched itinerary and traveler details
- Supplier rule complexity can lead to incorrect pricing or invalid bookings
- Lack of audit trail reduces accountability in dispute resolution

## Best practices
- Define a canonical system of record for each workflow domain
- Use integration middleware or APIs rather than spreadsheets for operational handoffs
- Train teams on the workflows and the limits of each system
- Review technology performance and data quality regularly

## Why this matters
A modern agency operation must treat technology as a coordination engine: it should enable fast, consistent service delivery rather than simply store information.
