# Domain Knowledge: Agency Sizes & Specializations

**Category**: Agency Ecosystem  
**Focus**: Operational differences between Boutique, Mid-size, and Mega TMCs.

---

## 1. Boutique Agencies (The "High-Touch" Specialist)
- **Size**: 1–20 agents.
- **Model**: Focus on high-net-worth individuals (HNWIs) or specific niches (e.g., Ultra-luxury, Safari, Arctic).
- **Advantage**: Extreme personalization; "Black Book" access to hotel GMs.
- **Challenge**: Low volume means less leverage with airlines; higher service fees to maintain margins.

---

## 2. Mid-Size Regional Agencies
- **Size**: 20–200 agents.
- **Model**: Mix of corporate (SME) and high-end leisure. Often members of networks like *Virtuoso* or *Signature*.
- **Advantage**: Better tech than boutiques; enough volume to get "Preferred" overrides from airlines.
- **Challenge**: Managing the "Middle Ground" — not large enough for global contracts, not small enough for total personalization.

---

## 3. Mega TMCs (Travel Management Companies)
- **Examples**: American Express GBT, BCD Travel, CWT.
- **Size**: 1,000+ agents globally.
- **Model**: Purely volume-driven corporate travel.
- **Advantage**: Massive GDS leverage; proprietary tech stacks (e.g., Egencia); global "Follow-the-sun" support.
- **Challenge**: "Call Center" feel; rigid compliance; slow to adapt to niche traveler needs.

---

## 4. Areas of Service (Verticals)
- **Leisure**: Focused on holidays, bucket lists, and family.
- **Corporate**: Focused on efficiency, cost-savings, and "Duty of Care."
- **MICE**: Focused on volume, venue sourcing, and event logistics.
- **Humanitarian**: Specialized in NGO travel (often involving one-way tickets and "Missionary Fares").
- **VIP/VVIP**: Specialized in privacy, charters, and "impossible" requests.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Model Mismatch"**: A boutique agency tries to take on a 5,000-employee corporate account but collapses under the administrative ticketing volume.
- **The "Personalization Failure"**: A Mega TMC tries to book a "Unique Boutique Ryokan" but fails because the hotel doesn't accept the TMC's standard billing portal.
- **The "Leverage Play"**: A mid-size agency joins a "Host Agency" to gain the volume leverage of a Mega TMC while keeping its boutique service.
- **Niche Pivot**: A leisure agency wants to start a "Humanitarian Desk" but doesn't understand the "Missionary Fare" GDS rules.
