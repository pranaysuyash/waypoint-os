# Domain Knowledge: Connection Mechanics & Interlining

**Category**: Traveler Taxonomy  
**Focus**: How different airlines and transport modes connect.

---

## 1. Interlining (The "Safe" Connection)
- **Logic**: Two airlines (e.g., United and Lufthansa) have a legal agreement to "Talk" to each other.
- **Benefits**:
    - Baggage is checked through to the final destination.
    - If Leg 1 is delayed, Leg 2 *must* re-book the traveler for free.
- **SOP**: Always prioritize "Interline" PNRs for high-stakes travel.

---

## 2. Self-Transfer (The "Risky" Connection)
- **Logic**: The traveler books two separate tickets (e.g., Ryanair to London, then Virgin to NY).
- **Risks**:
    - Baggage must be collected and re-checked.
    - If Leg 1 is delayed, Leg 2 is a **"No-show"** with zero protection.
- **SOP**: The agent must warn the client in writing: "This is a Self-Transfer. We recommend 4+ hours between flights."

---

## 3. Virtual Interlining (The Tech Hybrid)
- **Logic**: Tech platforms (e.g., Kiwi.com) "Bundle" separate tickets and provide their own "Guarantee" if Leg 1 fails.
- **SOP**: The agent must understand who provides the "Guarantee" — the airline or the tech provider.

---

## 4. Multi-Modal Connections (Air-to-Rail)
- **Logic**: Airlines like Lufthansa (Rail&Fly) or Air France (TGV Air) sell train tickets as "Flight Segments."
- **SOP**: The traveler must "Check-in" for the train at the airport or station to activate the ticket.

---

## 5. MCT (Minimum Connection Time)
- **Logic**: The shortest time an airport allows for a transfer.
- **SOP**: Professional agents use "MCT + 30 mins" as a safety buffer.

---

## 6. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Separate Ticket" Disaster**: A client's first flight is delayed by 3 hours. Their second flight (on a separate ticket) departs. The client is stranded. The agent must find a way to use the "Self-Transfer Insurance" or negotiate a "Flat-tire" rate with the second airline.
- **Baggage "Through-Check" Failure**: An agent promises a client their bags will go "Through" to Bali, but because the airlines don't have an interline agreement, the bags are stuck in Singapore.
- **MCT Violation**: The GDS allows a 30-minute connection in Frankfurt. The client misses the flight. The agent must re-book the client at the agency's expense because they didn't override the MCT.
- **Rail-Air Sync**: A traveler misses their "Flight-Train" due to a delay. The agent must ensure the train ticket is "Re-validated" by the airline staff.
