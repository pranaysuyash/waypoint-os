# Client Onboarding and KYC for Travel Agencies

## Overview
This document describes how travel agencies onboard new clients, verify identity, and collect required documentation while maintaining trust and compliance.

## Onboarding stages
1. Initial inquiry and service discovery
2. Client qualification and risk assessment
3. Identity and payment verification
4. Proposal review and engagement agreement
5. Data collection and itinerary build

## KYC and identity verification
- Verify traveler identity for corporate, high-net-worth, and risk-sensitive bookings
- Capture and validate government-issued ID, passport details, and visa eligibility
- Use digital identity verification tools when available to reduce friction
- Maintain an audit trail of verification steps and approvals

## Data collection best practices
- Use structured intake forms for traveler details, preferences, and special requirements
- Clearly explain why sensitive data is requested and how it will be used
- Minimize data collection to only what is needed for booking and compliance
- Store documents in secure, encrypted systems instead of email or chat

## Payment and terms
- Confirm payment method and authorize prepayments or retainers before ticketing
- Align cancellation terms and refund policies with supplier rules and service fee agreements
- Use transparent engagement letters for premium or subscription-based clients

## Operational controls
- Assign a single onboarding owner who manages the client through the first booking
- Flag high-risk clients or unusual travel requests for senior review
- Use checklists to ensure passports, visas, insurance, and health requirements are captured

## Why this matters
Strong onboarding reduces errors, sets clear expectations, and protects the agency from fraud, chargebacks, and compliance breaches.
