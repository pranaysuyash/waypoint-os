# Domain Knowledge: The "Grey Areas" & ADM Avoidance

**Category**: Agency Operations  
**Focus**: The high-risk, "Grey Market" tactics and how to avoid the "Audit Wrath" of airlines.

---

## 1. Churning (The "Holding" Tactic)
- **Logic**: Booking a seat to "Hold it" for a client, then cancelling and re-booking it just before the "Auto-cancel" timer expires.
- **Risk**: Airlines track the "PNR History." If they see 10 "Book/Cancel" cycles for the same name, they send an **ADM** (Booking Fee).
- **SOP**: Limit churning to 2 cycles. Never churn a "Sold Out" flight.

---

## 2. Speculative / Fictitious Bookings
- **Logic**: Booking 10 seats under the name "TEST/TRAVELER" or "MOUSE/MICKEY" to artificially lower the airline's inventory.
- **Risk**: **Immediate OID (Office ID) Suspension.**
- **SOP**: Only use "Live Names" with valid contact data.

---

## 3. "Cross-Border" Ticketing (Grey Market)
- **Logic**: A ticket for LHR-JFK might be $2,000 if bought in the UK, but $1,200 if "Issued" from an office in Egypt (due to currency fluctuation or local market pricing).
- **Risk**: Airlines hate "Point of Sale" (POS) fraud. They may cancel the ticket or bill the agency for the "Home Market" price.
- **SOP**: Only issue in the "Home Market" unless the agency has a legal entity in the other country.

---

## 4. ADM (Agency Debit Memo) Avoidance
- **The "Audit"**: Airlines use automated bots to scan every PNR issued by every agency worldwide.
- **Common Triggers**:
    - Incorrect taxes.
    - Missing commission percentage.
    - Violating "Minimum Stay" rules.
- **SOP**: Use **"Auto-Pricing"** 99% of the time. Manual pricing requires "Second-Pair-of-Eyes" approval.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Churning" ADM**: An agency receives a $500 bill for "Excessive PNR Churning" on a flight to Tokyo. The agent must explain the cost to the owner and implement a "Stop-Churn" software rule.
- **Speculative Booking Panic**: An agent booked 20 seats for a group that "Might" happen. The airline Sales Manager calls to "Threaten OID Termination." The agent must "Release the space" immediately and apologize.
- **Cross-Border Audit**: An airline discovers that 50% of an agency's UK tickets are being issued in India. They demand $10,000 in restitution.
- **The "Tax Error" ADM**: An airline changes the "Airport Tax" in Paris by $2. The agency issues 1,000 tickets with the old tax. The agency is hit with a $2,000 ADM.
