# Domain Knowledge: Dopamine Cycle & Behavioral Economics

**Category**: Behavioral Engineering  
**Focus**: Engineering the psychological stages of travel to maximize lifetime value (LTV).

---

## 1. The "Anticipation" Phase (The Pre-Trip High)
- **Logic**: Neurologically, the anticipation of a trip often provides more dopamine than the trip itself.
- **Agency Role**: "Drip-feeding" information. Sending a teaser of the hotel view 30 days out, a physical gift 14 days out, and a personalized video 7 days out.
- **SOP**: Prevent "Post-Purchase Dissonance" (Regret) by validating the client's choice immediately after payment.

---

## 2. The "Peak-End" Rule
- **Logic**: People judge an experience based on how it felt at its peak (best moment) and at its end (last moment), rather than the average of the whole trip.
- **SOP**: 
    - **The Peak**: Ensure the most "WOW" activity happens exactly in the middle of the trip.
    - **The End**: Never let the last memory be a stressful airport transfer. Provide a "VIP Lounge" or a "Surprise Upgrade" for the final flight.

---

## 3. FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) as a Product
- **Logic**: Creating "Scarcity" (e.g., "Only 4 slots left for the 2027 Eclipse Cruise").
- **SOP**: Use "Exclusivity Bracketing." Offer a product that is "Invite Only" to trigger the client's desire for status.

---

## 4. The "Post-Vacation Blue" Mitigation
- **Logic**: The crash in dopamine upon return.
- **SOP**: The "Welcome Home" call. Instead of asking for a review immediately, send a "Photo book" or a "Local gift" from the destination to arrive 2 days after they get home. Use this moment to seed the *next* trip ("Where are we going next year?").

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Bland" Middle**: A 14-day trip feels like a "Slog" on day 8. The agent must inject a "Surprise Helicopter Tour" to create a new "Peak."
- **The "Stressful" Departure**: A client has a 4 AM flight. The taxi doesn't show up. This "End" will ruin the memory of the $20k trip. The agent must proactively book a "Luxury Airport Hotel" for the final night to ensure a "Relaxed End."
- **The "Scarcity" Fake-out**: A client discovers that a "Sold Out" tour is actually still available on another site. The agent must explain the "Curated Allocation" vs. "Mass Market" inventory.
- **The "Regret" Email**: A client sends an email saying "I spent too much money" 24 hours after booking. The agent must pivot to "Value Affirmation" (e.g., "This is the last time your children will be this age for a trip like this").
