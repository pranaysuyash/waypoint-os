# Domain Knowledge: Customer Excellence & VIP Protocols

**Category**: Agency Operations  
**Focus**: Escalation matrices, proactive service, and VIP "White-Glove" standards.

---

## 1. The Escalation Matrix

A clear hierarchy of "Who solves what" ensures speed and accountability.

### Level 1: Junior Agent (P3)
- **Scope**: Standard bookings, simple cancellations, and general inquiries.
- **SLA**: Respond within 2 hours.

### Level 2: Senior Agent / Lead (P1)
- **Scope**: Complex multi-city disruptions, group manifest errors, and mid-tier traveler complaints.
- **SLA**: Respond within 30 minutes.

### Level 3: Agency Owner / Crisis Manager (P2)
- **Scope**: High-value VIP failures, life-safety crises, legal disputes, and major financial errors (e.g., $10k+ loss).
- **SLA**: Immediate intervention.

---

## 2. The "Assistant Protocol" (VIP Routing)

For C-suite and HNWI (High Net Worth Individuals), communication rarely goes directly to the traveler.

### Dual-Routing Logic
- **Primary Contact**: Executive Assistant (EA). Receives the technical info (Invoices, PNR codes, Visa docs).
- **Secondary Contact**: The Traveler. Receives the "Lifestyle" info (Restaurant reservations, Weather briefings, Car pickup alerts).
- **SOP**: Never send a "Payment Request" directly to the VIP unless explicitly instructed.

---

## 3. Proactive Service Standards (The "Magic" Moments)

### The "4-Hour Check-in"
- **Logic**: Checking the status of the flight/hotel 4 hours *before* the traveler arrives.
- **System Action**: Verifying with the hotel that the room is "Ready" and the "Welcome Amenity" is in place.

### The "Disruption Pre-emption"
- **Logic**: If a storm is forecasted for the traveler's destination, start looking for alternative routes *before* the airline cancels the flight.
- **Action**: Presenting the traveler with an "Option B" before they even know they have a problem.

---

## 4. Conflict Resolution (The "Service Recovery" SOP)

### The "Acknowledge-Act-Amaze" Framework
1. **Acknowledge**: Respond to a complaint within 5 minutes, even if a solution isn't ready.
2. **Act**: Solve the immediate problem (e.g., book a new hotel).
3. **Amaze**: Provide a "Recovery Gift" (e.g., free Spa credit or a luxury car transfer for the next leg) to restore loyalty.

---

## 5. Operational Best Practices
- **Voice of the Customer (VoC)**: Conduct a "Post-Trip Debrief" call for every Luxury booking.
- **Log Everything**: Every complaint must be logged in the `AuditStore` to prevent "Contradiction Blindness" in future trips.
- **Emotional Intelligence**: Train agents to recognize "Traveler Stress" signals and adjust their tone accordingly.
