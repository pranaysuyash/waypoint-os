# Creator Content Performance Dashboards and Ops Runbooks

Creators and travel apps need a shared operational dashboard for content performance, campaign health, and troubleshooting runbooks. This document outlines the product, data, and operational processes required to keep creator-led travel programs accountable, predictable, and scalable.

## 1. Dashboard architecture

- Core metrics: engagement, conversion, booking velocity, itinerary value, repeat traveler retention.
- Audience signals: share rate, save rate, content reach, creator attribution, and referral momentum.
- Operational visibility: campaign health flags, policy issues, and supplier capacity stress.

## 2. Runbook design

- Incident classification: performance drops, booking anomalies, content compliance failures, fraud alerts.
- Response playbooks: root cause analysis templates, escalation contacts, content remove/replace procedures, and recovery messaging.
- Feedback loops: creator brief updates, supplier remediation, and product roadmap changes.

## 3. Collaboration model

- Shared ownership: creator teams, product operations, analytics, and quality assurance.
- Governance cadence: weekly performance reviews, post-campaign retrospectives, and continuous improvement cycles.
- Communication templates: status reports, risk summaries, and executive dashboards.

## 4. Data quality and trust

- Data lineage: source tracking for content signals, booking attribution models, and fraud detection alerts.
- Alerting: automated thresholds for outlier bookings, content moderation flags, and audience privacy risks.
- Audit readiness: versioned dashboards, historical audit logs, and compliance-ready reporting.
