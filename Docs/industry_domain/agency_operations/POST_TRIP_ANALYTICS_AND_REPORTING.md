# Domain Knowledge: Post-Trip Analytics & Reporting

**Category**: Agency Operations  
**Focus**: Corporate ROI, travel spend optimization, and compliance auditing.

---

## 1. The "Quarterly Business Review" (QBR)

For corporate clients, the agency's value is proven through data, not just bookings.

### Key Metrics
- **Total Spend vs. Budget**: Tracking "Leakage" (when employees book outside the agency).
- **Average Ticket Price (ATP)**: Benchmarking against industry averages for the same routes.
- **Advance Purchase Compliance**: % of tickets booked >14 days out (the "Savings Lever").

---

## 2. Savings Analytics

### Realized vs. Missed Savings
- **Logic**: If an employee chooses a $800 flight when a $600 flight was available (and compliant), that is **"Missed Savings."**
- **SOP**: Provide a monthly "Exception Report" to the client's Finance Director.

---

## 3. The "CO2 Footprint" Report

### The ESG Mandate
- **Logic**: Corporate travel managers must report total carbon emissions to the board.
- **SOP**: The agency must aggregate CO2 data across all flights, rail, and hotels, providing a "Sustainability Scorecard."

---

## 4. Operational "Leakage" Auditing

- **Hotel Attachment Rate**: % of flights that have a matching hotel booking. (Low attachment = high leakage/risk).
- **Unused Ticket Management**: Tracking non-refundable tickets that were cancelled but have "Future Travel Credit."
- **SOP**: Proactively apply "Unused Credits" to new bookings before they expire.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "QBR Crisis"**: A corporate client's travel spend has increased 30% YoY. The agent must analyze the data to find the cause (e.g., last-minute bookings or "Premium Economy" upgrades) and propose a solution.
- **The "Leakage" Audit**: A company discovers that 50% of their hotel stays are booked on personal cards. The agent must explain the "Duty of Care" risks and propose an "Incentive Program" for booking via the agency.
- **Sustainability Penalty**: A company is fined for under-reporting their carbon emissions. The agent must reconcile the "GDS Data" with the "Final Itinerary Data" to provide an accurate audit.
- **Unused Credit Expiry**: $50,000 in flight credits are expiring in 30 days. The agent must create a "Targeted Booking Campaign" to use these credits for upcoming team retreats.
