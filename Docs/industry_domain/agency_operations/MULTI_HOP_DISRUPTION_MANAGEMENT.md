# Multi-Hop Disruption Management

This document covers multi-hop disruption management for itineraries spanning air, rail, sea, and ground connections.

## Overview
- Multi-hop journeys require coordination across multiple operators and segments.
- Disruptions in one segment can cascade across the full itinerary.
- Effective management reduces traveler stress and preserves trip value.

## Operational Design
- Disruption detection and impact assessment for each itinerary hop.
- Automated rebooking strategies that account for connection risk.
- Cross-supplier communication and recovery orchestration.

## Decision Framework
- Prioritizing options based on traveler preference, cost, and service reliability.
- Balancing on-time vs. comfort, and minimizing additional transfers.
- Escalation rules for manual intervention when automation cannot resolve issues.

## Commercial Tradeoffs
- Compensation, protection products, and service-level guarantees.
- Supplier negotiation for alternate routing and priority inventory access.
- Pricing for disruption resilience and guarantee add-ons.

## Future Trends
- AI-driven itinerary resilience scoring and dynamic journey recovery.
- Integrated disruption insurance tied to multi-hop travel plans.
- Real-time traveler communication with adaptive itinerary updates.
