# Creator Co-Marketing and Destination Partner Programs

Travel creators often work with destinations, tourism boards, and supplier partners to co-market travel products. This document defines the structure, governance, and measurement of creator co-marketing programs in travel agency and app ecosystems.

## 1. Program structure

- Joint campaigns: align creator storytelling with destination promotions, supplier offers, and platform merchandizing.
- Sponsorship tiers: define levels of creator involvement, exposure, and financial support from destination partners.
- Shared branding: set co-branding rules, messaging approval flows, and visual identity guidelines.

## 2. Activation models

- Experience launches: pair creator content drops with limited-time travel packages or event-based itineraries.
- Destination ambassadorships: long-term partnerships where creators serve as destination representatives.
- Partner-driven bundles: package creator-led experiences with local destination partners, tours, and hospitality products.

## 3. Measurement and attribution

- Campaign metrics: bookings, media reach, partner uplift, and destination engagement.
- Partner ROI: measure contribution of creator activations to partner revenue, awareness, and destination demand.
- Transparency: share campaign performance with destination partners and creators through dashboards and retrospectives.

## 4. Governance

- Approval gates: destination partner review, compliance validation, and content quality checks.
- Risk sharing: define cancellation, liability, and reputation management responsibilities across creators and partners.
- Renewal and evolution: cycle partner programs based on performance, destination capacity, and creator fit.
