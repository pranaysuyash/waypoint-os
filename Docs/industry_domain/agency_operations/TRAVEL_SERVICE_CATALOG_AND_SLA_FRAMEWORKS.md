# Travel Service Catalog and SLA Frameworks

This document explores service catalogs and SLA frameworks for travel operations.

## Overview
- A travel service catalog defines offerings, fulfillment expectations, and service boundaries.
- SLA frameworks codify response times, issue resolution, and delivery quality.
- Well-defined service contracts support consistency across agents, partners, and clients.

## Technology Architecture
- Catalog management systems with service definitions, tiers, and referrals.
- SLA monitoring dashboards and automated escalation triggers.
- API contracts for service handoffs and partner performance telemetry.

## Operational Implications
- Alignment between sales promises, operations delivery, and traveler experience.
- Service tiering for standard, premium, and white-glove travel services.
- Root cause analysis when service commitments are breached.

## Commercial Impact
- Clear service packaging enables predictable pricing and premium upsells.
- SLAs improve accountability for disruption handling and traveler communications.
- Better partner orchestration through shared service definitions and scorecards.

## Future Trends
- Dynamic service catalogs that evolve with portfolio and traveler segmentation.
- Automated SLA remediation with smart contracts and real-time performance feeds.
- Experience-as-a-service offerings built on transparent delivery guarantees.
