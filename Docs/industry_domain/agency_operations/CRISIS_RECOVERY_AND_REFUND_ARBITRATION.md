# Domain Knowledge: Crisis Recovery & Refund Arbitration

**Category**: Agency Operations  
**Focus**: Mass cancellations, "Force Majeure," and refund recovery.

---

## 1. The "Force Majeure" Event

Events like pandemics, volcanic ash, or airspace closures trigger "Involuntary" cancellation rules.

### Airline Obligations (EU261 / DOT)
- **Logic**: If an airline cancels a flight (even due to weather), the traveler is usually entitled to a full refund (not just a voucher).
- **SOP**: The agent must fight "Auto-Voucher" systems to get "Cash Refunds" for clients.

---

## 2. Mass Re-accommodation SOP

When a major hub closes (e.g., LHR or DXB), thousands of PNRs go into "UN" (Cancelled) status.

### The "Queue" Management
- **Logic**: GDS queues fill up with error messages.
- **SOP**: 
    - **Prioritization**: Re-book "High Yield" (S1) and "Vulnerable" (Families/Elderly) passengers first.
    - **Alternate Hubs**: Instead of waiting for the hub to open, re-route via a secondary hub (e.g., re-routing a London flight via Paris/Rail).

---

## 3. Refund Arbitration (The "Chargeback Threat")

### Dealing with "Defaulting" Vendors
- **Logic**: If a hotel or small airline goes bankrupt (e.g., Thomas Cook), the agency is at risk.
- **SOP**: 
    - **GDS "Recall"**: Attempt to recall the funds via the BSP (Bank Settlement Plan) before the airline's accounts are frozen.
    - **Chargeback Support**: Providing the client with the "Letter of Non-Performance" to help them file a credit card chargeback.

---

## 4. Trip Interruption Insurance

- **Logic**: Many "Non-refundable" costs can be recovered via the agency's "Default Insurance" or the client's policy.
- **SOP**: The agent must act as the "Claims Assistant," providing all proof of payment and cancellation.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Cloud of Ash"**: A volcano erupts, closing European airspace for 7 days. The agent must manage 500 stranded passengers, 2000 refund requests, and constant airline policy changes.
- **Vendor Bankruptcy**: A major hotel chain goes into administration. The agent must find "Alternative Housing" for guests arriving tomorrow and recover the "Pre-paid" funds.
- **Refund Denial**: An airline refuses a refund, claiming the "Ticket was non-refundable," despite the flight being cancelled by the airline.
- **The "Voucher Trap"**: A client is pressured by an airline's app to accept a voucher. The agent must step in to demand a cash refund.
