# Creator Partnership Performance and Lifecycle

This document explains how travel apps manage creator partnerships from onboarding through renewal.

## Overview

- Creator partnerships are ongoing relationships that must be measured, nurtured, and optimized.
- A lifecycle view helps identify which creators should be promoted, supported, or retired.
- Lifecycle management keeps creator programs aligned with product capacity, demand, and brand strategy.

## Lifecycle Stages

- Recruitment: source creators aligned to product themes, destinations, and audience segments.
- Activation: onboard creators with briefs, portals, verification, and campaign templates.
- Performance: track conversion, booking value, refund rate, and traveler satisfaction.
- Growth: invest in high-performing creators with co-developed products, higher tiers, or exclusive access.
- Renewal: renew terms based on demonstrated value and rising opportunity.
- Exit: deprecate creators with declining performance, compliance risks, or misalignment.

## Performance Metrics

- Booking volume, revenue share, and average order value.
- Conversion lift vs baseline channels and retention of creator-sourced travelers.
- Refund/dispute rate and quality-adjusted creator contribution.
- Content velocity, campaign completion rate, and creator responsiveness.

## App Implications

- Use lifecycle signals in the app to surface creator partners with active, verified status.
- Prioritize creator-driven products during high-demand periods and new launches.
- Automate lifecycle actions where possible, but preserve human judgment for high-value creators.

## Operational Controls

- Regular reviews: set quarterly performance and compliance check-ins.
- Alignment: sync creator lifecycle decisions with supply planning and marketing calendars.
- Incentives: adapt commission and support levels as creators move through lifecycle stages.
- Risk management: watch for creators whose style or product claims drift away from brand standards.
