# Agent Handoff and Escalation Paths

## Overview
Effective travel agency operations require clear transitions between teams and escalation paths when issues arise. This document defines handoff criteria, ownership boundaries, and escalation triggers.

## Common handoff boundaries
- Inquiry → sales/consulting intake
- Proposal approval → booking fulfillment
- Booking fulfillment → pre-trip readiness review
- Pre-trip readiness → travel support / disruption response
- Post-trip follow-up → loyalty / account management

## Handoff best practices
- Share a single source of truth for traveler details and itinerary state
- Record reason for handoff, next expected action, and ownership
- Use standardized status labels and handoff tickets for accountability
- Keep customer-facing messaging consistent across teams

## Escalation triggers
- Supplier confirmation delay or booking failure
- Document or visa risk detected within a critical window
- Traveler health, safety, or security incident during travel
- High-value client request or VIP service interruption
- Billing dispute or contract exception requiring leadership review

## Escalation layers
1. Frontline agent / operations coordinator
2. Senior operations manager or team lead
3. Risk & compliance specialist
4. Executive sponsor or concierge director

## Why this matters
Structured handoff and escalation paths reduce dropped accountability, accelerate recovery, and ensure travelers receive the right level of expertise at each stage.
