# Domain Knowledge: Traveler Segmentation & Behavior

**Category**: Traveler Taxonomy  
**Focus**: Behavioral archetypes and their unique operational requirements.

---

## 1. The VFR Traveler (Visiting Friends & Relatives)
- **Logic**: Not a "Tourist." They stay in homes, but need high-baggage allowances and flexible dates.
- **SOP**: Prioritize airlines with high "Piece allowance" (e.g., Emirates, Ethiopian) over "Low-cost" carriers.

---

## 2. The "Bleisure" Traveler
- **Logic**: Combines a corporate trip with a weekend holiday.
- **SOP**: Requires **"Split Billing"**. The agent must bill the corporate leg to the company card and the weekend leg to the personal card, all within one PNR.

---

## 3. The Digital Nomad
- **Logic**: Long stays (30–90 days); highly dependent on "Wi-Fi Speed" and "Co-working" access.
- **SOP**: Verify the "Upload/Download" speeds of the hotel/apartment before booking. Prioritize "Monthly Rates" over daily.

---

## 4. The "Honeymooner" / Celebration Traveler
- **Logic**: High emotional stakes; zero tolerance for error.
- **SOP**: The **"Anniversary Alert"** in the GDS PNR. Ensure the hotel front desk has a "Special Amenity" (Champagne/Cake) ready at check-in.

---

## 5. The "Solo Female" Traveler
- **Logic**: Safety is the #1 priority.
- **SOP**:
    - **Room Allocation**: Never book a "Ground Floor" or "End of Hallway" room.
    - **Transport**: Pre-booked "Meet & Greet" transfers are mandatory; no public transport on arrival.

---

## 6. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Bleisure" Billing Mess**: A company accidentally pays for the employee's weekend at a luxury spa. The agent must "Reverse & Re-bill" to avoid a corporate audit.
- **Nomad Wi-Fi Crisis**: A digital nomad arrives at a villa in Bali to find the Wi-Fi is 2 Mbps. They have a "Board Meeting" in 4 hours. The agent must find a "Co-working Space" and a "Mobile Hotspot."
- **Honeymoon Disaster**: A couple arrives at their honeymoon suite to find "Two Twin Beds." The agent must negotiate an "Upgrade to King" or a move to a partner hotel.
- **Solo Safety Breach**: A hotel gives a solo female traveler a room with a broken door lock. The agent must move her immediately, even if the hotel is "Sold Out."
