# Domain Knowledge: Vendor Management SOP

**Category**: Agency Operations  
**Focus**: Net Fares, Override Commissions, and Supplier Performance Audits.

---

## 1. Supplier Categories

### Bed Banks (Hotel Aggregators)
- **Examples**: Hotelbeds, Expedia (EPS), WebBeds.
- **Role**: Aggregating thousands of hotels into a single XML feed.
- **Economics**: Agencies get "Net Rates" (lower than public) and add their own markup.

### Consolidation (Air Aggregators)
- **Examples**: TBO, Riya Travel, Akbar Online.
- **Role**: Buying "Block Seats" or having special "Consolidator Fares" with airlines.
- **Economics**: Commissions can range from 1% to 15% depending on the route.

---

## 2. Commercial Contracts

### Net Fares vs. Commissionable Fares
- **Net Fares**: You pay the supplier $100, sell to the client for $120. You keep the $20 (Markup).
- **Commissionable**: You sell for $100. The supplier pays you $10 (Commission) after the trip.
- **SOP**: Prioritize "Net Fares" for cash flow, and "Commissionable" for high-ticket luxury hotels.

### Override Commissions (The "Golden Handcuffs")
- **Logic**: If an agency sells $1M worth of "Emirates" tickets in a year, they get an extra 2% back as an "Override."
- **SOP**: The Agency Owner (P2) sets "Preferred Carriers" in the system to steer Junior Agents (P3) toward these targets.

---

## 3. Supplier Performance Audits

### The "Health" Check
- **Metrics**: 
    - **Inventory Reliability**: Does the hotel actually have the room when we book?
    - **Support Response**: How fast do they help during a crisis?
    - **Payment Terms**: Do they allow a 30-day credit cycle?

### FAM Trips (Familiarization)
- **Focus**: Suppliers invite agents to experience their product for free/low-cost.
- **SOP**: Agents must file a "Product Report" after every FAM trip to update the internal domain knowledge.

---

## 4. Operational Best Practices
- **Never rely on one supplier**: Maintain "Redundancy" for critical routes.
- **Audit your Invoices**: Suppliers often "Overcharge" or "Under-pay" commissions.
- **Check the Fine Print**: Pay attention to "Non-Refundable" terms and "Blackout Dates."
