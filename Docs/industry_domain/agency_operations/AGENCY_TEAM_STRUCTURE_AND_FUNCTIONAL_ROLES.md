# Agency Team Structure and Functional Roles

## Overview
This doc describes the core functional roles inside a travel agency and how teams are organized to deliver service, manage suppliers, and execute bookings.

## Core agency functions
- Sales and account management
- Operations and fulfillment
- Product and supplier sourcing
- Finance and pricing
- Compliance and risk
- Customer success and loyalty

## Key roles
### Travel consultant / advisor
- Qualifies traveler needs
- Proposes itinerary options
- Serves as the primary customer contact

### Operations coordinator
- Books transportation and lodging
- Manages itinerary logistics
- Coordinates supplier confirmations and changes

### Product manager / sourcing specialist
- Negotiates supplier contracts
- Curates inventory and package offers
- Maintains supplier relationships and quality standards

### Finance and pricing analyst
- Builds pricing models and service packages
- Tracks margin performance and revenue recognition
- Ensures tax, commission, and fee compliance

### Compliance and risk officer
- Maintains visa/documentation rules
- Monitors insurance and liability coverage
- Reviews high-risk travel and emergency protocols

### Customer success / post-trip care
- Manages traveler communications before, during, and after trips
- Collects feedback and handles service recovery
- Supports loyalty and repeat traveler programs

## Typical team structures
### Boutique agency
- Smaller teams with hybrid roles
- High-touch service, custom itineraries
- Few dedicated specialists, strong generalist capabilities

### Corporate travel agency
- Separate account managers, operations teams, and risk/compliance functions
- Standardized processes for corporate clients
- Strong integration with expense and policy systems

### Digital-first travel service
- Emphasis on product, automation, and self-service workflows
- Lean operations with specialized sourcing and supplier performance management
- Digital support for both consumers and business clients

## Why this matters
Understanding team structure helps align product design with real agency workflows. It also clarifies which internal roles need support from the travel agent system and where automation can deliver the most value.
