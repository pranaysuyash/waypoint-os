# Ground Transport and Local Logistics

## Overview
This document covers the agency's role in arranging ground transport, local transfers, and destination logistics.

## Typical ground transport services
- Airport chauffeur pickups and drop-offs
- Intercity coach, minivan, and rental-car coordination
- Local transfers for events, meetings, and group arrivals
- VIP cars, limousines, and executive shuttles

## Planning considerations
- Match vehicle type to traveler profile and itinerary length
- Account for local traffic, seasonal congestion, and city-specific regulations
- Verify supplier credentials and service quality
- Include clear buffer time for border crossings, ride-share waits, and immigration delays

## Operational workflows
- Create a ground transportation itinerary with pickup windows, driver contact, and backup plans
- Confirm vehicle details and passenger lists 24 hours before departure
- Monitor local conditions and reroute if needed
- Provide travelers with a single point of contact for all transfers

## Risk and contingency
- Use trusted local providers in unfamiliar destinations
- Build in alternatives for each leg (e.g., taxi, ride-sharing, hotel shuttle)
- Have a plan for late arrivals, vehicle breakdowns, or supplier no-shows
- Ensure travelers have emergency contact information, including local driver/dispatcher numbers

## Why this matters
Ground logistics are often the most visible touchpoint for travelers on arrival and departure days. Smooth transfers can make or break the traveler experience and support the agency's duty of care.
