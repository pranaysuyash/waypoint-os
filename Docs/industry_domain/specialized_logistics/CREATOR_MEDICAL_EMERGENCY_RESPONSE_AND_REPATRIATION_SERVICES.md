# Creator Medical Emergency Response and Repatriation Services

Creator-led travel experiences require a medical emergency framework that supports both travelers and on-site creator teams. This document defines the response, evacuation, and repatriation services needed for high-risk creator journeys.

## 1. Emergency response architecture

- Local medical partnerships: vetted clinics, ambulance networks, and telemedicine providers.
- Response coordination: real-time incident reporting, medical case managers, and emergency dispatch protocols.
- Creator crew support: production medical officers, risk managers, and on-site safety captains.

## 2. Repatriation and evacuation

- Evacuation triggers: medical necessity, political unrest, natural disaster, or mass event disruption.
- Repatriation pathways: direct medical flights, commercial travel with care escorts, and ground-to-air transfer coordination.
- Insurance orchestration: medical evacuation coverage, repatriation rider management, and carrier claims handling.

## 3. Service integration

- Experience continuity: alternate travel plans, production rescheduling, and travel credits for affected participants.
- Communication flow: traveler notifications, creator status updates, and stakeholder escalation.
- Documentation: medical release forms, liability waivers, and cross-border medical records transfer.

## 4. Operational resilience

- Drill readiness: emergency exercises, scenario planning, and local contingency drills.
- Reporting and post-mortem: incident review, supplier performance scoring, and process improvement.
- Duty of care: legal obligations, traveler assurance, and creator reputation protection.
