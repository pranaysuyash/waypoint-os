# Creator Production Logistics and Content Production Support

Creator travel productions require logistics support for content creation, crew, equipment, and on-location production needs. This document defines the operational requirements for creator production logistics in travel programs.

## 1. Production planning

- Scouting and scheduling: location access, shoot permits, travel windows, and creator availability.
- Crew coordination: local production teams, guide support, logistics managers, and content leads.
- Equipment logistics: transport, storage, power, insurance, and customs clearance for production gear.

## 2. On-location execution

- Set management: staging areas, talent movement, safety zones, and local community impact.
- Production support: catering, accommodation, transport, and communication for creator teams.
- Content workflows: shot lists, delivery timing, backup plans, and post-production handoff.

## 3. Risk and compliance

- Permit compliance: film permits, drone use, location access, and local authority approvals.
- Safety management: risk assessments, medical support, and emergency logistics.
- Insurance coverage: equipment insurance, production liability, and location-specific risk policies.

## 4. Integration with travel delivery

- Traveler experience alignment: ensure content production does not degrade traveler service.
- Supplier collaboration: coordinate with experience suppliers, destination partners, and guest-facing teams.
- Post-production reconciliation: wrap logistics, crew billing, and production costs within the travel program.
