# Domain Knowledge: On-Board Courier (OBC) Operations

**Category**: Elite & Specialized Verticals  
**Focus**: The highest-stakes logistics where a human carries critical cargo as "Hand Luggage" or "Checked Bags."

---

## 1. What is an OBC?
- **Logic**: When "Express Cargo" (FedEx/DHL) is too slow. Used for:
    - Prototype car parts for assembly lines (to prevent factory shutdown).
    - Life-saving medical samples/organs.
    - High-value jewelry or legal contracts.
- **Service**: A courier flies on the next available commercial flight, holding the item in their sight at all times.

---

## 2. Agency Operational SOP
- **The "Go-Now" Booking**: The agent must book a ticket *within minutes* of the request.
- **Visa-Ready Couriers**: The agency maintains a database of couriers with multiple passports and active visas (e.g., US B1/B2, China, India, Russia).
- **Customs Clearing**: The agent coordinates with "Customs Brokers" at both ends to ensure the item is cleared as "Passenger Baggage" (ATA Carnet).

---

## 3. Ticketing Logic (The "Excess Baggage" Master)
- **Logic**: The item is often 32kg+. The agent must "Pre-purchase" excess baggage and ensure the aircraft type (e.g., Embraer vs. 777) can actually hold the dimensions of the cargo.
- **SOP**: Use "Full Flex" tickets only. If the courier is stopped at customs, the return flight must be changeable.

---

## 4. Communication Protocol
- **Milestone Updates**: 
    1. Courier at Airport.
    2. Item Checked/Cleared.
    3. Courier Boarded.
    4. Wheels Up.
    5. Landed.
    6. Handover Complete.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Factory Shutdown" Save**: A German car factory will shut down in 12 hours if a specific chip doesn't arrive from Taiwan. The agent must find a courier *already at the airport* in Taipei.
- **Customs "Carnet" Refusal**: The courier is detained in Dubai because the ATA Carnet (Customs Document) is missing a stamp. The agent must wake up a customs broker at 3 AM local time.
- **The "Over-dimensional" Bag**: The OBC arrives at the gate with a prototype wing-tip. The gate agent says it won't fit in the overhead. The OBC must "Buy a Seat" for the cargo (CBBG - Extra Seat for Baggage).
- **Courier Fatigue**: A courier is on their 3rd back-to-back long-haul flight. The agent must detect "Fatigue Risk" and swap the courier at a hub (e.g., Doha) for a fresh one.
