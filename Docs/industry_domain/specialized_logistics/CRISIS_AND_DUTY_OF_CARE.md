# Domain Knowledge: Crisis Management & Duty of Care

**Category**: Specialized Logistics / Regulatory  
**Focus**: Life safety, emergency extraction, and legal accountability.

---

## 1. Defining "Duty of Care"

"Duty of Care" is the legal and moral obligation of a company (and its travel agency) to ensure the safety of its employees while traveling.

### The "Golden Rule"
- **Logic**: If you know a traveler is in a high-risk area, you must provide them with the tools and information to stay safe.
- **Liability**: Failure to provide a "Welfare Check" during a disaster can lead to massive lawsuits.

---

## 2. Crisis Archetypes

### Natural Disasters
- Earthquakes, Hurricanes, Tsunamis.
- **Action**: Immediate location tracking. Identify which travelers are in the "Red Zone."

### Political / Civil Unrest
- Coups, Riots, Terrorist attacks.
- **Action**: Shelter-in-place instructions followed by Secure Extraction.

### Medical Emergencies
- Outbreaks (e.g., COVID-26), individual MedEvac needs.
- **Action**: Coordinate with Medical assistance providers (e.g., SOS International).

---

## 3. The Extraction Hierarchy

### Level 1: Commercial Re-accommodation
- Booking the first available commercial flight out of the country.

### Level 2: Private Charter
- Chartering a private jet to a safe-haven country (e.g., moving travelers from Sudan to Dubai).

### Level 3: Tactical Extraction
- Using secure ground transport and private security to move travelers to an airport or border.

---

## 4. Communication SOP (The "Welfare Check")

### The "Red Alert" Broadcast
- **Logic**: Use the Mid-Office system to send an automated SMS/WhatsApp to all travelers in a specific GPS radius.
- **Requirement**: "Are you safe? Reply 1 for Yes, 2 for No/Need Help."

### The "Next of Kin" (NOK) Protocol
- **SOP**: Never contact the family until the situation is verified. Use the "Emergency Contact" info from Phase 1.

---

## 5. Duty of Care Tech Stack
- **ISOS / Healix / Crisis24**: Third-party providers that offer "Intelligence Briefings" and "Boots on the Ground" extraction.
- **GDS Tracking**: Using "PNR Passive Segments" to track hotel locations even if the hotel was booked externally.

---

## 6. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Missing" Traveler**: A coup occurs in a West African nation. All travelers are accounted for except one S1 VIP who isn't answering their phone.
- **MedEvac Coordination**: A traveler has a heart attack in a remote part of Mongolia. The agent must coordinate a helicopter to Ulaanbaatar and then a medical jet to Singapore.
- **Insurance Denial**: An insurance provider refuses to cover an extraction because the country was already on the "Red List" before the trip started.
