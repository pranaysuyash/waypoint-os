# Space Debris and Launch Scrub Insurance

This document maps the specialized risk and logistics framework for orbital launch operations, with a focus on insurance, cancellation protocols, and debris liability.

## Overview
- Unique risk profile of launch and reentry operations.
- Launch scrub costs versus debris avoidance costs.
- Regulatory responsibility across launch providers, insurers, and spaceport operators.

## Key Concepts
- Launch scrub: intentional delay of a launch due to weather, range safety, or technical issues.
- Debris liability: ecosystem impact of spent stages, orbital fragments, and ground debris.
- Mission abort coverage, third-party liability, and customer indemnity.

## Insurance Structures
- Pre-launch abort policies.
- Payload loss versus launch delay compensation.
- Multi-party coverage for launch providers, payload owners, and ground support.

## Operational and Contractual Implications
- Scheduling windows and standby provisioning.
- Refund triggers for passenger and cargo commitments.
- Coordination with regulators, range safety offices, and launch site operators.

## Risks and Mitigations
- Weather holdbacks and scrub thresholds.
- Space debris avoidance obligations.
- Insurance exclusions for orbital pollution, force majeure, and third-party damage.

## Future Trends
- Commercial passenger launch insurance.
- Data-driven scrub decision modeling.
- Cross-border regulatory harmonization for orbital risk.
