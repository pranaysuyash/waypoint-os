# Domain Knowledge: Wine & Gastronomy Travel

**Category**: Specialized Logistics  
**Focus**: Culinary tourism, Michelin-starred logistics, and wine transport.

---

## 1. The "Hard-to-Get" Reservation SOP

For gastronomy travelers, the "Meal" *is* the trip.

### Reservation "Hacking"
- **Logic**: Top restaurants (e.g., Noma, El Celler de Can Roca) release tables months in advance and sell out in seconds.
- **SOP**:
    - **Concierge API Integration**: Using platforms like *SevenRooms* or *Resy* to monitor cancellations.
    - **Amex/Centurion Relationship**: Leveraging credit card concierge desks who have "Block Tables" at top sites.

---

## 2. Wine Logistics & Shipping

### The "Excess Liquid" Problem
- **Logic**: Travelers buying cases of wine in Tuscany or Bordeaux cannot take them on the plane easily.
- **SOP**: Coordinate with specialized **International Wine Shippers** (e.g., Mail Boxes Etc. or specialized local carriers) who handle customs and temperature-controlled shipping.

---

## 3. Culinary Itinerary Design

### The "Palate Fatigue" SOP
- **Logic**: Booking three 15-course tasting menus in three days leads to "Palate Fatigue."
- **SOP**: Balance "High Gastronomy" with "Authentic Local Street Food" or "Market Tours" to provide a diverse experience.

---

## 4. Seasonal & Harvest Cycles

- **Truffle Season**: October–December (Alba, Italy).
- **Grape Harvest (Vendange)**: September–October (France/Spain).
- **SOP**: Itineraries must be synced with the **Agricultural Calendar** of the region.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Cancelled Star"**: A Michelin-starred restaurant cancels a reservation due to a private event. The agent must find an "Equivalent Culinary Experience" in the same city within 2 hours.
- **The "Corked Cargo"**: $5,000 worth of wine is shipped home but arrives "Heat-damaged" because the shipper didn't use a refrigerated container. The agent must manage the insurance claim.
- **The "Allergy Crisis"**: A traveler with a "Severe Nut Allergy" is booked at a high-end Thai restaurant. The agent must verify the kitchen's "Cross-contamination SOP" in the local language.
- **The "Harvest Mismatch"**: A client travels to Bordeaux for the harvest, but due to climate change, the harvest was moved 2 weeks earlier. The agent must adjust the entire regional itinerary.
