# Domain Knowledge: High-Value Asset Transport (Art & Instruments)

**Category**: Ultra-Luxury & Frontier  
**Focus**: Moving multi-million dollar assets that require their own seats and climate control.

---

## 1. Musical Instruments (The Stradivarius Rule)
- **Logic**: Fine instruments (Cellos, Violins, Harps) cannot be checked into the hold due to temperature fluctuations and physical risk.
- **CBBG (Cabin Baggage)**: The agent must book an **"Extra Seat"** for the instrument.
- **GDS Coding**: `SSR CBBG` must be entered with the weight and dimensions.
- **SOP**: The instrument must be booked in a "Window Seat" (to prevent blocking egress) and cannot be in an "Emergency Exit Row."

---

## 2. Fine Art (Escorted Courier)
- **Logic**: Moving a painting or sculpture.
- **SOP**:
    - **Climate-Controlled Cases**: The agent coordinates with specialized "Art Shippers" (e.g., Crozier, Masterpiece).
    - **Tarmac Supervision**: The agent (or a hired courier) must be present on the tarmac to watch the crate be loaded into the aircraft.
    - **Insurance**: Requires a "Binder" for the specific duration of the transit.

---

## 3. The "CITES" Requirement
- **Logic**: Many old instruments (and some art) contain ivory, tortoiseshell, or rare rosewood. These are protected under **CITES**.
- **SOP**: The agent must ensure the traveler has the **"CITES Passport"** for the instrument. Without it, customs can seize and destroy the instrument at the border.

---

## 4. White-Glove Handover
- **Logic**: The transition from the airport to the museum/gallery/concert hall.
- **SOP**: Pre-cleared "High-Security" transport with two-man teams.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Broken Strad" Nightmare**: A gate agent insists a violin must be checked into the hold. The agent must intervene with the "Airline Station Manager" to cite the FAA/EASA "Musical Instrument Policy."
- **The "Ivory Seizure"**: A musician's 18th-century cello is seized in Germany because of an ivory tip on the bow. The agent must coordinate with "CITES Authorities" to prove the age and legality.
- **Art "Off-load"**: A crate containing a $10M painting is "Off-loaded" due to aircraft weight issues. The agent must ensure the crate is moved to a "Secure Climate Vault" at the airport, not a standard warehouse.
- **The "Harpsichord" Logistics**: Moving a harpsichord for a tour. It won't fit in a standard van. The agent must source a "Tail-lift truck with humidity control."
