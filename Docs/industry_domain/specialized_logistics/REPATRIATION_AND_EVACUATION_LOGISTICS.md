# Domain Knowledge: Repatriation & Evacuation Logistics

**Category**: Elite & Specialized Verticals  
**Focus**: The logistics of moving large groups of people out of crisis zones (War, Pandemic, Natural Disaster).

---

## 1. Repatriation vs. Evacuation
- **Repatriation**: Returning citizens to their home country (e.g., during COVID-19 or post-conflict). Often government-funded.
- **Evacuation**: Urgent removal from a "Point of Impact" to a "Safe Zone." Often private or insurance-funded.

---

## 2. Charter "Air Bridges"
- **Logic**: Commercial airlines stop flying. The agency must "Charter" aircraft to create an "Air Bridge" between the crisis zone and a nearby hub (e.g., Kabul to Dubai).
- **SOP**: Coordinate with the **"Ground Handler"** at the origin to ensure the plane can land, refuel, and depart without being "Impounded."

---

## 3. The "Passenger Manifest" (Emergency)
- **Logic**: In a crisis, standard GDS rules fail. The agent must use "Manual Spreadsheet Manifests" and coordinate with the local **"Embassy Liaison."**
- **SOP**: Prioritize "Vulnerable Persons" (Children, Elderly, Injured) based on the **"Priority List"** provided by the client/government.

---

## 4. Landing Permits & War Risk Insurance
- **Landing Permits**: Requires "Overflight Permits" from multiple countries.
- **Insurance**: The agency must verify that the charter operator has **"Hull War Risk Insurance"** to fly into a "Hot Zone."

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Cancelled Air Bridge"**: A charter plane is 1 hour from landing in a conflict zone when the "Landing Permit" is revoked. The agent must find a "Diversion Airport" and secure ground transport for 200 people to reach that airport.
- **Manifest Confusion**: 5 people arrive at the plane who are "Not on the list" but claim to be family of a VIP. The agent must make a "Live Decision" based on **"Seat Availability vs. Safety Protocol."**
- **Medical Evac Priority**: One passenger on a repatriation flight has a heart attack. The plane must divert. The agent must coordinate "Medical Ground Support" at the diversion point.
- **The "Overflight" Denial**: A country refuses to let a repatriation flight cross its airspace. The agent must find a "Reroute" which adds 4 hours of flight time and requires a "Refueling Stop."
