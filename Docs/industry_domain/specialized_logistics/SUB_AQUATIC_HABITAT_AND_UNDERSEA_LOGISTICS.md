# Domain Knowledge: Sub-Aquatic Habitat & Undersea Logistics

**Category**: Frontier & Future Logistics  
**Focus**: Managing travel to pressurized underwater habitats and research stations.

---

## 1. Pressure Management & "Saturation" Travel
- **Logic**: For habitats at depth (e.g., Undersea hotels or deep-sea labs), the traveler may need to undergo "Decompression" protocols.
- **SOP**: The agent must coordinate with a **"Medical Dive Officer"** to ensure the traveler's post-stay flight is scheduled at least 24–48 hours after surfacing to prevent "The Bends."

---

## 2. Life Support Supply Chains (O2 & Waste)
- **Logic**: Unlike a hotel on land, an undersea habitat requires constant oxygen, CO2 scrubbing, and pressurized waste removal.
- **SOP**: The agent must verify the **"Life Support Redundancy"** of the supplier. If the O2 system fails, what is the "Bail-out" time for the client?

---

## 3. Emergency "Surface" Protocols
- **Logic**: In the event of a hull breach or fire, surfacing can be fatal due to pressure changes.
- **SOP**: Secure a **"Hyperbaric Chamber"** on standby at the surface. Coordination with a "Deep Sea Rescue" team (SAR).

---

## 4. Visual Logistics & Light Pollution
- **Logic**: Maintaining the view (the main selling point) requires external cleaning robots and specific light-spectrum management to avoid disturbing local marine life.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Bends" Flight**: A client insists on flying 4 hours after surfacing from a 3-day underwater stay. The agent must "Cancel" the flight and explain the **"Fatal Risk"** of decompression sickness.
- **Hull Breach Evacuation**: A small leak is detected in the "Observation Dome." The agent must coordinate the **"Slow Decompression"** evacuation of the client.
- **O2 Supply Chain Cut**: A storm prevents the "Oxygen Supply Ship" from reaching the habitat. The agent must calculate the "Reserve Time" and decide whether to initiate an emergency surface.
- **The "Pressure" Panic**: A client develops "Claustrophobia" at 20 meters depth. The agent must coordinate a "Sedated Extraction" with the on-site medic.
