# Autonomous Ground Robotics and Last-Mile Service

This document explores autonomous ground robotics for last-mile delivery, passenger assistance, and property service in travel ecosystems.

## Overview
- Ground robotics in airports, hotels, resorts, and urban transport hubs.
- Last-mile delivery of bags, amenities, food, and concierge services.
- Operational models for autonomous vehicles and service robots.

## Service Categories
- Hotel delivery robots and contactless in-room provisioning.
- Airport autonomous tugs, porter bots, and baggage shuttles.
- Urban micro-mobility and on-demand curbside service robots.

## Operational Implications
- Fleet orchestration, charging, safety, and collision avoidance.
- Integration with property management systems and airport operations.
- Maintenance and fallback procedures when robotics services degrade.

## Supplier Impacts
- Vendor selection criteria for robotics-as-a-service partners.
- Service-level definitions and connectivity requirements.
- Cost and ROI for hotels, carriers, and ground operators.

## Future Direction
- Shared robotics platforms across hotels and ground handlers.
- Seamless handoffs between human and autonomous service agents.
- Data-driven optimization for new service categories and guest experiences.
