# Domain Knowledge: Academic & Research Logistics

**Category**: Specialized Logistics  
**Focus**: University travel, field research expeditions, and grant-compliant booking.

---

## 1. The "Academic" Travel Profile

Academic travel is often budget-constrained but logistically complex (long stays, remote locations, and multi-national researchers).

### Grant Compliance (Fly America Act, etc.)
- **Logic**: If the trip is funded by a US government grant, the traveler *must* use a US flag carrier (even if more expensive).
- **SOP**: The agent must verify the "Funding Source" before booking flights to ensure compliance with donor rules (e.g., NIH, NSF, EU Horizon).

---

## 2. Field Research & remote Logistics

### The "Off-Grid" SOP
- **Logic**: Researchers going to the Amazon or Antarctica need more than a hotel.
- **SOP**:
    - **Satellite Comms**: Coordination for Sat-phone rentals.
    - **Medical Clearances**: Verifying the traveler has the necessary vaccinations/medications for remote field sites.
    - **Ground Support**: Booking 4x4 vehicles, river boats, or local "fixers."

---

## 3. Student Group Travel

### Safety & Duty of Care
- **Logic**: Universities have extreme "Liability" for student safety.
- **SOP**:
    - **"Buddy System" Tracking**: Ensuring the GDS PNR includes emergency contacts for every student.
    - **Alcohol-Free Housing**: Requesting specific "Dry" floors or hotels for undergraduate groups.

---

## 4. Academic Rates & "Open-Jaw"

- **Academic Fares**: Special discounted fares (with flexible changes) available only to students/faculty.
- **Open-Jaw**: Booking "A to B" and returning "C to A" (common for researchers moving through a region).

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Compliance Failure"**: A researcher books a non-US carrier on a federal grant. The university refuses to reimburse. The agent must "Re-issue" or find a "Code-share" solution.
- **Remote Medical Emergency**: A researcher in a remote desert site gets a snake bite. The agent must trigger the "MedEvac" protocol and coordinate with the local ground team.
- **Student Group Disruption**: A student loses their passport in a foreign city. The agent must coordinate with the embassy and re-book the student's return flight separately from the group.
- **Grant Deadline Rush**: A department needs to "spend" their travel budget before the fiscal year ends. The agent must process 50 "Pre-paid" flight vouchers.
