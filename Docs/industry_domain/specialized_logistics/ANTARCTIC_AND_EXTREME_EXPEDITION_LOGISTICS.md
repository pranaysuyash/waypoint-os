# Domain Knowledge: Antarctic & Extreme Expedition Logistics

**Category**: Niche Global Logistics  
**Focus**: The management of travel to regions where no commercial infrastructure exists.

---

## 1. IAATO Regulations
- **Logic**: The **International Association of Antarctica Tour Operators (IAATO)** governs almost all civilian travel to the continent.
- **SOP**: The agent must verify the operator's IAATO membership and ensure the traveler has signed the **"Antarctic Treaty"** compliance forms.

---

## 2. Permit Management
- **Logic**: Travel to the South Pole or deep interior (e.g., Union Glacier) requires a **"National Permit"** from the traveler's home government (e.g., US Department of State / British Antarctic Survey).
- **SOP**: The agent must manage the application 6–12 months in advance.

---

## 3. The "MedEvac" Reality
- **Logic**: There are no hospitals in Antarctica. A medical evacuation can cost $200,000+.
- **SOP**: The agent must verify a specialized **"Search & Rescue" (SAR)** insurance policy that specifically covers "Antarctica" and includes "Ship-to-Air" extraction.

---

## 4. Ice-Class Vessel & Polar Flight Logistics
- **Vessels**: Coordinating the connection between a commercial flight to Ushuaia/Punta Arenas and an "Ice-Class" expedition ship.
- **Flights**: Managing the manifest for the **"Ilyushin-76"** or **"Basler BT-67"** ski-planes that fly to the interior.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Ushuaia" Delay**: A traveler's commercial flight to Ushuaia is delayed. The expedition ship (which only sails once every 14 days) is departing in 2 hours. The agent must coordinate a "High-Speed Private Transfer" or negotiate a "Pilot Delay" with the ship.
- **Medical Rejection**: A traveler fails the "Extreme Expedition Medical" 30 days before departure. The agent must manage the $20,000 "Non-refundable" loss via the travel insurance "Cancel for Any Reason" clause.
- **The "South Pole" Permit Denial**: A traveler is denied a permit by the State Department due to "Environmental Impact" concerns. The agent must find an "Alternate Itinerary" (e.g., Arctic/Svalbard) that doesn't require the same permit.
- **Polar Wind-Out**: A flight from Punta Arenas to the ice is delayed for 10 days due to "Katabatic Winds." The agent must manage the hospitality and psychological "Fatigue" of the traveler stuck in a hotel.
