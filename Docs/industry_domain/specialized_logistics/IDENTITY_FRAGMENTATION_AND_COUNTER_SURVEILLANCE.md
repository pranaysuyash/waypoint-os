# Domain Knowledge: Identity Fragmentation & Counter-Surveillance

**Category**: Advanced Security & Ethical Systems  
**Focus**: Protecting the movements and digital footprint of high-threat/VVIP travelers.

---

## 1. Identity Fragmentation
- **Logic**: Never book the entire trip under one name.
- **The Split**: 
    - Flight booked as "Real Name" (Mandatory for TSA/Passport).
    - Hotel booked as "Alias / Corporate Name."
    - Ground transport booked by a "Third Party" with no names.
- **SOP**: The agent must "Stitch" these fragmented records manually, as standard GDS "Integrated" PNRs are too easy to track.

---

## 2. Digital "Burner" Protocols
- **Logic**: Preventing "Device Seizure" or "Malware Injection" at border crossings.
- **SOP**: 
    - **Hardware**: Client travels with a $200 laptop and $100 phone (wiped clean).
    - **Data**: All data is stored in an encrypted cloud, accessed only *after* passing the border.
    - **SIMs**: Using "Travel eSIMs" that are discarded after each city.

---

## 3. Physical Counter-Surveillance
- **The "Bumper" Car**: Coordination with a security firm to provide a second car that "Follows" the client to detect tailing.
- **The "Decoy" Departure**: Booking two flights at the same time. The client takes one, the "Decoy" (Staff member) takes the other.

---

## 4. "Grey" Travel
- **Logic**: Traveling through "Secondary" borders to avoid high-scrutiny hubs (e.g., flying into a small regional airport vs. a major international hub).

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Data" Seizure**: A client's phone is seized at a border. Because of the "Burner Protocol," there is no sensitive data on the device. The agent must coordinate the "Remote Wipe" of the device just in case.
- **The "Identified" Alias**: A hotel clerk recognizes a VVIP despite the "Alias" booking and asks for a selfie. The agent must immediately "Relocate" the client to a different "Dark" property.
- **Decoy Success**: A paparazzi follows the "Decoy" to the airport, allowing the VVIP to depart via a private FBO unnoticed.
- **Secondary Border Failure**: A "Secondary" border is closed suddenly. The agent must find an "Alternative Crossing" that maintains the client's "Invisible" status.
