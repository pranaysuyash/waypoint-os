# Domain Knowledge: Charter Brokerage (ACMI & Empty Legs)

**Category**: Advanced Operations  
**Focus**: The high-margin business of leasing aircraft and selling "Ferry" flights.

---

## 1. ACMI (Wet vs. Dry Lease)
- **Wet Lease (ACMI)**: **A**ircraft, **C**rew, **M**aintenance, **I**nsurance. The operator provides everything.
- **Dry Lease**: The client rents just the "Metal" (The plane) and provides their own crew/insurance.
- **Damp Lease**: Aircraft, Maintenance, Insurance — but the client provides their own Cabin Crew (common for airlines).
- **Agency Role**: The broker (agent) finds the right lease for a specific mission (e.g., a music tour or a sport team's season).

---

## 2. The "Empty Leg" (The Arbitrage)
- **Logic**: A jet flies from London to Ibiza to pick up a client. The flight *to* Ibiza was empty (The "Ferry").
- **Monetization**: Brokers sell these "Empty Legs" at a 50–75% discount.
- **Risk**: If the primary client cancels their Ibiza trip, the "Empty Leg" is cancelled. The agent must warn the traveler.

---

## 3. Positioning Fees
- **Logic**: If the requested aircraft is in Paris but the client is in London, the client pays a "Positioning Fee."
- **SOP**: The agent searches for "Floating Fleets" (Jets already in the air) to reduce these fees for the client.

---

## 4. Slot & Permit Management
- **Logic**: A jet cannot land without a "Slot" (Time) and a "Permit."
- **SOP**: The agent coordinates with **"Handling Agents" (FBOs)** to secure slots at busy airports (e.g., Davos during WEF).

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Cancelled" Empty Leg**: A client booked a $5k empty leg on a Global 6000. 4 hours before flight, the primary client cancels. The traveler is stuck. The agent must find a commercial alternative or a "Discounted Charter" from a nearby airport.
- **Wet Lease Crew Strike**: The crew on a Wet Lease aircraft goes on strike. The agent must find a "Replacement Crew" from a different operator under the same ACMI contract.
- **Davos Slot Crisis**: A client wants to fly into St. Moritz for Davos. No slots are available. The agent must find a "Drop & Go" slot (Land, drop passenger, take off) and park the plane in a different city (e.g., Milan).
- **The "Technical" Swap**: A jet has a mechanical failure. The operator has no spare. The broker must find a "Sub-charter" from a competitor and negotiate a "Price Match" to protect the client's experience.
