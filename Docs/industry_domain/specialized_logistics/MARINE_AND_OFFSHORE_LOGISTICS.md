# Domain Knowledge: Marine & Offshore Logistics

**Category**: Elite & Specialized Verticals  
**Focus**: Moving ship crews and oil rig workers globally under "Seaman Fare" regulations.

---

## 1. The "Seaman Fare" (SC)
- **Logic**: Specialized airfares (e.g., Cat 35) exclusively for merchant marines, cruise crews, and offshore workers.
- **Benefits**: 
    - 40kg+ baggage allowance (mandatory for life at sea).
    - Fully refundable and changeable without penalty (critical because ships are often delayed by weather/port congestion).
    - One-way tickets are priced at 50% of return (unlike standard retail).
- **SOP**: The traveler must carry a **"Seaman's Book"** (SID) and a **"Letter of Guarantee"** from the shipping company. Failure to present these at check-in results in "Off-loading" or a massive fare difference bill.

---

## 2. Port Agent Coordination
- **Logic**: The travel agent does not just book the flight; they coordinate with the **"Port Agent"** in cities like Singapore, Rotterdam, or Houston.
- **SOP**: The travel agent sends the flight details to the Port Agent, who arranges the "Launch Boat" or "Transfer Van" to take the crew member directly to the vessel.

---

## 3. Remote Offshore Shuttles
- **Logic**: For oil rigs (e.g., North Sea, Gulf of Mexico), the travel agent manages the connection between a commercial flight and a **"Private Helicopter Shuttle."**
- **SOP**: Requires strict "Weight & Balance" data for the helicopter manifest and verification of "HUET" (Helicopter Underwater Escape Training) certification for the traveler.

---

## 4. The "Joining & Leaving" Vessel Logic
- **Joining**: High stress. If the crew member misses the flight, the ship sails without them.
- **Leaving**: Variable. The ship might be delayed by 2 days due to a storm. The agent must "Hold" multiple flight options.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Missing SID" Crisis**: A crew member arrives at Manila airport without their Seaman's Book. The airline refuses to honor the $400 Seaman Fare and demands $3,000 for a walk-up retail ticket.
- **Vessel Diversion**: A ship is diverted from Singapore to Port Klang due to a port strike. 50 crew members need their flights changed in 4 hours.
- **The "Weight Overload"**: A helicopter shuttle is grounded because the crew members brought too much "Personal Gear." The agent must coordinate a "Sea-Freight" for the excess gear while getting the humans on the flight.
- **Offshore Medical Evac**: A worker is injured on a rig. The agent must coordinate a "MedEvac" flight that bypasses standard immigration (Emergency Shore Leave).
