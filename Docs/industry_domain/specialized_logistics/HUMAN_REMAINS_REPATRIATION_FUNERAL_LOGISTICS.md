# Domain Knowledge: Human Remains Repatriation (Funeral Logistics)

**Category**: Niche Global Logistics  
**Focus**: The highly regulated transport of deceased persons across international borders.

---

## 1. GDS Coding: HUM
- **Logic**: The traveler is codified as **"HUM"** (Human Remains) in the GDS.
- **SOP**: The agent must book a specialized "Cargo Space" on a commercial aircraft. Not all aircraft can carry HUM (e.g., small regional jets).

---

## 2. The "Zinc Coffin" Requirement
- **Logic**: For aviation safety and hygiene, international repatriation requires a **"Hermetically Sealed Zinc-lined Coffin"** inside a standard wooden casket.
- **SOP**: The agent coordinates with the local "Funeral Director" to verify the "Sealing Certificate."

---

## 3. Consular & Legal Paperwork
- **Death Certificate**: Must be apostilled.
- **Embalming Certificate**: Mandatory for almost all international flights.
- **Consular Permit**: The embassy of the destination country must issue a **"Permit to Import Human Remains."**
- **Non-Infectious Disease Letter**: A letter from the local health authority.

---

## 4. Ash Repatriation (Cremains)
- **Logic**: Carrying ashes is simpler but still requires a "Certificate of Cremation."
- **SOP**: Ashes must be in a non-metallic urn (to pass through X-ray) and can often be carried as "Cabin Baggage."

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Baggage Hold" Rejection**: An airline refuses to load a HUM because the flight is "Heavy" on fuel. The agent must fight for "Priority Status" or find an alternative route within 12 hours.
- **Consular Strike**: A traveler has passed away in Italy, and the consulate is on strike. The "Import Permit" cannot be issued. The agent must find a "Legal Workaround" via a neighboring consulate or a "Temporary Burial" protocol.
- **Ash Seizure**: A traveler's ashes are seized at security because the urn is made of lead. The agent must find a local funeral home to "Re-pot" the ashes into a plastic container.
- **The "Corpse" Manifest Error**: A name on the death certificate is misspelled by one letter. The airline refuses to issue the "Air Waybill." The agent must get an "Affidavit of Identity" from a local lawyer.
