# Global Travel Risk Matrix

## Overview
This document defines the major risk categories travel agencies use to assess destinations and itineraries.

## Risk dimensions
- **Geopolitical risk**: protests, conflict, government stability
- **Health risk**: disease outbreaks, medical infrastructure, vaccine requirements
- **Environmental risk**: climate events, natural disasters, extreme weather
- **Operational risk**: airline strikes, supplier insolvency, airport closures
- **Social risk**: civil unrest, terrorism, crime, safety perceptions

## Risk matrix structure
- **Low risk**: stable destination with strong infrastructure and predictable travel procedures
- **Moderate risk**: managed risk with clear mitigation steps and contingency options
- **High risk**: elevated likelihood of disruption or emergency response needs
- **Extreme risk**: travel is often discouraged or restricted by insurers and governments

## Assessment criteria
- Local entry restrictions and advisories
- Availability of medical and security support
- Supplier reliability and historical disruption patterns
- Traveler profile and vulnerability (e.g., age, health, purpose)

## Agency use cases
- Corporate program approval for travel to elevated-risk destinations
- Custom sourcing for clients requiring enhanced duty-of-care services
- Dynamic travel advisories linked to booking and pre-departure workflows
- Insurance policy selection and claims preparation

## Why this matters
A consistent risk matrix helps agencies make better operational decisions, tailor briefings, and deploy the right support for every trip.
