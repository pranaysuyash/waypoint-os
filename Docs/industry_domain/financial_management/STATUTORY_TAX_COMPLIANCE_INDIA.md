# Domain Knowledge: Statutory Tax Compliance (India)

**Category**: Financial Management  
**Focus**: LRS, TCS, and GST compliance for travel agencies in India.

---

## 1. Liberalized Remittance Scheme (LRS)

Indian residents are allowed to remit up to **$250,000** per financial year for various purposes, including travel.

### Agency Responsibility
- **PAN Verification**: The agency must collect and verify the PAN card of the traveler (and each family member for large groups).
- **Limit Monitoring**: The agency must check if the traveler has already utilized their $250k limit with other authorized dealers/agencies.
- **Reporting**: All international remittances must be reported to the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) via the authorized bank.

---

## 2. Tax Collected at Source (TCS)

As of October 2023, the Indian government has strict TCS rules for "Overseas Tour Program Packages."

### TCS Rate Structure
- **Up to ₹7 Lakhs**: 5% TCS (only if for education/medical, otherwise 20% in some cases).
- **Above ₹7 Lakhs**: 20% TCS for all overseas tour packages.
- **Logic**: The ₹7 Lakh threshold is *cumulative* across all expenses for the traveler in the financial year.

### Operational SOP
1. **Extraction**: Identify if the trip is "International."
2. **Calculation**: Apply the 20% surcharge on the total package value (excluding GST).
3. **Disclosure**: Clearly show TCS as a separate line item in the invoice.
4. **Remittance**: The agency must remit this tax to the government and issue a "Form 27EQ" certificate to the traveler.

---

## 3. Goods and Services Tax (GST)

### SAC Codes (Service Accounting Code)
- **9964**: Passenger transport services.
- **9967**: Supporting and auxiliary transport services.

### GST on Air Tickets
- **Domestic (Economy)**: 5% (with ITC on input services only).
- **Domestic (Business)**: 12%.
- **International**: 5% (Economy) / 12% (Business).

### GST on Hotel/Tour Packages
- **Standard**: 5% without Input Tax Credit (ITC) OR 18% with ITC. Most agencies opt for 5% to keep prices competitive.

---

## 4. Operational Best Practices
- **Vetting**: Always verify the traveler's "Tax Status" (Resident vs. Non-Resident) early in the funnel.
- **Automation**: Use a "Statutory Calculator" in the booking flow to avoid manual errors.
- **Audit**: Maintain a 10-year record of all PAN cards and TCS certificates issued.
