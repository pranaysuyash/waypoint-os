# BSP and IATA Settlement Architecture

This document explores BSP and IATA settlement systems and their relationship to travel finance.

## Overview
- The Billing and Settlement Plan (BSP) is the airline industry clearinghouse for travel agents.
- IATA settlement processes define how ticketing, commissions, and refunds flow through the system.
- Understanding this architecture is essential for agency cash flow and compliance.

## Technology Architecture
- BSP reporting, validation, and invoice reconciliation.
- Ticketing data flows, fare calculation artifacts, and refund authorization.
- Interfaces with agent accounting, corporate billing, and multi-currency settlement.

## Operational Implications
- Monthly BSP cycles, payment guarantees, and rectification deadlines.
- Audit controls for ticket stock, agency IDs, and settlement accuracy.
- Exception handling for refunds, voids, and BSP disputes.

## Commercial Impact
- Working capital risk from BSP payment deadlines.
- Commission timing, invoice matching, and dispute resolution costs.
- Agency reputation and airline relationships in settlement performance.

## Future Trends
- Digital BSP workflows, API-based IATA settlement, and near-real-time clearing.
- Settlement resiliency through cross-border liquidity and alternative payment rails.
- Greater transparency into carrier payment status and commission flows.
