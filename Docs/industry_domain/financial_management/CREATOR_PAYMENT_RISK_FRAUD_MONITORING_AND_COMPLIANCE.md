# Creator Payment Risk, Fraud Monitoring, and Compliance

This document explains how travel apps manage payment risk and fraud for creator commerce and creator-backed travel bookings.

## Overview

- Creator commerce introduces new payment risks across digital goods, creator payouts, and cross-border transactions.
- Fraud monitoring must cover infection points from influencer promotions, fake bookings, and payment arbitrage.
- Compliance extends to AML, KYC, and creator tax obligations when payees are individuals or small businesses.

## Risk Domains

- Payment fraud: fake bookings, chargebacks, stolen cards, and promotional abuse.
- Creator payout risk: misclassified contractors, non-compliant cross-border settlements, and incorrect tax withholding.
- Affiliate misuse: creators gaming attribution, cookie stuffing, or false influencer referrals.
- Digital asset payments: ensuring transparency, proof of purchase, and platform dispute resolution.

## Monitoring Controls

- Transaction scoring: use behavioral signals, creator campaign context, and payment metadata.
- Payout validation: automated checks for creator identity, tax details, and bank account legitimacy.
- Alerting: trigger reviews for unusual creator-related payment flows or rapid commission spikes.
- Dispute management: centralize disputes for creator bookings, marketplace sales, and creator payout corrections.

## Compliance Design

- Apply AML/KYC controls to creators when required by jurisdiction or payout volume.
- Document creator compensation terms clearly in platform agreements.
- Keep audit trails for creator-related transactions and promotional incentives.
- Align risk controls with platform trust signals to maintain traveler confidence.
