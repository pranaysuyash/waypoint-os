# Creator Advance Funding and Promotion Finance

This document explains advance funding and promotional finance for creator travel programs.

## Overview

- Creator campaigns often require upfront investment in inventory, marketing, and product development.
- Advance funding helps creators and suppliers plan launch-ready offerings while preserving cash flow.
- Promotion finance governs how discounting, co-op spend, and creator incentives are funded and reconciled.

## Advance Funding

- Prepayment: fund supplier deposits, creator event costs, and venue guarantees ahead of launch.
- Inventory reserve funding: support creator-exclusive inventory holds with financial backing.
- Creator onboarding finance: invest in creator enablement, content production, and launch support.
- Risk sharing: define how unbooked inventory and campaign shortfalls are handled financially.

## Promotion Finance

- Co-op budgets: allocate platform and supplier funds for creator campaigns and promotional incentives.
- Campaign reconciliation: track actual spend against approved promotion budgets.
- Discount liability: manage coupon and discount costs as part of creator campaign finance.
- Financial controls: approve creator promotion spend based on forecasted return and margin impact.

## Product and Ops

- Clear funding rules: make it easy for product, finance, and creator teams to request and approve funding.
- Visibility: show creators how funding is used and how campaign performance affects future support.
- Outcome alignment: tie funding to goals like sell-through, retention, and incremental revenue.
- Audit readiness: maintain records of advance payments, co-op support, and sponsored creator promotions.
