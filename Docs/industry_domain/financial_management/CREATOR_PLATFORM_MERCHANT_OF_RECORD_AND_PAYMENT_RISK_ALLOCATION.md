# Creator Platform Merchant of Record and Payment Risk Allocation

Travel creator commerce often operates through a platform merchant of record. This document explains how payment risk is allocated across creator programs, platforms, and supplier flows.

## 1. Merchant of record models

- Platform-as-MOR: the app controls checkout, payment processing, and settlement.
- Creator-assisted commerce: creators drive demand, but the platform remains the payment principal.
- Marketplace split: creator-sponsored inventory flows through separate merchant accounts or partner acquirers.

## 2. Payment risk allocation

- Chargeback and fraud liability: who bears disputed bookings, fraudulent travel claims, and refund demand.
- Settlement timing: prepaid holdback, deferred payout, and reconciliation against cancellations.
- Currency risk: multi-currency settlements, FX exposure, and conversion agreements.

## 3. Compliance and controls

- Payments compliance: PCI, KYC, AML, sanctions screening, and travel-specific payment regulation.
- Escrow overlays: holdback controls for high-risk creator-led bookings and pre-event deposits.
- Auditability: payment trail transparency, reconciliation dashboards, and operational escalations.

## 4. Product integration

- Creator payout pipelines: routing payouts through creator wallets, direct deposit, or pooled settlement accounts.
- Risk-based pricing: adjusting commission or service fees based on payment risk and booking profile.
- Customer experience: clear payment disclosure, refund policy notice, and traveler support handoffs.
