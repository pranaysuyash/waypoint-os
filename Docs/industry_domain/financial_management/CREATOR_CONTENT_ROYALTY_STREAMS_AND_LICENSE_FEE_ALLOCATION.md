# Creator Content Royalty Streams and License Fee Allocation

Creator intellectual property is a commercial asset that needs royalty governance and licensing fee allocation. This document explains how travel platforms structure creator content royalties, licensing terms, and the financial flows behind re-use of creator assets.

## 1. Royalty streams

- Direct content royalties: creator payouts for usage of photos, videos, guides, and branded itineraries.
- Secondary usage fees: re-licenses for marketplace reuse, partner syndication, and cross-platform distribution.
- Performance royalties: paid based on bookings, engagement, or revenue generated by creator content.

## 2. License fee design

- Territory and channel scope: define where content can be used and for how long.
- Usage rights: earned, exclusive, non-exclusive, and sublicensable licenses.
- Fee structures: flat license fees, percentage-based licenses, and usage-based micropayments.

## 3. Financial allocation

- Revenue attribution: linking content use to the right creator payout streams.
- Cost reporting: capturing royalty expense, license amortization, and content production recoveries.
- Embedded licensing: bundling license fees into packages, marketplace inventory, or subscription offerings.

## 4. Governance

- Contract guardrails: approval workflows for reuse, renewal terms, and audit rights.
- Transparency: creator statements, royalty dashboards, and settled license reports.
- Compliance: copyright management, moral rights, and destination-specific content laws.
