# Creator Escrow and Financial Risk Sharing

This document explains escrow mechanisms and financial risk sharing for creator travel programs.

## Overview

- Creator programs often involve prepayments, tickets, and supplier guarantees that require escrow or risk-sharing frameworks.
- Escrow protects travelers and creators by holding funds until delivery conditions are met.
- Risk sharing aligns incentives across platform, creator, and supplier partners.

## Escrow Models

- Traveler funds escrow: hold booking payments until key milestones such as visa approval, ticket issuance, or event confirmation.
- Supplier escrow: reserve payments to suppliers until service delivery or quality checks are completed.
- Creator payout escrow: defer creator commissions until traveler satisfaction or post-trip completion.
- Escrow triggers: define release conditions, refunds, and dispute-handling rules.

## Risk Sharing

- Shared liability: allocate responsibility for cancellations, no-shows, and event disruptions across partners.
- Financial buffers: maintain reserves for creator promotion refunds or operational failures.
- Insurance integration: use insurance products to transfer specific creator risk exposures.
- Communication: make risk-sharing terms visible to creators and travelers.
