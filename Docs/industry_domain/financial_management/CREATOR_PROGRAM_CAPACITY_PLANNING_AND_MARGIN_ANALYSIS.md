# Creator Program Capacity Planning and Margin Analysis

This document explains how travel apps plan capacity and analyze margins for creator programs.

## Overview

- Creator programs require capacity planning to ensure supply can support creator-driven demand.
- Margin analysis reveals which creators, products, and campaigns are financially sustainable.
- Effective planning balances creator growth with operational and supplier constraints.

## Capacity Planning

- Demand signals: use forecasted bookings, campaign schedules, and creator reach to model needs.
- Supplier capacity: evaluate partner inventory, staffing, and load limits.
- Buffer planning: reserve capacity for high-value creator campaigns while preserving flexibility.
- Campaign coordination: align creator launches with seasonal and supplier capacity cycles.

## Margin Metrics

- Gross margin: revenue minus direct creator payouts, supplier costs, and fulfillment expenses.
- Contribution margin: the incremental value of creator programs after variable costs.
- Program ROI: compare creator payout expense against lifetime traveler value and retention.
- Cost per lead/booking: measure efficiency across creator channels.

## Product and Operational Insights

- Prioritize creator products with strong margins and reliable capacity.
- Use margin analysis to decide which creator segments deserve premium support.
- Align pricing, commissions, and financing with margin targets.
- Build dashboards that show capacity risk, margin health, and trend signals.
