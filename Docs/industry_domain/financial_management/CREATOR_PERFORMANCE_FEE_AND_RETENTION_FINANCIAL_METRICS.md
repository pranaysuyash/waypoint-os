# Creator Performance Fee and Retention Financial Metrics

This document explains financial metrics for creator performance fees and retention-focused creator programs.

## Overview

- Creator compensation should be tied to measurable performance and long-term traveler value.
- Retention metrics are critical because creator travel products often depend on repeat engagement.
- Financial discipline helps keep creator programs profitable and scalable.

## Performance Fee Metrics

- Revenue per booking: measure how much creator bookings contribute to gross revenue.
- Gross margin: subtract creator payout, supplier costs, and fulfillment from creator booking revenue.
- Commission efficiency: compare creator fees against conversion lift and incremental revenue.
- Payment timing: manage cash flow for creator payouts, chargebacks, and reconciliations.

## Retention Metrics

- Repeat booking rate: track how many travelers book again after a creator experience.
- Customer lifetime value: estimate the future value of creator-acquired travelers.
- Churn reduction: measure whether creator programs improve loyalty and decrease attrition.
- Retention-driven ROI: evaluate creator incentives based on long-term revenue rather than one-time bookings.

## Product and Finance

- Incentive alignment: structure creator compensation to reward retention and quality, not just volume.
- Reporting: create dashboards that show both short-term campaign performance and long-term financial impact.
- Budgeting: allocate creator funding based on predicted retention lift and performance margins.
- Risk control: monitor for creator programs that produce low retention but high promotional spend.
