# Launch Strategy

**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Purpose**: How do you go from hidden to visible?

---

## Launch Philosophy

> "You don't launch once. You launch in stages."

Each stage serves a purpose:
1. Validate product works
2. Validate people want it
3. Prove people will pay
4. Scale what works

---

## Stage 0: Friends & Family (Now)

**Goal**: First users, brutal feedback

**Who**:
- 3-5 agencies you know personally
- Or: Agencies from discovery calls who were enthusiastic

**Offer**:
- Free access forever
- Weekly call with you
- Direct input on features

**Success criteria**:
- They use it for at least 3 real trips
- They say "I'd pay for this" or give specific feedback

**Duration**: 2-4 weeks

---

## Stage 1: Design Partners (Next 1-2 months)

**Goal**: Committed feedback loop, product-market fit signals

**Who**:
- 5-10 agencies from discovery/friends
- Committed to weekly usage + feedback

**Offer**:
- Free for 6 months
- Lifetime discount after launch
- Direct influence on roadmap
- Priority support

**Success criteria**:
- 50%+ use it weekly after month 1
- At least 2 say "I'd be sad if this went away"
- Clear retention > 40% at week 4

**Duration**: 2-3 months

---

## Stage 2: Beta Launch (Months 3-4)

**Goal**: Prove strangers will use it

**Who**:
- 20-50 agencies
- Application-based (you can say no)
- Mix of warm leads + some cold

**Offer**:
- 50% off for first year
- 14-day trial, then pay
- Normal support (email)

**Success criteria**:
- 30%+ activation (use it in first week)
- 20%+ convert to paid after trial
- Week 4 retention > 30%

**Where to announce**:
- Travel agent Facebook groups
- LinkedIn (your network + cold outreach)
- Indie Hackers (for developer audience)

**Duration**: 1-2 months

---

## Stage 3: Public Launch (Months 5-6, if metrics look good)

**Goal**: Scale acquisition, get PR, build momentum

**Who**:
- Anyone who signs up
- Self-service onboarding

**Offer**:
- Full pricing
- 14-day trial (credit card required or not — your call)
- Normal support

**Launch channels**:
1. **Product Hunt** (best for builder audience)
2. **Indie Hackers** (already have presence)
3. **Hacker News** (if tech angle is strong)
4. **Travel agent forums** (your actual customers)
5. **Twitter/X** (build in public audience)
6. **Medium/Dev.to** (launch story)

**Launch assets needed**:
- Landing page (clear value prop)
- Demo video (2-3 minutes)
- Screenshots (3-5)
- Testimonials from beta users
- Press release (optional, for travel tech publications)

**Success criteria**:
- 100+ signups in launch week
- 20+ conversions to paid in first month
- Positive PH/IH reception

---

## Stage 4: Growth (Months 6+)

**Goal**: Optimize acquisition, scale what works

**What changes**:
- Paid acquisition (if LTV:CAC > 3)
- Content marketing (SEO, blog)
- Partnership outreach (host agencies)
- Referral program

**Success criteria**:
- 20-30% month-over-month growth
- Sustainable CAC
- Healthy retention

---

## Launch Assets Checklist

### Before Any Launch

- [ ] Core feature works reliably
- [ ] Onboarding is clear (< 5 min to first success)
- [ ] Major bugs fixed
- [ ] Basic analytics installed
- [ ] Error tracking (Sentry) set up
- [ ] Support email working

### Before Public Launch

- [ ] Landing page with clear value prop
- [ ] Pricing page
- [ ] Demo video (2-3 min)
- [ ] 3-5 testimonials from beta users
- [ ] FAQ page
- [ ] Terms of service
- [ ] Privacy policy
- [ ] Launch announcement copy (for multiple platforms)

---

## Launch Day Timeline (for Public Launch)

### Week Before

- [ ] Practice demo video (record 3 times, use best)
- [ ] Test onboarding flow yourself
- [ ] Prepare social media posts
- [ ] Email beta users: "We're launching, want to give you early access code"
- [ ] Set up Product Hunt listing (scheduled)

### Day Before

- [ ] Final site check (links work, no typos)
- [ ] Prepare for traffic spike (scaling config)
- [ ] Rest (you'll be busy tomorrow)

### Launch Day

**Morning**:
- [ ] Product Hunt goes live (12:01am PST)
- [ ] Post on Twitter, LinkedIn, Indie Hackers
- [ ] Email beta users + ask for upvotes
- [ ] Monitor for bugs/issues

**Throughout day**:
- [ ] Respond to every PH comment
- [ ] Respond to social media mentions
- [ ] Fix any critical bugs immediately
- [ ] Be on support (WhatsApp/email)

**Evening**:
- [ ] Thank everyone who participated
- [ ] Document lessons learned
- [ ] Sleep

### Day After

- [ ] Follow up with signups
- [ ] Reach out to media/press if launch went well
- [ ] Plan post-launch content

## Product Hunt Strategy

### Why Product Hunt?

- Your initial audience might not be travel agents
- But: Other builders, potential partners, press follow PH
- Good PH launch → social proof → easier to sell to actual customers

### PH Tips

| Tip | Why |
|-----|-----|
| **Launch Tuesday-Thursday** | Higher engagement |
| **Great thumbnail** | First impression matters |
| **Demo video** | People prefer watching over reading |
| **Hunter** | Find someone with audience to "hunt" your product |
| **Engage with comments** | Boosts ranking |
| **Share with community first** | Ask for upvotes (not spammy) |

### PH Description Template

```
Headline: AI assistant for travel agents

Short description: Turn messy client messages into organized trip options

Long description:
Travel agents waste hours extracting intent from WhatsApp
messages, checking feasibility, and drafting options.

Agency OS does the heavy lifting:
- Extracts constraints from freeform messages
- Flags contradictions and blockers
- Generates 2-3 ranked options with rationale
- Keeps the human agent in control

Built for travel agents, by people who understand that
agencies sell judgment and trust — not just information.

🚀 Early bird: 50% off for first year
```

## Launch: Soft vs. Hard

### Soft Launch (Recommended First)

- No big announcement
- Let people discover organically
- Fix issues quietly
- Learn from real usage

**Do this first** for 1-2 weeks after beta.

### Hard Launch (Public)

- Big announcement
- Marketing push
- Prepared for traffic spike

**Do this when** product is stable and you're confident.

## Launch Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it's bad | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Launching too early | Bad first impression | Only launch when core works reliably |
| Over-promising | Disappointment, churn | Under-promise, over-deliver |
| No onboarding | People sign up, don't use it | Make first success obvious |
| Ignoring feedback | Miss improvement opportunities | Respond to every comment |
| Launching on Friday | Lower weekend engagement | Launch Tuesday-Thursday |
| No follow-up | Signups don't convert | Email sequence after signup |

## Launch Channels Priority

For your actual customers (travel agents):

1. **Facebook groups** — where they hang out
2. **Direct outreach** — personalized messages
3. **Host agencies** — partnerships
4. **Referrals** — from happy users

For ecosystem/press (indirect value):

5. **Product Hunt** — builders, press
6. **Indie Hackers** — indie founders
7. **LinkedIn** — professional network
8. **Twitter/X** — build in public

---

## Summary

**Don't "launch" once. Launch in stages:**

1. Friends/family ( brutal feedback)
2. Design partners (committed loop)
3. Beta (prove strangers want it)
4. Public (scale)

**Only move to next stage** when metrics look good.

**Your real customers aren't on Product Hunt** — they're in Facebook groups. Launch there second, after you know product works.

**Success** ≠ big launch day numbers. Success = 30 days later, people are still using it.
