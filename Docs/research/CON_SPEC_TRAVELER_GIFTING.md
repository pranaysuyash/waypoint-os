# Con Spec: Agentic 'Traveler-Gifting' Intelligence (CON-REAL-033)

**Status**: Research/Draft
**Area**: Traveler Relationship Deepening & Unexpected Delight Engineering

---

## 1. The Problem: "The Transactional-Gift-Miss"
Agencies occasionally send generic birthday gifts or Christmas cards. These "Transactional-Gestures" feel hollow because they're not rooted in genuine knowledge of the traveler. The real opportunity is in "Unexpected-Delight" — a surprise gift that arrives at exactly the right moment, is perfectly calibrated to the traveler's identity, and communicates that the agency has been truly paying attention.

## 2. The Solution: 'Gift-Intelligence-Protocol' (GIP)

The GIP acts as the "Delight-Curator."

### Gifting Actions:

1.  **High-Resonance-Moment-Detection**:
    *   **Action**: Identifying the optimal moments for gift delivery: post-trip (within 72 hours of return), on a meaningful anniversary, at a life-stage transition moment, or when a traveler has just experienced a disruption or hardship.
2.  **Psychographic-Gift-Matching**:
    *   **Action**: Using the traveler's DPP psychographic profile and Memory-Crystals to identify gifts with genuine resonance (e.g., a traveler who was "deeply moved by Kyoto temple bells" might receive a hand-cast Japanese temple bell from a local artisan).
3.  **Autonomous-Gift-Sourcing**:
    *   **Action**: Matching the gift concept to real, purchasable items from the agency's curated "Artisan-Vendor-Network" — local makers, destination-specific craftspeople, and experience providers.
4.  **Agency-Branded-Delivery**:
    *   **Action**: Managing the gift delivery with a handwritten-style agency note, personally authored by the AI in the agency's brand voice, ensuring the gesture feels human and warm, not automated.

## 4. Key Logic Rules

- **Rule 1: The 'No-Spam-Gift' Boundary**: The agent MUST limit gift frequency to a maximum of 3 per year per traveler to preserve the "Specialness" of the gesture. Excessive gifting becomes noise.
- **Rule 2: The 'Budget-Proportionality' Rule**: Gift budget MUST be proportional to the traveler's lifetime value tier — ensuring the agency's gifting program is financially sustainable.
- **Rule 3: The 'Human-Authenticity' Standard**: Gift notes MUST be written in the first person from the agency owner or agent's voice — not "Our AI has selected a gift for you." The gift must feel human, not algorithmic.

## 5. Success Metrics (Gifting)

- **Post-Gift-Rebooking-Rate**: % of travelers who book again within 90 days of receiving a gift.
- **Gift-Resonance-Score**: Traveler-reported rating of how meaningful/surprising the gift felt.
- **Gifting-LTV-Multiplier**: Increase in lifetime value for travelers in the active gifting program vs. those outside it.
