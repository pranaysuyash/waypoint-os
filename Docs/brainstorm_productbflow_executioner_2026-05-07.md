# Product B Flow Brainstorm - Executioner - 2026-05-07

## 10,000ft
Kill case: Product B may distract from Product A and absorb disproportionate product effort without predictable monetization.

## 1,000ft
Reasons to kill:
- Acquisition can become expensive if consumer traffic is required at scale
- Trust burden is high: one wrong high-severity finding can damage brand credibility
- Agencies may perceive it as adversarial and resist Product A adoption
- Review loops might be too slow to create measurable growth signal

## Ground level
Critical kill tests:
1. Share rate below 25% after first 100 qualified users
2. Agency revision rate below 20% after receiving findings
3. Product A demo pull-through statistically insignificant
4. Compliance overhead exceeds practical team capacity

If these fail, stop Product B and move GTM to direct agency channels.

## The thing most people miss about this
A wedge that does not create measurable pull into the core product is not a wedge. It is a side product with hidden tax.

## Addendum - Kill Criteria Calibration (2026-05-07)
Assessment: Real and necessary.

What to tighten:
1. Add sample-quality guardrails before kill decisions:
   - Use only qualified users (actual quote/trip intent) for threshold tests.
   - Require minimum sample size and confidence bounds before declaring failure.
2. Add trust-risk hard stop:
   - If high-severity false-positive findings exceed an agreed threshold, pause rollout and fix quality before further growth.
3. Distinguish "channel failure" from "product failure":
   - If consumer acquisition is expensive but agency-side behavior change is strong, switch distribution path rather than kill Product B immediately.
4. Keep kill logic tied to Product A pull-through, not vanity usage:
   - If pull-through is insignificant after calibrated tests, terminate Product B GTM role.

Execution call: Maintain strict kill discipline, but ensure thresholds are statistically and operationally valid so we do not kill a valid wedge due to noisy early data.
