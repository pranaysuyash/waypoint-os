## Part 1: Pricing Strategy

### First Principles: What Can Agencies Afford?

#### Agency Economics

| Agency Type | Monthly Revenue | Annual Revenue | Monthly Run Rate | Typical Profit Margin |
|-------------|----------------|---------------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Solo Agent** | ₹50K - 1L | ₹6L - 12L | Just themselves | 15-30% of revenue |
| **Small Agency** (3-5 people) | ₹2L - 5L | ₹24L - 60L | ₹40K - 1L | 10-25% of revenue |
| **Medium Agency** (5-15 people) | ₹4L - 12L | ₹48L - 1.4Cr | ₹1L - 3L | 10-20% of revenue |
| **Large Agency** (15+ people) | ₹10L+ | ₹1.2Cr+ | ₹2L+ | 5-15% of revenue |

#### Tool Budget Analysis

**Rule of thumb:** Agencies spend 1-5% of revenue on software/tools.

| Agency Revenue | Annual Tool Budget | Monthly Tool Budget |
|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| ₹6L/year | ₹60K/year | ₹5K/month |
| ₹24L/year | ₹2.4L/year | ₹20K/month |
| ₹60L/year | ₹6L/year | ₹50K/month |
| ₹1.2Cr/year | ₹12L/year | ₹1L/month |

**Reality check:** Solo agents spend less percentage (they're cheap), but larger agencies spend more.

### Value-Based Pricing

#### What Is the Value?

| Problem | Cost to Agency (if not solved) | Value of Solution | Break-Even Price |
|---------|------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Lost lead (slow response) | Lose 1 deal = ₹20-50K commission | Save 2 deals/month | ₹40-100K/month value |
| Scope creep (wasted time) | 5-10 hours × ₹500/hr = ₹2.5-5K | Save 3+ hours/instance | ₹1.5K/month value |
| Junior mistake (disaster) | Visa error = ₹20-100K loss | Prevent 1-2 disasters/year | ₹20-100K/year value |
| Forgotten preferences | Lose repeat customer = ₹50K+ lifetime value | Save 1 customer/year | ₹50K/year value |

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Is the product complex? | Yes - need to prove value |
| Is trust important? | Yes - agencies won't commit without trying |
| Is setup time needed? | Yes - need to onboard, add customers |
| Is competition fierce? | Yes - need to lower barrier to entry |

```
14-Day Free Trial (No credit card required)

### Alternative: Usage-Based Pricing

**Question:** Should we charge per trip/inquiry instead of flat fee?

**Pros:**
- Agencies pay for what they use
- Low commitment to try
- Scales with agency growth

**Cons:**
- Unpredictable revenue
- Harder to forecast
- Agencies might optimize away from using the tool

**Verdict:** Stick to flat monthly subscription for now. Usage-based could be a "pay-per-lead" option for very small agencies later.

| Host Agency | Agents Under Them | What They Provide |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Tirketi** | 1,000+ | Licensing, back-office, suppliers |
| **Odess** | 2,000+ | Booking engine, back-office |
| **Travstar** | 5,000+ | Technology, marketing, backend |
| **Cruise Nation** | 3,000+ | Cruise specialization, training |

#### Why Host Agencies Are Powerful

```
One conversation with Tirketi
    ↓
Tirketi offers Agency OS to their 1,000+ agents
    ↓
1,000 agents try free trial
    ↓
If 2% convert = 20 paying customers
    ↓
20 × ₹1,499 = ₹30,000/month
```

#### Host Agency Value Proposition

**For the host agency:**
- Make your agents more productive
- Standardize quality across agents
- Differentiate from other host agencies
- Reduce support burden (fewer agent mistakes)
- Revenue share opportunity

**For the agents:**
- Get access to tools at discounted rate
- Host agency endorsement = trust
- Group pricing leverage
- Training included

├─ Answer questions helpfully
├─ Share insights from your research
├─ Offer free feasibility checks
├─ Become known as "the helpful person"

Phase 3: Soft Launch
├─ "I've built a tool to solve [specific problem]"
├─ "Happy to give free access to this group"
├─ First 10 users get extended trial

Phase 4: Convert
├─ "This has been really helpful. If you want to continue..."
├─ Convert free users to paid
├─ Ask for testimonials (social proof)
```

## Conversion Metrics to Track

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|------|
| **Trial to paid conversion** | 15-25% | Industry standard for good SaaS |
| **Lead to trial signup** | 10-20% | From lead source to trial |
| **Activation rate** | 60%+ | Use tool within first 7 days |
| **Retention (Month 1)** | 80%+ | Still using after month 1 |
| **Retention (Month 6)** | 50%+ | Still using after 6 months |

### Churn Analysis

| Churn Reason | How to Address |
|-------------|-----------------|
| "Not using it enough" | Send activation tips, show value of unused features |
| "Too expensive" | Offer annual payment (discount) or downgrade plan |
| "Agency closed" | Nothing you can do, ask for referral if they closed |
| "Found alternative" | Ask what they chose, why it was better |
| "Didn't solve my problem" | Feedback for product roadmap |

## Summary: Pricing & Acquisition

### Pricing Principles

1. **Anchor to value** - Save 1 deal = 40x ROI
2. **Simple tiers** - Solo, Small, Medium (clear differentiation)
3. **Free trial** - 14 days, no credit card required
4. **Annual incentive** - 2 months free (17% discount)
5. **Revenue share option** - For host agencies

### Acquisition Principles

1. **Start where they are** - Facebook groups, LinkedIn
2. **Provide value first** - Answer questions, share insights
3. **Audit Mode wedge** - End customers → agencies discover you
4. **Host agency leverage** - One conversation = 100s of agents
5. **Content for SEO** - Blog posts answering their questions

### The Key Insight

> **"The best GTM is a product that sells itself through value and word of mouth."**

Focus on:
- Making the tool undeniably valuable
- Making it easy to try (free trial)
- Making it hard to leave (accumulated customer data, workflow integration)
- Letting happy customers bring in more customers
