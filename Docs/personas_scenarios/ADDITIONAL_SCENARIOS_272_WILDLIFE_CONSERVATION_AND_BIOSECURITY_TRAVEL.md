## Scenario: Wildlife Conservation and Biosecurity Travel

### Situation
Conservation biologists, wildlife managers, and biosecurity specialists need travel arrangements for field research, sanctuary visits, and disease prevention programs.

### What the system should do
The travel agent should coordinate protected-area access, arrange low-impact transport, manage biosecurity protocols, and support research timelines.

### Why this matters
Wildlife travel must preserve fragile ecosystems, comply with biosecurity regulations, and support sensitive conservation work without introducing risk.

### Success criteria
- Protected-area access and conservation site coordination
- Low-impact transportation and biosecurity compliance
- Research and sanctuary visit scheduling support
- Respect for ecosystem sensitivity and conservation goals
