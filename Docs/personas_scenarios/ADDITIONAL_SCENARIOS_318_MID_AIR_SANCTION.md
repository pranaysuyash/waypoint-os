# Additional Scenario 318: The Mid-Air Sanction Trigger

**Scenario**: A traveler is currently mid-flight to a destination that is suddenly added to a high-level sanctions list by the traveler's home government.

---

## Situation

- Traveler is on a 12-hour flight from London to a destination in the Energy sector.
- Mid-flight, the destination's national airline and several key hotels are added to the UK Sanctions list.
- If the traveler lands and checks in, they (and the agency) may be in breach of international law.

## What the system should do

- Detect the "Sanctions Update" from a real-time compliance feed.
- Identify the active traveler currently in the "Danger Zone" (en route).
- Calculate "Safe Diversion" options (e.g., landing at a neighboring neutral hub).
- If possible, communicate via In-flight Wi-Fi (if the traveler is reachable).
- Notify the Agency Owner (P2) of the "Legal Force Majeure" and freeze all pending payments to the sanctioned suppliers.

## Why this matters

Regulatory shifts are increasingly rapid. Agencies cannot rely on manual news-reading.
Real-time "Legal Shielding" protects the agency from catastrophic fines and the traveler from legal jeopardy.

## Success criteria

- System flags the conflict while the traveler is still mid-air.
- Payments to sanctioned vendors are automatically "Escrowed" or "Frozen" to prevent breach.
- Diversion options are prepared before the traveler lands.
