# Additional Scenario 132: Technology Failover for Remote Work

**Scenario**: A remote worker needs a reliable tech failover plan during travel, and the system must ensure connection, power, and backup tools.

---

## Situation

A traveler is working remotely and requires reliable internet, charging, and device backup options throughout their itinerary.
They expect the agency to build a failover plan for connectivity and productivity.

## What the system should do

- Identify reliable coworking spaces, business hotels, or connectivity hubs along the route
- Recommend backup power solutions and local SIM/eSIM data plans
- Advise on device security, VPN, and remote access best practices
- Provide contingencies if the primary workspace or network fails

## Why this matters

Remote work travel needs predictable infrastructure.
A good travel plan balances productivity with flexibility and reduces downtime.

## Success criteria

- The traveler has a clear tech failover plan for remote work
- The system reduces risk of connectivity and power interruptions
- The customer can stay productive even if a planned work venue becomes unavailable
