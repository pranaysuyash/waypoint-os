# Additional Scenario 322: The Cold-Chain Meds Failure

**Scenario**: A traveler's refrigerated medication (e.g., insulin, biologics) is compromised due to a hotel power outage or a broken fridge. The system must find a local pharmacy with the exact brand/dosage and coordinate a new prescription.

---

## Situation

- Traveler (S1) is in a remote boutique hotel in Costa Rica.
- A power outage lasted 12 hours; the medication is now "Warm" and potentially dangerous.
- The meds are a specialized biological brand.

## What the system should do

- Search the "Predictive Health" database for the nearest accredited pharmacy with the specific brand.
- If the brand is unavailable locally, find the "Generic Equivalent" approved for the traveler's home jurisdiction.
- Coordinate a "Tele-health" session with the traveler's doctor (or an on-call agency doctor) to issue a local prescription.
- Dispatch a courier to pick up the meds and deliver them to the hotel.
- Audit the hotel's "Cold Chain" reliability and log for future risk assessment.

## Why this matters

Medication failure can end a trip instantly or lead to hospitalization.
Providing "Logistical Pharmacy Support" is the ultimate concierge service for travelers with chronic conditions.

## Success criteria

- Replacement meds are secured within < 8 hours.
- A local doctor verifies the prescription.
- The traveler can continue their trip safely.
