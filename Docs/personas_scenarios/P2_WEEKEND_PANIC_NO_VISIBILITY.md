# P2-S5: Weekend Panic, No Visibility

**Persona**: Agency Owner
**Scenario**: The owner is out of the office over a weekend and needs visibility into urgent customer issues without constant messaging.

---

## Situation

It is a busy Saturday, and the owner is not at the desk.
The team is handling several bookings, and the owner wants a quick way to know if there are any critical issues requiring immediate attention.

### Owner’s pain
- No consolidated view of critical tickets
- No way to distinguish urgent issues from routine tasks
- Wants to avoid being interrupted for low-priority items
- Needs confidence that the team is handling emergencies

## What the system should do

1. **Provide an urgent issue feed**
   - Show only critical items: imminent departures, document failures, customer emergencies
   - Filter out routine updates and low-priority tasks

2. **Show ownership and status**
   - Indicate which agent owns each urgent item
   - Show current status and expected next action

3. **Enable rapid triage**
   - Allow the owner to mark an item as "needs my attention"
   - Show a short summary of why it is urgent

4. **Reduce noise**
   - Only surface true emergencies and escalations
   - Keep the owner from getting overwhelmed with normal booking work

## Required system behaviors

- Urgency classification and escalation detection
- Weekend/off-hours visibility dashboard
- Clear owner triage interface
- Differentiation between urgent and normal workflow items
- Confidence-building summary of team progress

## Why this matters

Owners need to manage risk without constant interruption.
This scenario proves the system can provide visibility without generating noise.

## Success criteria

- The owner sees a concise list of urgent issues.
- Each issue includes owner, status, and urgency reason.
- The owner can identify true emergencies quickly.
- The owner does not receive unnecessary alerts for routine work.
