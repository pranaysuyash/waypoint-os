# Additional Scenario 124: Multi-Airline Baggage Transfer Coordination

**Scenario**: A traveler is connecting across different airlines, and the system must coordinate baggage transfer and minimize missed connection risk.

---

## Situation

A customer’s itinerary includes flights on separate carriers with a tight connection.
They want assurance that their baggage will be handled and that they have a practical transfer plan.

## What the system should do

- Determine whether baggage can be checked through across carriers
- Advise the customer on transit procedures, layover timing, and baggage pickup requirements
- Provide backup options if the connection is too risky for checked luggage
- Notify the traveler of airport terminals, transfer services, and baggage policies

## Why this matters

Multi-airline transfers are one of the top sources of lost luggage and missed flights.
A coordinated plan reduces anxiety and prevents operational breakdowns.

## Success criteria

- The system clearly explains baggage transfer expectations and responsibilities
- The traveler can execute the connection with confidence
- The agency minimizes the risk of baggage delays or missed onward flights
