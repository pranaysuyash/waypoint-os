# Additional Scenario 320: The Tanker-Missed Rotation

**Scenario**: A flight delay for a 50-person maritime crew means they will miss their "Launch Window" to board a tanker in Singapore. The vessel cannot wait, and a hotel block for 50 people is needed instantly in a peak-season market.

---

## Situation

- 50 crew members (Energy sector) are on a delayed flight from Mumbai.
- The Tanker (Vessel V) is at port and must depart at 2 AM.
- The crew will arrive at 4 AM.
- 50 rooms are needed in Singapore during a major tech conference (Market Compression).

## What the system should do

- Monitor the Vessel's AIS (Location) and the flight's real-time ETA.
- Automatically trigger the "Missed Rotation" playbook when the "Zero-Wait" threshold is crossed.
- Access the "Autonomous Resource Futures" (Locked Inventory) or "Dark Hotels" (Unlisted Blocks) to find 50 rooms.
- Notify the shipping company of the delay, the hotel booking, and the new "Launch Window" for the following morning.
- Recalculate the "Marine Fare" flexibility to ensure no ticket-loss occurs.

## Why this matters

In Maritime travel, "Time is Money" on a massive scale.
Automating the crisis response saves the shipping client from logistical paralysis and prevents 50 sailors from being stranded at an airport.

## Success criteria

- 50 rooms are secured before the flight even lands.
- The shipping company has a "Recovery Dashboard" showing the new ETA and costs.
- Zero "No-Show" penalties are incurred due to proactive supplier notification.
