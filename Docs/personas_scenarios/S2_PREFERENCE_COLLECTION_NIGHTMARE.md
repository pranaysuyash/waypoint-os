# S2-S1: Preference Collection Nightmare

**Persona**: Family Coordinator
**Scenario**: A family coordinator is trying to collect travel preferences from multiple family members and needs the agency to organize the information clearly.

---

## Situation

A mother is coordinating a family trip for three generations.
She has received input from grandparents, parents, and children, but the preferences are scattered across chat, email, and voice notes.
The coordinator wants the agency to make sense of it and build a plan.

### Customer notes
- Grandmother: wants comfort and easy walking
- Father: wants a business-class flight if possible
- Children: want a water park and character breakfast
- Everyone: wants vegetarian meals
- Budget: around ₹6L total for 8 people

### Coordinator’s state
- Overwhelmed by conflicting requests
- Frustrated that the agency keeps asking the same questions
- Wants a single summary she can approve
- Needs the quote to reflect family consensus, not just loud voices

## What the system should do

1. **Aggregate preferences**
   - Capture each family member’s key needs
   - Organize them into a clear summary
   - Highlight conflicts and tradeoffs

2. **Resolve ambiguity**
   - Identify when requests conflict (comfort vs theme parks)
   - Ask the coordinator for priority order instead of repeating all questions

3. **Provide a family-friendly proposal**
   - Suggest a plan that balances comfort and fun
   - Show which elements satisfy which family member
   - Keep the coordinator in control of final decisions

4. **Avoid repeated clarification loops**
   - Use the assembled summary to stop asking the same thing twice
   - Reference the collected preferences in every quote

## Required system behaviors

- Multi-party preference capture
- Conflict detection and prioritization support
- Structured summary for the coordinator
- Family-aware quote generation
- Reduced question fatigue

## Why this matters

Family coordinators are high-value customers.
If the agency can make the planning easy, the coordinator becomes a strong advocate.

## Success criteria

- The system generates a single family preference summary.
- The coordinator does not need to re-state the same requests.
- The quote explains how each preference is addressed.
- The coordinator can approve or adjust priorities easily.
