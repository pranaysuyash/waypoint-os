# S1-S2: Post-Booking Anxiety

**Persona**: Individual Traveler
**Scenario**: A customer has already booked a trip and begins worrying about whether they made the right choices. The agency must provide reassurance and clear next steps.

---

## Situation

A customer has confirmed a booking and is now second-guessing the decision.
They are worried about budget, itinerary pace, or missed details.

### Customer message
> "We just booked Bali, but I’m worried we chose the wrong hotel and the kids will get bored. Can you check if this is okay?"

### Customer state
- Anxious and uncertain
- Wants reassurance from the agency
- May ask to change or upgrade the booking
- Needs clear next steps

## What the system should do

1. **Review the booked plan**
   - Summarize the itinerary and hotel choice
   - Confirm how it matches the customer’s original preferences

2. **Identify risk points**
   - Highlight any areas that may be problematic (e.g., long transfers, lack of kid activities)
   - Offer mitigation suggestions

3. **Offer reassurance**
   - Provide a supportive message that validates the customer’s concerns
   - If the plan is strong, explain why it is still a good fit

4. **Provide options for changes**
   - If needed, suggest a simple change or upgrade path
   - Explain any cost or timing implications

## Required system behaviors

- Post-booking itinerary review
- Risk and reassurance messaging
- Change management workflow
- Clear communication of what is normal vs what needs fixing
- Support for customers who need second opinions

## Why this matters

Post-booking anxiety is a powerful moment for agency trust.
The agency can win loyalty by reassuring the customer and fixing issues early.

## Success criteria

- The customer receives a clear review of their booking.
- The agency points out real risks without creating unnecessary fear.
- The customer feels supported and confident.
- The agency can offer change options if needed.
