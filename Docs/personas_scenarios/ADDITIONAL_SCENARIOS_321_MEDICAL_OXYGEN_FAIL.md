# Additional Scenario 321: The Oxygen-Depleted Transatlantic

**Scenario**: A traveler with a medical oxygen requirement finds their Portable Oxygen Concentrator (POC) has failed mid-flight. The system must coordinate emergency ground-supply for the hub-arrival and the connecting flight.

---

## Situation

- Traveler (S1) is on a flight from NYC to London.
- The POC battery is failing or the unit is malfunctioning.
- The traveler sends an "Emergency Chat" via in-flight Wi-Fi.
- They have a connection in London to Dubai.

## What the system should do

- Contact the airline's "Special Assistance" desk at the arrival hub (LHR) immediately.
- Coordinate a "Tarmac Oxygen" delivery (ambulance or airport medical services).
- Verify if the connecting carrier has a spare POC or can provide "On-board Oxygen" (requires MEDIF re-validation).
- If the unit is unfixable, source a rental POC in London for the remainder of the trip.
- Notify the traveler's emergency contact (family) and providing "Calm Assurance."

## Why this matters

Medical travel is the highest "Duty of Care" responsibility.
A system that can coordinate cross-border medical supply in real-time is a literal life-saver.

## Success criteria

- Oxygen is waiting at the gate in London.
- The connection is either secured with medical support or rebooked on a "Medical Ready" carrier.
- Zero "Oxygen Gap" for the traveler.
