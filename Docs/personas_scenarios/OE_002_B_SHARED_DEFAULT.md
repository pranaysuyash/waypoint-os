# OE-002-B: The 'Shared-Segment' Default

**Persona**: Family Coordinator (S2) + Junior Agent (P3)
**Scenario**: In a multi-party group booking (3 families sharing a villa), one family fails to pay their share of the deposit. The system must isolate the default and protect the booking for the other two families.

---

## 1. Situation

- **Travelers**: 
  - Family A (Mehtas), Family B (Smiths), Family C (Johnsons).
- **Booking**: A $15,000 villa in Bali, secured with a $3,000 non-refundable deposit.
- **Problem**: 
  - Family A and B have paid their $1,000 shares.
  - Family C has defaulted on their $1,000 payment.
  - The supplier (Villa Owner) will cancel the ENTIRE booking if the full $3,000 is not received by 5 PM today.

---

## 2. What the System Should Do

### Step 1: Default Detection & Impact Mapping
- **System Action**: Identify the `Partial_Payment_Status` in the `Shadow_Ledger`.
- **Impact**: Map the risk to the entire `CanonicalPacket` (The whole villa booking).

### Step 2: Adaptive Mitigation (Consensus Trigger)
- **AI Action**: Instead of just alerting the agent, the AI proactively calculates two recovery options:
  - **Option 1 (Downsize)**: Find a 2-bedroom alternative for Families A & B (preserving their original budget).
  - **Option 2 (Bridge)**: Propose that Families A & B "bridge" Family C's share temporarily to save the villa, with a "Re-payment Agreement" generated by the AI.

### Step 3: Automated Communication
- **To Family C**: "Your payment is overdue. We've notified the coordinator. The booking is at risk."
- **To the Coordinator (S2)**: "Family C hasn't paid. I've found a smaller villa for A & B if you want to drop them, or you can bridge their $1,000 now to keep the original stay. What should we do?"

---

## 3. Operational Logic & Rules

- **Rule 1: Isolation**: A single party's default must NEVER result in the silent cancellation of the whole group if others have paid.
- **Rule 2: 'Split-PNR' Readiness**: If the coordinator decides to drop Family C, the system must auto-trigger a "Split-PNR" request to the GDS (for flights) and notify the hotel.
- **Rule 3: Deposit Preservation**: Prioritize recovery options that preserve the non-refundable deposits already paid by Families A & B.

---

## 4. Success Criteria

- **Safety**: Families A & B still have a confirmed trip.
- **Financial**: The agency does not take a loss on the unpaid share.
- **Commercial**: The "Bridge Payment" logic is handled legally with a clear audit trail.

---
