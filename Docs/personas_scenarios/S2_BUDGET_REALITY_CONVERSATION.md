# S2-S3: Budget Reality Conversation

**Persona**: Family Coordinator
**Scenario**: A family coordinator has a budget expectation that is lower than the realistic cost for the requested trip, and the agency must manage expectations honestly while keeping the customer engaged.

---

## Situation

A family coordinator requests a luxury family trip but has a budget that does not match the destination or timing.
They need the agency to explain the gap clearly and offer workable alternatives.

### Customer request
- Destination: Switzerland
- Duration: 8 days in July
- Party: 2 adults, 2 teens, 1 child
- Preferences: mountains, easy walking, family-friendly
- Budget: ₹4L total

### Coordinator’s state
- Wants a premium experience
- Assumes the budget is sufficient
- Expects the agency to make it fit
- Doesn’t want to be told “no” without alternatives

## What the system should do

1. **Assess budget realism**
   - Compare the requested destination and season to budget benchmarks
   - Show a clear gap between requested experience and budget

2. **Explain the tradeoff**
   - "Switzerland in July for a family of 5 typically starts at ₹6.5-7L"
   - "For ₹4L, a better fit is a simpler Alpine stay or a different destination"

3. **Offer alternatives**
   - Option A: Move to a lower-cost destination with similar scenery
   - Option B: Shorten stay or shift season to shoulder months
   - Option C: Keep Switzerland but adjust expectations (fewer nights, budget hotel)

4. **Keep the conversation positive**
   - Frame the budget gap as a choice, not a failure
   - Help the coordinator feel empowered to decide

## Required system behaviors

- Budget sanity checking
- Transparent explanation of costs
- Alternative recommendations
- Customer-facing language for expectation management
- Preservation of trust during a difficult budget conversation

## Why this matters

This scenario turns a potential rejection into a productive dialogue.
The agency can retain the coordinator’s business by helping them revise the plan rather than simply saying it is impossible.

## Success criteria

- The system identifies the budget mismatch quickly.
- The coordinator sees a clear explanation and alternatives.
- The agent can keep the customer engaged constructively.
- The coordinator does not feel the agency is dismissive.
