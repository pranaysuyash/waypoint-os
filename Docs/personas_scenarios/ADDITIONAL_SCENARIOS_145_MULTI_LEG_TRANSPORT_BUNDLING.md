# Additional Scenario 145: Multi-Leg Transport Bundling

**Scenario**: A traveler needs to bundle multiple transport legs, and the system must coordinate a seamless chain of flights, trains, and transfers.

---

## Situation

A customer’s itinerary includes several transport segments across airlines, trains, and ground services.
They expect the agency to bundle the legs so connections work smoothly and baggage is managed.

## What the system should do

- Plan transfer durations and verify the feasibility of each connection
- Coordinate baggage handling and check-through options when possible
- Provide detailed transfer instructions for each leg, including terminal changes
- Offer backup plans if a segment is delayed or missed

## Why this matters

Multi-leg travel multiplies operational risk.
A coordinated transport bundle keeps the itinerary workable and reduces missed connections.

## Success criteria

- The traveler receives a coherent multi-leg transport plan
- The agency minimizes connection risk and clarifies transfer expectations
- The customer can move through each leg with confidence
