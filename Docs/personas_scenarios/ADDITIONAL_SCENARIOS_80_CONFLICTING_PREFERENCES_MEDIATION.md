# Additional Scenario 80: Conflicting Preferences Mediation

**Scenario**: Travel party members have conflicting preferences, and the system must mediate options that balance competing needs.

---

## Situation

A family or group has different desires – one wants adventure while another wants relaxation.
The agent must propose a trip that satisfies the group without making anyone feel ignored.

## What the system should do

- Capture the different preferences clearly and equally
- Recommend itinerary elements that offer choice and compromise
- Suggest flexible days with optional activities
- Frame the plan as a balanced solution, not a compromise for one side

## Why this matters

Conflicting preferences are common in group travel.
Good mediation prevents dissatisfaction and keeps the group together.

## Success criteria

- The system presents a balanced itinerary with options
- Each group member’s priorities are acknowledged
- The plan feels intentional and inclusive, not just “everyone gets a bit of everything”
