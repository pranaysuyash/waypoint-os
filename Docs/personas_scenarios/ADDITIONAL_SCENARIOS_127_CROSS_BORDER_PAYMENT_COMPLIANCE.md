# Additional Scenario 127: Cross-Border Payment Compliance

**Scenario**: A booking involves cross-border payment rules, and the system must ensure the payment path complies with local regulations and tax requirements.

---

## Situation

A customer is paying from one country for services in another, triggering cross-border remittance or invoicing rules.
They expect the agency to handle compliance without adding unnecessary complexity.

## What the system should do

- Identify any regulatory requirements for cross-border payment processing
- Recommend compliant payment methods and invoice handling
- Disclose fees, currency conversions, and tax implications clearly
- Prevent bookings from failing due to missing compliance steps

## Why this matters

International payments can be blocked by local controls or taxation rules.
Compliance protects the agency and the traveler from delays or legal exposure.

## Success criteria

- The payment plan is compliant and well-explained
- The traveler understands currency, fee, and documentation requirements
- The booking moves forward without avoidable cross-border payment failure
