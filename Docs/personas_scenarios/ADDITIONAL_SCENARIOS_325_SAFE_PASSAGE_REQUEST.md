# Additional Scenario 325: Autonomous Diplomacy Safe-Passage Request

**Scenario**: A traveler is caught in a sudden diplomatic rift between two nations (e.g., immediate visa revocation for all citizens of Country A). The system must negotiate "Safe Passage" via a neutral third-country consulate.

---

## Situation

- Traveler (Country A citizen) is in Country B.
- Country B suddenly revokes all visas for Country A citizens due to a diplomatic spat.
- Border police are beginning to check hotels.

## What the system should do

- The "Autonomous Diplomacy" agent detects the news flash and revokes current "Public" location data.
- Search for "Neutral Third-Party Consulates" in the city (e.g., Swiss or Icelandic consulate).
- Generate a "Safe Passage Request" using pre-verified legal templates and the traveler's secure identity documents.
- Coordinate a "Dark Transfer" (unlisted vehicle) to move the traveler to a neutral safe-house or airport hub.
- Notify the home country's Foreign Ministry and providing the traveler's exact (encrypted) GPS location.

## Why this matters

"Safe Passage" is the ultimate insurance. 
When the rules of the world change instantly, the system must act as the traveler's "Digital Diplomat."

## Success criteria

- The traveler is moved to a "Neutral Zone" (Consulate or Safe-house) before the police sweep.
- A "Temporary Exit Visa" is negotiated via the third-party consulate.
- The traveler is repatriated without being detained.
