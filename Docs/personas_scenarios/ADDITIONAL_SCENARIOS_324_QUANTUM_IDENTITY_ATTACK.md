# Additional Scenario 324: Post-Quantum Identity Theft Attempt

**Scenario**: A malicious actor attempts to intercept a traveler's "Diplomatic Pouch" credentials during a high-stakes trip. The system detects the anomaly and triggers a "Quantum Re-keying" event.

---

## Situation

- Traveler (VVIP) is in a high-surveillance jurisdiction.
- An unusual "Man-in-the-Middle" (MITM) attack is detected on the traveler's secure app connection.
- The attacker is using advanced decryption tools attempting to breach the "Pouch."

## What the system should do

- The "Post-Quantum Privacy" module detects the signature of a non-standard decryption attempt.
- Immediately trigger "Automatic Key Rotation" using PQC (Post-Quantum Cryptography).
- Lock the current "Pouch" and generate a "Ghost Pouch" (Decoy) with fake itineraries to confuse the attacker.
- Alert the traveler to switch to a "Secondary Hardware Key" (e.g., YubiKey).
- Notify the Agency's "Security Orchestrator" and the traveler's private security team.

## Why this matters

In the "Frontier," data is as valuable as the trip itself.
Protecting VVIPs from state-level or high-tech data interception is a core "Deep Infrastructure" requirement.

## Success criteria

- Zero PII is leaked during the attack.
- The attacker spends resources on the "Ghost Pouch."
- The traveler is moved to a secure communication channel within minutes.
