# Additional Scenario 37: Weather Disruption Contingency

**Scenario**: A trip may be affected by extreme weather, and the system must present contingency plans before the customer commits.

---

## Situation

A customer is booking an outdoor adventure during a season known for storms or flooding.
They need the agency to show that risk has been considered and that backup plans exist.

## What the system should do

- Identify weather-sensitive elements of the itinerary
- Explain the risk in plain language
- Offer contingency options: alternate dates, indoor alternatives, flexible bookings
- Confirm what support will be available if the weather affects the trip

## Why this matters

Weather risk is a hidden, high-impact source of dissatisfaction.
Proactive contingency planning builds credibility and reduces future conflict.

## Success criteria

- The system flags weather-sensitive timing automatically
- The customer sees concrete fallback plans, not vague assurances
- The itinerary feels resilient and trustworthy
