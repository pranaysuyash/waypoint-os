# Additional Scenario 81: Intermodal Travel Failure Recovery

**Scenario**: A traveler’s train or ferry connection fails mid-itinerary, and the system must recover the route with alternative transport options.

---

## Situation

A customer is using a combination of flights, trains, and ferries when one leg is cancelled or significantly delayed.
They need the agency to fix the route quickly and keep the trip on track.

## What the system should do

- Detect intermodal disruption automatically
- Identify alternate connections that preserve the itinerary as closely as possible
- Communicate the updated travel plan and any extra costs
- Offer compensation or contingency plans if the itinerary changes significantly

## Why this matters

Intermodal travel is fragile and often outside airline control.
Quick recovery protects customers from cascading disruptions.

## Success criteria

- The system reroutes the affected leg rapidly
- The customer receives a clear, actionable updated itinerary
- The agency minimizes the impact on the remainder of the trip
