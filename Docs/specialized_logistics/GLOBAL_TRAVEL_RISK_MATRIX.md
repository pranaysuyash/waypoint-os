# Domain Knowledge: Global Travel Risk Matrix

**Category**: Global Risk & Crisis  
**Focus**: Categorizing threats and implementing mitigation protocols.

---

## 1. Geopolitical & Security Risks

### Threat Levels (Level 1 to 4)
- **Level 1 (Exercise Normal Precautions)**: e.g., Japan, Canada.
- **Level 4 (Do Not Travel)**: e.g., North Korea, Active war zones.
- **SOP**: If a destination moves to **Level 3/4** after booking, the agent must trigger the "Involuntary Cancellation" refund protocol.

---

## 2. Financial & Credit Risks

- **Vendor Default**: The risk that a hotel or airline goes bankrupt before the trip.
- **SOP**: Prioritize vendors with **"IATA Protection"** or use the agency's "Supplier Default Insurance."

---

## 3. Health & Environmental Risks

- **Logic**: Pandemics, Hurricane Seasons, or Volcanic Ash.
- **SOP**: "Seasonality Audit." Never book a "Non-refundable" Caribbean trip during peak hurricane season without explicit client waiver.

---

## 4. Legal & Civil Risks

- **Logic**: Protests, strikes (Lufthansa/SNCF), or sudden visa rule changes.
- **SOP**: Monitor the **"Strikes Calendar"** and provide the client with a "Proactive Re-booking" option 48 hours before a planned strike.

---

## 5. Proposed Scenarios
- **The "Sudden Coup"**: A military coup occurs in a destination country while 10 clients are there. The agent must activate **"Duty of Care" tracking** and coordinate with a private security firm for extraction.
- **Airline Default**: A national carrier goes bankrupt. The agent must re-book all passengers on "Rescue Fares" and file for the refund via the BSP administrator.
- **The "Hurricane Trap"**: A client is booked into a luxury resort in Florida. A Category 4 hurricane is projected to hit in 48 hours. The hotel refuses a refund because "the airport is still open." The agent must argue for "Life Safety" cancellation.
- **Strike Chaos**: French air traffic controllers go on strike. The agent must move all clients to "Rail" or "Private Car" alternatives across the border.
