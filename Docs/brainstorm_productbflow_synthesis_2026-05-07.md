# Product B Flow Brainstorm Synthesis - 2026-05-07

## 1) North Star
Product B exists to create traveler-side negotiation leverage that converts into agency-side urgency, which then pulls agencies toward Product A adoption.

## 2) Convergent threads
- Product B must stay a focused audit flow, not a consumer booking product.
- Freeform text intake is mandatory for scale; uploads are additive.
- Output quality is defined by conversation usability: findings must be shareable and actionable in agency dialogue.
- Flow success depends on early time-to-value and visible evidence for each finding.
- Re-audit loop (before/after revisions) is strategically important.

## 3) Strongest contradictions
- Champion/Strategist see high leverage if behavior loop works; Executioner warns it can become a non-monetizing side tax.
- Operator wants richer workflow control; Skeptic warns against feature sprawl and trust collapse from generic output.

## 4) Decisions required now
1. Scope lock: Product B limited to audit + action packet + re-audit compare.
2. Output contract: each finding must include severity, rationale, confidence, and exact question to ask the current agent.
3. Measurement contract: instrument share rate, revision rate, and Product A pull-through.
4. Risk posture: legal-safe language templates and confidence gating for high-severity claims.

## 5) What this brainstorm corrected
- Rejected automated handoff architecture assumptions between Product B and agency inboxes.
- Reinforced day-1 global framing and non-India-first logic.
- Reinforced freeform text as primary path, not quote-upload dependency.

## 6) Horizon view
- 6 months: nail core audit/re-audit loop and behavior instrumentation.
- 12 months: improve evidence calibration and archetype-specific packs.
- 24 months: build pattern intelligence that strengthens Product A workflows.

## 7) Build first vs dream later
Build first:
- Intake (text first, upload optional)
- Prioritized findings board
- Agent-ready share packet
- Re-audit comparison
- Event instrumentation

Dream later:
- Benchmarks and anonymized network intelligence
- Deep vertical specialization layers
- Advanced negotiation copilots per locale/channel

## 8) Kill-test verdict
Product B survives the kill test only if it creates measurable downstream pull into Product A.
If share/revision/pull-through metrics fail early thresholds, terminate or redesign the wedge.

## 9) Recommended execution sequence
Phase 0: Instrumentation foundation
- Define and capture: time-to-first-insight, share event, revision event, Product A demo pull-through.

Phase 1: Core flow hardening
- Ship strict audit flow: intake -> findings -> share packet.
- Keep findings capped and evidence-linked.

Phase 2: Re-audit loop
- Add before/after comparison to validate real conversation impact.

Phase 3: Product A bridge
- Convert agency exposure into structured Product A onboarding narrative.

## 10) Working hypothesis for Product B flow
"If travelers receive specific, evidence-backed negotiation prompts within 90 seconds, they will use them with their existing agent, increasing plan revision frequency and creating measurable demand pull for Product A."

## 11) Addendum - Decision Boundary (2026-05-07)
Verdict after role-by-role review:
1. Product B is valid only as a behavior-change wedge into Product A.
2. Primary success event is agency revision achieved, not content sharing alone.
3. Launch gate is credibility + forwardability + pull-through, not usage volume.
4. Scope must remain audit + action packet + re-audit compare.
5. Kill logic must use calibrated sample-quality and confidence thresholds.

Execution implication:
- Prioritize instrumentation, evidence grounding, and language quality before any expansion features.
