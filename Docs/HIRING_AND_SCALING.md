# Hiring and Scaling

**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Purpose**: When and how to grow beyond solo

---

## The Solo Builder Reality

> "You can't stay solo forever if you're successful. But hire too early and you burn cash."

**Principle**: Hire as late as possible, but not so late that you're the bottleneck.

---

## When to Hire (Triggers)

### Clear Signals It's Time

| Trigger | What it means | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| **Revenue > ₹50K/month** | Can afford hire | Consider part-time help |
| **You're the bottleneck** | Features/bugs waiting on you | Hire engineer |
| **Support > 20 hours/week** | Can't focus on building | Hire support |
| **Sales leads > you can handle** | Product works, need growth | Hire sales |
| **You're working > 60 hours/week** | Unsustainable | Hire or cut scope |

### Wrong Reasons to Hire

| Wrong reason | Why |
|--------------|------|
| "Startups are supposed to hire" | Premature scaling kills startups |
| "Investors expect it" | You're not raising (yet) |
| "I'm lonely" | Join a co-working space, don't hire yet |
| "Other startups have 10 people" | They might be burning cash |

## Role-by-Role Breakdown

### 1. Junior Engineer

**When**: You have more feature work than time, but not enough revenue for senior hire

**Responsibilities**:
- Bug fixes
- Simple features
- Testing
- Documentation

**Cost**: ₹25,000-50,000/month (India)

**Pros**: Cheaper, you can mentor, they grow with company
**Cons**: Need your time to manage, slower initially

**Net ROI positive when**: They free up 20+ hours/week of your time

---

### 2. Senior Engineer

**When**: You have revenue ₹1L+/month and complex technical needs

**Responsibilities**:
- Own major features
- Architecture decisions
- Mentor juniors

**Cost**: ₹80,000-1,50,000/month

**Pros**: Self-sufficient, high output
**Cons**: Expensive, might leave for better offers

**Hire when**: Their cost < revenue they enable

---

### 3. Customer Support

**When**: Support eating > 20 hours/week of your time

**Responsibilities**:
- Respond to tickets
- Onboarding calls
- Documentation updates
- User feedback collection

**Cost**: ₹20,000-40,000/month (full-time) or ₹15,000/month (part-time)

**Hire**: Contractor first, convert to full-time if needed

---

### 4. Sales / Account Executive

**When**: Product works, you need more customers, you're bad at sales

**Responsibilities**:
- Outreach to agencies
- Demo calls
- Closing deals
- Account management

**Cost**: ₹30,000 base + commission OR commission-only

**Structure**: Commission-only early (10-20% of first-year revenue)

---

### 5. Contractor vs. Full-Time

| Contractor | Full-time |
|------------|-----------|
| One-off projects | Ongoing work |
| No long-term commitment | Benefits, stability |
| More expensive per hour | Cheaper per hour |
| Easy to end if not working | Harder to let go |

**Rule**: Start with contractors for everything except core engineering.

## Hiring Process

### 1. Write a Clear Job Description

```markdown
# Title: [Role] at Agency OS

## About us
[2 sentences: what you do, who it's for]

## About the role
[What they'll do day-to-day]

## Requirements
[Must-haves, not wish-list]

## Compensation
[Be transparent: ₹X-₹Y/month]

## How to apply
[Simple: email with resume + why interested]
```

### 2. Sourcing Channels

| Channel | Best for |
|---------|----------|
| **Your network** | Highest quality, trusted |
| **LinkedIn** | Senior roles |
| **AngelList** | Startup-focused |
| **Twitter/X** | Developer community |
| **Y Combinator jobs board** | Startup talent |
| **Local meetups** | Junior talent |

### 3. Interview Process (Keep It Short)

| Stage | Purpose | Time |
|-------|---------|------|
| **Screening call** | Basic fit, communication | 15 min |
| **Skill check** | Can they actually do the work? | 30-60 min |
| **Values fit** | Will they work well with you? | 30 min |
| **Trial** (optional) | Real work before committing | Paid, 1 week |

**Don't**: 5+ rounds, take-home projects, whiteboard coding.

## Managing Your First Hire

### Week 1

- Clear expectations: what success looks like
- Access to tools (Git, Slack, etc.)
- Pair program on first task
- Daily check-ins

### Month 1

- Weekly 1:1s
- Clear feedback on what's working/not
- Gradually increase autonomy
- Still fairly hands-on

### Month 3+

- Monthly 1:1s
- They own their area
- You provide direction, not management
- Regular feedback still important

| Your behavior | What it teaches |
|---------------|-----------------|
| You respond quickly | Respond quickly |
| You cut corners | Cut corners |
| You obsess over quality | Obsess over quality |
| You communicate clearly | Communicate clearly |

**Be the behavior you want to see.**

## When Hiring Goes Wrong

### Signs It's Not Working

- They're not delivering after 3 months
- They're disruptive to team culture
- They're unhappy
- You dread working with them

### What to Do

1. **Document**: Specific examples of issues
2. **Feedback**: Clear conversation about expectations
3. **PIP** (Performance Improvement Plan): 30 days to fix
4. **Let go**: If no improvement, act fast

**Don't let bad hires linger.** It's worse for everyone.

## Scaling Beyond First Hire

### Second Hire

**When**: First hire is at capacity and you still have more work

**Who**: Usually complementary role
- If first was engineer → hire support or sales
- If first was support → hire engineer

### Third Hire

**When**: You have ₹2L+ MRR

**Who**: Usually another engineer or senior role

### Team Structure at 5-10 People

```
You (CEO/Builder)
├── Engineering lead
│   ├── Engineer 1
│   └── Engineer 2
├── Customer success lead
│   └── Support 1
└── Sales/Growth (maybe you or dedicated)
```

## Alternatives to Hiring

### Before You Hire, Consider:

| Option | When to use |
|--------|-------------|
| **No-code tools** | Automate repetitive tasks |
| **Contractors** | One-off projects |
| **Agencies** | Design, marketing bursts |
| **Integrations** | Instead of building yourself |
| **Just say no** | Some work doesn't need doing |

---

## Summary

**Hire when**: Revenue supports it OR you're clearly the bottleneck

**First hire**: Usually junior engineer or part-time support

**Process**: Keep it short — screening call + skill check + values fit

**Compensation**: Be transparent, pay fairly but within means

**Manage closely**: First 3 months need hands-on management

**Fire fast**: If it's not working, part ways quickly

**Remember**: Every hire is a bet. Only bet when you can afford to lose.
